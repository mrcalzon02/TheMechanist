# Milestone Supplement - Noble Luxury, Narcotics, and Draught Trade

This supplement attaches to the economy, faction market, provenance, world-generation, and medical/narcotics milestone groups. It preserves the design rule that noble factions, especially the Ashbourne houses, participate in luxury-good and narcotics trade while reserving certain ultra-rare off-world substances as locked-vault prestige goods rather than ordinary vendor stock.

## Scope

Noble houses and high-status factions should have preferences, stores, trade routes, estate rooms, vaults, servants, brokers, and social rituals around luxury goods, rare narcotics, high-status medicines, imported pleasures, and draught items.

This is not the same as gang narcotics production. Gang and illicit factions may prefer bulk manufacture and street distribution. Noble houses may prefer acquisition, curation, private use, patronage, controlled distribution, blackmail, medicalized indulgence, elite hospitality, and vault-preserved scarcity.

## Core rule

The Ashbourne houses and other noble factions should maintain luxury and narcotics economies appropriate to their rank, wealth, and estate footprint.

They may:

- Buy luxury goods in bulk through brokers, trade houses, smugglers, or formal import routes.
- Consume luxury goods internally as household status displays.
- Gift luxury items to allies, retainers, lovers, specialists, or political dependents.
- Use rare substances as negotiation tools, bribes, favors, blackmail leverage, or elite hospitality.
- Stock private stores, pantries, cellars, medicine cabinets, pleasure rooms, locked cabinets, and vaults.
- Maintain relationships with legal merchants, black-market brokers, smugglers, doctors, chemists, and illicit suppliers.
- Trade common luxury narcotics while hoarding the rarest substances.
- Keep draught items locked away and unavailable for normal sale.

## Draught items

Draught items are rare off-world substances that noble houses can sometimes possess, but should almost never sell. They should be explicitly extremely valuable, socially significant, and mechanically distinct from ordinary narcotics or medicines.

Draught items should be treated as:

- Extremely valuable.
- Rare off-world imports.
- Prestige goods.
- Noble-vault items.
- Possible quest objectives.
- Possible blackmail, diplomacy, ransom, bribery, or inheritance objects.
- Possible medical, narcotic, ritual, longevity, stimulant, sedative, or exotic-status goods depending on future item definitions.
- Potentially illegal, restricted, hereditary, licensed, contraband, or socially protected depending on house and jurisdiction.

Draught items should normally be stored in locked vaults, sealed cabinets, noble estate strongrooms, private reliquaries, physician-controlled stores, or guarded luxury stores inside noble estate stamps.

They should not appear as ordinary shop goods. A noble house may possess a supply but refuse to sell it. The player may encounter draught items through quests, theft, inheritance disputes, blackmail, rescue payments, diplomacy, smuggling, rare gifts, house collapse, confiscation, vault raids, or special favor—not casual purchase.

- Phase 2 - Asset promotion for luxury goods, noble vault goods, sealed containers, draught-item icons, estate storage, and high-status narcotic/medicine assets.
- Phase 9 - Item provenance for luxury goods, narcotics, medicines, draught items, off-world origin, house ownership, trade route origin, and vault custody.
- Phase 11 - Leadership schemes involving noble import, hoarding, smuggling, bribes, vault protection, theft recovery, and prestige politics.
- Phase 16 - Noble estate room stamps, vaults, luxury stores, pleasure rooms, private clinics, and secured storage.

## Phase 2 addition - Noble luxury and draught asset promotion pass

Promote assets for noble luxury and draught systems before they are used in estate stamps or item systems.

Candidate asset categories include:

- Luxury bottles, vials, sealed ampoules, reliquary boxes, luxury packets, estate ledgers, sealed tins, imported crates, ornate cabinets, vault containers, locked display boxes, rare off-world containers, noble medicine cases, and draught-item icons.
- Estate room assets for vaults, cellars, pleasure rooms, private clinics, luxury storerooms, import receiving rooms, guarded cabinets, and broker offices.

Exit criteria:

Luxury, narcotic, and draught assets have semantic IDs, player-facing labels, publish-safe status, value category, legal/restricted status, and valid storage/room contexts.

## Phase 9 addition - Noble luxury and draught provenance pass

Item provenance should distinguish noble luxury goods from common contraband or ordinary medicine.

Required provenance fields may include:

- Noble house owner.
- Ashbourne house variant or sub-house owner where applicable.
- Off-world origin.
- Local broker, smuggler, merchant, or physician source.
- Import route.
- Vault custody history.
- Legality or restriction status.
- Whether the item is for household use, gifting, bargaining, blackmail, medical privilege, private indulgence, or pure hoarding.
- Whether it is genuine, diluted, counterfeit, contaminated, stolen, misdeclared, or house-certified.

Exit criteria:

Luxury goods and draught items can be traced to house ownership, source, custody, and value rather than appearing as generic valuables.

## Phase 11 addition - Noble luxury and draught leadership scheme pass

Noble leadership schemes should be able to interact with luxury and draught goods.

Possible schemes include:

- Acquire rare off-world draught.
- Protect the house vault.
- Recover stolen draught.
- Smuggle luxury narcotics through rival territory.
- Bribe officials or rivals with controlled luxury goods.
- Host an elite gathering requiring rare substances.
- Hoard medicine or draught during crisis.
- Blackmail a rival over narcotic dependence.
- Poison, adulterate, or counterfeit a rival house supply.
- Use luxury gifts to secure a marriage, compact, contract, or alliance.
- Conceal illegal imports from inspection.
- Destroy evidence of house indulgence.

Exit criteria:

Noble luxury goods and draught items can drive faction schemes, quests, politics, and estate security behavior.

## Phase 16 addition - Noble estate luxury and draught vault stamp pass

Noble estate generation should include room stamps and storage logic for luxury goods and draught items.

Possible rooms include:

- Locked noble vault.
- Sealed off-world substance vault.
- Luxury cellar.
- Private medicine cabinet.
- House physician room.
- Pleasure salon.
- Imported-goods store.
- Broker receiving room.
- Guarded estate storehouse.
- Reliquary display or sealed cabinet.
- Hidden noble stash.

Draught items should appear only in controlled contexts and should normally be locked behind access, ownership, stealth, diplomacy, quest, inheritance, faction permission, or violence.

Exit criteria:

Noble estate stamps can contain luxury and draught storage as meaningful protected assets, not generic treasure rooms.

## Phase 17 addition - Noble luxury and restricted narcotics trade pass

Noble factions should have market behavior around luxury goods and narcotics, but draught items should normally be hoarded rather than sold.

Required behavior:

- Noble brokers or representatives may buy and sell luxury goods.
- Selected narcotics or high-status medicines may be traded through official, private, or black-market channels.
- The rarest draught items should be visible as known desires, rumors, vault contents, quest targets, or political goods rather than normal stock.
- Nobles may pay extremely well for draught items, but may refuse to sell their own.
- Acquiring, stealing, exposing, adulterating, or returning draught items should affect faction relations, heat, reputation, and schemes.

Exit criteria:

Noble trade supports luxury goods and narcotics while preserving draught items as exceptional locked-vault valuables.

## Phase 18 addition - Noble luxury, narcotics, and draught editor/audit pass

Editor/audit surfaces should inspect:

- Noble luxury item definitions.
- Draught item definitions.
- Extremely valuable item flags.
- Off-world origin flags.
- Vault-only storage flags.
- Sale restrictions.
- House ownership.
- Noble estate room placement.
- Broker/vendor stock rules.
- Quest objective hooks.
- Theft, inheritance, diplomacy, blackmail, and vault-raid hooks.
- Counterfeit, adulterated, stolen, or genuine quality/provenance states.

Exit criteria:

Noble luxury and draught systems can be audited through data definitions rather than hidden ad hoc loot placement.

## Phase 19 addition - Noble luxury and draught release audit

Before release claims, verify:

- Ashbourne houses and noble factions have luxury/narcotics trade preferences where appropriate.
- Draught items are explicitly extremely valuable.
- Draught items are rare off-world substances rather than ordinary drugs or medicines.
- Draught items are normally not for sale.
- Draught items can appear in locked vaults or secured noble estate contexts.
- Luxury goods, narcotics, and draught items have provenance and ownership where relevant.
- Noble vendors, brokers, or representatives do not expose vault-only items as ordinary shop stock.
- Player-facing text distinguishes common luxury goods, narcotics, medicines, restricted substances, and draught items.
- Related quests, theft paths, diplomacy paths, and faction consequences hide raw IDs.

Exit criteria:

The game may claim noble luxury/draught economies only when noble houses can acquire, hoard, protect, trade around, desire, and politically leverage these goods in traceable ways.

## Deferred checkpoint line - Noble luxury, narcotics, and draught goods

The Ashbourne houses and noble factions should participate in luxury-goods and narcotics trade while treating draught items as extremely valuable rare off-world substances. Noble houses may sometimes possess draught supplies but should not offer them for normal sale. Draught items should be held in locked vaults, sealed cabinets, estate strongrooms, or other secured noble estate stamps and used as quest targets, political leverage, prestige objects, blackmail material, rare medical/luxury goods, or diplomatic prizes.
