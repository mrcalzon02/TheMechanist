# 003G AETERIC GUARD
## Concordial Military Archive Record Concerning the Guard of Immortality Infrastructure, High Legate Protection, Draught Vault Security, Continuance Sites, Distillation Convoys, Legio Sanctums, and the Armed Silence Around the Machinery by Which the Concord Pretends Death Has Been Administratively Defeated

Filed beneath the parent seal of **003 Eternus Legio and Military**, cross-sealed by **008 The Draught and Immortality**, **011 Collegia Mortis**, **012 Custodia Veritatis**, **016 Ferrum Sanctum**, and all restricted offices whose survival depends upon things no citizen is permitted to see.

This record concerns the **Aeteric Guard**.

They guard the doors behind which continuity becomes material.

They are not ceremonial.

Ceremony is what lesser persons are allowed to see from a distance.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Aeteric Guard are elite Legio formations assigned to protect immortality infrastructure, high Legates, Draught repositories, distillation apparatus, rejuvenation vaults, continuity chambers, sealed medical sanctums, Mortis-Aeteric laboratories, noble renewal convoys, First-Draught reliquaries, and those living persons whose death would cause more paperwork than war.

They are guardians of the means by which the Concord extends life, restores authority, repeats command, preserves old knowledge, and maintains the useful fiction that death can be negotiated with if sufficiently armed offices attend the meeting.

They are distinct from noble house guards, Custodia Veritatis agents, Praetoria enforcement, Cohors Noctis correction forces, and ordinary Legio honor detachments. A noble guard protects a person. A Praetorian protects order. A Custodian protects truth or its absence. A Noctis executioner protects the Concord from its own immortal teeth.

The Aeteric Guard protect continuity as infrastructure.

A High Legate is infrastructure.

A Draught vault is infrastructure.

A resurrection cradle is infrastructure.

A sealed artery carrying fractional distillate through a hospital system is infrastructure.

A candle rumored to contain one particle of distillate is not infrastructure, unless a riot has formed around it, at which point the distinction becomes tactical.

---

## ORIGIN

The Aeteric Guard arose after the first successful attacks against distillation sites during the late Continuity Wars. Earlier Legio authorities had assumed that secrecy, sanctity, and remoteness would protect the apparatus of renewal. This proved optimistic in the way that dead officials often prove educational.

Enemies sought the Draught.

Nobles sought the Draught.

Desperate citizens sought the Draught.

Cultists sought to corrupt the Draught.

Machine synods sought to measure the Draught.

The Collegia Mortis sought to classify it, the Cultus Vitae sought to inhale it, the Didam sought to devour it, and the Legio sought to ensure that enough remained to keep the correct people alive long enough to continue arguing.

The first Aeteric detachments were drawn from veterans who had defended Immogic field sanctums and Legio revival chambers. They were given better armor, worse secrets, and permission to kill people whose titles would normally require correspondence before execution.

The formal Aeteric Guard was sealed after the Amber Cistern Assault, when three noble houses, one machine synod, a hunger cult, and an unauthorized medical charity attempted to seize the same Draught-processing reservoir within a single night. The Guard killed them all, then spent six months determining which dead had technically been on the same side.

## THE AETERIC OATH

The Aeteric Oath is never administered in public.

It is spoken before a sealed vessel, a dead witness, and an officer old enough to know why both are necessary:

*I guard the means and not the myth. I guard the vessel and not the vanity. I guard the continuity of command against hunger, theft, pity, ambition, and despair. I shall not drink. I shall not bargain. I shall not open the door because a beloved voice asks from the other side. I shall not confuse the life of the guarded with the worth of the guarded. I stand until relieved by lawful seal or until no living thing remains to misuse what I protect.*

The clause **I shall not drink** is repeated three times.

No one laughs.

## PROTECTED SITES

The Aeteric Guard protect several classes of site.

**Draught vaults** contain stored distillate, stabilized fractions, precursor reagents, contaminated residues, false ampules, testing lots, and substances whose legal classification changes depending on whether they are in a bottle, a bloodstream, a corpse, or a prayer.

**Continuance chambers** are used for high Legate restoration, noble rejuvenation under license, immortal officer stabilization, memory preservation, and medical interventions that ordinary citizens are encouraged to believe do not exist.

**Distillation apparatus sites** range from ancient sealed engines to modern fractional processing laboratories. Even a broken apparatus may attract wars.

**Aeteric hospitals** administer minute indices of distillate-amplified restoratives. The Guard are present not for the patients but for the storage cabinets.

**Reliquary convoys** transport restricted substances, ancient samples, founder-associated materials, sealed candles, machine components, and occasionally persons whose bodies contain more state interest than their opinions justify.

## RELATION TO HIGH LEGATES

High Legates protected by the Aeteric Guard often mistake protection for loyalty.

The Guard permit this if it improves obedience to security protocol.

Their loyalty is not to the mood, dignity, desire, romance, nostalgia, hunger, religious anxiety, or private terror of the protected person. Their loyalty is to continuity of command as determined by lawful seal. A High Legate who attempts unauthorized access to Draught stores may be restrained. A High Legate under conceptual compromise may be sedated. A High Legate who orders the Guard to abandon a vault for personal rescue may be disobeyed if the vault is judged more important than the Legate.

The protected do not enjoy learning this.

The Aeteric Guard do not enjoy explaining it.

This is why explanations are minimized.

## RECRUITMENT

Aeteric candidates are drawn from decorated Linea veterans, Ferrata crew survivors, Custodia Cineris personnel with stable interpretive resistance, Praetoria officers with proven incorruptibility, Limes Wardens who endured long silence without cultic drift, Mortis Auxilia veterans who handled Draught-contaminated dead without theft, and rare noble scions whose houses are too politically useful to insult and too untrustworthy to place near actual vault keys.

Every candidate is tested for greed, pity, awe, disgust, blackmail exposure, familial leverage, heat hunger, Draught sensitivity, memory intrusion, noble obedience reflex, and the ability to watch a dying child without opening a restricted cabinet.

The last test removes more candidates than cowardice.

The Archive does not celebrate this.

It records it.

## ARMOR AND ARMAMENT

Aeteric Guard equipment is expensive, restricted, and intentionally plain.

They wear sealed armor rated against chemical exposure, ash residue, small-arms fire, ceremonial assassination attempts, noble dueling weapons, medical gases, and several forms of grief-induced hallucination. Their helmets include witness filters, identity denial visors, sealed comms, false-face screens, and memory locks.

Their weapons include compact rifles, close-quarter blades, stun projectors, incendiary charges, vault denial mines, ampule destruction pistols, shield emitters, and white-fire burners used to destroy stores rather than allow capture.

The Guard are trained to destroy what they protect.

This is the foundation of their trustworthiness.

A guard who cannot burn the vault is only a thief waiting for permission.

## VAULT DISCIPLINE

Aeteric vault discipline is absolute.

No one enters alone.

No one drinks.

No sample is moved without double seal.

No dying plea alters inventory.

No noble title supersedes chamber law.

No priest blesses a vault without chemical audit of incense.

No machine synod attaches instruments without Ferrum and Mortis counter-witness.

No corpse leaves until searched for hidden distillate crystallization.

No guard assigned to a vault may maintain personal debt to a house with active rejuvenation petition.

No candle from a vault chapel may be burned outside audited airflow.

The final rule exists because citizens have killed for less than rumor.

## RELATION TO OTHER SECURITY BODIES

The Custodia Veritatis investigate threats before the Guard sees them. The Guard distrust them because secret police often arrive with correct authority and insufficient warning.

The Null Court grants seizure, censure, interdiction, and emergency destruction orders. The Guard obey such orders unless ident codes fail validation, in which case everyone present may spend several tense hours discovering whether paperwork or bullets are faster.

The Cohors Noctis may appear if a Legio immortal attempts to seize Aeteric stores. The Guard are instructed not to impede the Noctis unless the Noctis threaten the infrastructure itself. This instruction has produced no comfort.

The Ferrum Sanctum audits machines attached to continuance sites. The Guard audit the auditors' access.

The Collegia Mortis controls many technical procedures. The Guard controls doors.

Many institutional conflicts can be summarized by that sentence.

---

## THREATS

The Aeteric Guard defend against obvious assault and subtle ruin.

Noble raids are common in periods of succession panic.

Cult infiltration increases during famine, plague, or mass mourning.

Didam agents attempt to frame Draught consumption as holy inheritance.

Nezram cells seek resurrection chambers because grief near failed revival can become architecture.

Ferrum heretics attempt to scan distillate behavior for continuance engineering.

Desperate physicians attempt theft to save patients.

High officers attempt unauthorized restoration of lovers, children, predecessors, rivals, pets, witnesses, and once a horse whose legal status remains sealed.

The Guard are instructed to treat all such attempts according to threat, not sympathy.

## PUBLIC MYTH

The citizen imagines the Aeteric Guard as gold-armored saints standing beside immortal heroes.

The reality is colder.

Most Aeteric Guard service occurs in corridors without windows, vault rooms smelling of antiseptic and old metal, hospital sublevels, convoy interiors, sealed elevators, furnace-adjacent laboratories, and silent chambers where something that looks like medicine is worth more than a district.

They are not sung about because proper citizens are not meant to know where they stand.

When they are seen, rumors multiply: that they cannot die, that they drink the Draught daily, that they are selected from children born without fear, that their armor contains founder-bone, that they kill anyone who sees their faces, that they protect a hidden Immog, that they are all sterile, that they are all clones, that they are all already dead.

The Archive confirms none of these rumors.

The Archive denies only the ones that are useful to deny.

## CASES PRESERVED FOR INSTRUCTION

During the **Amber Cistern Assault**, the newly formalized Guard held a distillate reservoir against four mutually hostile infiltrating forces. Guard Captain Eres Vant ordered the reservoir flooded with neutralizing acid when containment seemed likely to fail. Reinforcements arrived before the acid reached the central sump. Vant was decorated for willingness to destroy the asset and reprimanded for damaging the outer valves. Both judgments were correct.

At the **Sainted Ward Breach**, a physician attempted to administer restricted restorative fractions to plague children without authorization. The Guard stopped the theft, secured the stores, and then remained on post while the children died within audible range. Three guards later requested transfer to Mortis Auxilia. One was granted. Two were retained because grief alone is not disqualification unless it changes obedience.

At **Legate Harrow's Seventh Restoration**, the protected Legate awoke compromised and ordered the vault opened for a dead voice he identified as his mother. The Guard sedated him, sealed the chamber, and reported possible Nezram contamination. Harrow later thanked them, then ordered them reassigned because he could not bear their faces. The reassignment was denied.

## FINAL DOCTRINAL NOTE

The Aeteric Guard protect the machinery of second chances from everyone who believes their need is exceptional.

Most needs are exceptional to the person experiencing them.

The vault cannot care.

The Guard cannot care in any way that opens the door.

This is why they are chosen from those who can suffer without converting suffering into permission.

Let them stand in silence.

Let them be supplied without display.

Let no commander forget that immortality infrastructure is not safe because it is sacred.

It is sacred because enough armed people have kept it safe.
