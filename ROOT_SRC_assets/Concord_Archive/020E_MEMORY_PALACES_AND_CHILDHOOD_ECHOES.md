A **Memory Palace** is a Conceptual environment built from remembered places, repeated domestic rites, preserved family phrases, childhood objects, and the longing to return to a time before the world became ash-heavy. A **Childhood Echo** is the mobile or vocal form that emerges from such an environment.

These entities are among the most despised by veteran Custodia officers because they make professionals look cruel. To burn a monster is one thing. To burn a nursery that smells exactly as a dead citizen remembered it is another.

### Formation

Memory Palaces form in refugee populations, long-lived noble houses, preserved schoolrooms, evacuation ships, sealed lower-stack neighborhoods, old orbital habitats, and plague districts where children died faster than records could be updated. They also form around immortals who have outlived everyone who can contradict their preferred childhood.

The entity feeds on the desire for safe return. It strengthens when citizens repeat, *before*, *home*, *when we were*, *as it used to be*, or family diminutives no longer anchored to living persons.

### Manifestation

Doors open into lost kitchens. Corridors smell of old bread. Dead siblings call from under beds. Toys move in ways too careful to be playful. School bells ring on worlds without schools. Mothers sing songs in dialects extinct outside archive cylinders. Soldiers find barracks transformed into childhood homes and lay down rifles because no war was allowed there.

The strongest Childhood Echoes do not threaten. They ask to be tucked in. They ask whether father has returned. They ask why the officer grew old. The correct response feels monstrous and must be performed anyway.

### Hazard

Victims remain voluntarily. They eat meals that do not nourish. They sleep while bodies desiccate. They adopt dead roles. A fifty-year-old officer becomes a twelve-year-old daughter. A widower becomes the husband of a woman dead for sixty years. A noble lord becomes a frightened child and orders his guard to protect the nursery from rescuers.

The Memory Palace preserves identity by replacing it with an earlier version. The process is not healing. It is taxidermy performed from the inside.

### Countermeasures

Use present anchors: current injuries, legal names, unpaid debts, living dependents, recent insults, active orders, adult tasks, and unpleasant bodily needs. Never say *you are safe now* unless the room has been inspected by Mortis instruments. Never allow recovered subjects to keep objects from the Palace. The object will remember the way back.

Required countersign: **Home is not lawful if it refuses the present.**

### Closing Note

The promise of unending childhood is one of the Cinerum's favorite obscenities. It offers innocence by eating every year that came after.
