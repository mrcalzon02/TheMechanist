# 003A LEGIO LINEA
## Concordial Military Archive Record Concerning the Line Formations, Planetary Defense Cohorts, Spire-Hold Garrisons, Civic Muster Rights, Outer Gate Discipline, and the Necessary Ordinary Violence by Which the Extraordinary State Survives

Filed beneath the parent seal of **003 Eternus Legio and Military** by order of the Central Military Archive, witnessed by the Concordia Logistica, cross-noted by the Praetoria Civica, and retained for instruction of planetary legates, spire-hold wardens, district provosts, bonded supply factors, and all officers entrusted with the defense of places too common to be saved by myth and too populous to be abandoned by law.

This record concerns the **Legio Linea**.

No citizen shall mistake the term ordinary for the term lesser.

The Linea are ordinary only in the sense that hunger is ordinary, rain is ordinary, tax is ordinary, and death is ordinary. They are the ranked, counted, issued, rationed, billeted, beaten, instructed, and redeployed soldiery of the Eternus Legio. They stand on walls whose names are never carved into triumphal arches. They sleep in barracks under leaking conduit. They guard ash-gates, ration vaults, water locks, noble approach roads, lower-spire bridges, furnace yards, refugee channels, cineric exclusion lines, and those miserable kilometers of road between one important place and another where all history eventually tries to happen.

The Concord survives because the immortals cannot stand everywhere.

The Legio Linea stand in their stead.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Legio Linea are the standard planetary and spire-defense formations of the Eternus Legio. They are not militia, though they often absorb militia. They are not civil police, though their presence may quiet riots more reliably than any civil writ. They are not noble house guards, though noble houses are perpetually attempting to rent, influence, redirect, flatter, starve, marry, purchase, or ceremonially adopt them. They are not penal troops, though many of their best veterans have committed crimes that the Concord judged more useful under arms than under ash.

Linea formations hold ground.

That is the beginning and end of their sanctity.

Where the Cohortes Ferrata break hardpoints, where the Custodia Cineris hunt conceptual infection, where the Limes Wardens vanish into outer dead districts, and where the Cohors Noctis corrects treason too dangerous to name, the Linea remain with the walls, the roads, the generators, the bridgeheads, and the citizens. Their duty is not glamorous enough for song until the song has become a lament. Their failures are measured in breached gates, ration stampedes, dead children, extinguished districts, and spire crowns forced to notice the existence of the foundations below them.

A Legio Linea cohort may be assigned to a hive foundation trench for sixty years and never once fire upon an enemy worthy of the word. Another may be raised at sunrise, issued rifles by noon, and dead beneath a Cineric breach by midnight. The Archive recognizes no moral distinction between these services. One prevents the need for the other. Both are forms of war.

## ORIGIN AND CONTINUITY

The earliest Linea were not called Linea. They were wall-soldiers, road-spears, furnace guards, shield levies, distiller camp sentries, ash-dike watchers, and the last frightened remnants of local armies pressed beneath Immogic command during the Continuity Wars. The name became formal only after the Fourth Consolidation of Military Measure, when the Legio required a term for soldiers who were neither immortal officers nor specialist cohorts nor noble retinues nor emergency civic levies.

The term **Linea** was taken from the old command phrase: *hold the line until the line becomes law*.

The phrase is older than the current Concord.

It has been found scratched into bunker doors, carved into ration crates, welded onto the interior of armored shutters, tattooed across the backs of condemned soldiers, and written in soot on the inside of a child shelter whose Linea garrison died outside the door after delaying a breach long enough for the shelter furnace to seal.

Legio doctrine does not consider the Linea the birth of military glory. It considers them the proof that glory must eventually learn to stand watch.

## CIVIC MYTH AND CIVIC ERROR

Lower citizens usually know the Linea by their boots.

They hear the boots on stairwells during ration disputes, on metal bridges during curfew, on noble parade roads during mourning processions, and on ash-clotted paving when a district has been declared uncertain. Children imitate the boots before they understand the uniforms. Street vendors curse the boots and sell them hot broth. Cultists listen for the boots and plan their escape through the vents. The dying ask for the boots because boots mean somebody still holds the corridor.

The common error is to believe the Linea are the army of the people.

They are not.

They are the army between the people and the end of the people.

This distinction is not gentle.

The Linea may distribute bread in the morning and fire into a bread riot by evening if the riot endangers the district ration vault. They may rescue a hab-block from flood and then execute the same block's water thieves at dawn. They may salute a saint's procession and later cordon the chapel because the saint's relic has begun whispering in the wrong tense. They may carry infants from a breach shelter and then burn the same shelter with the infants' grandparents still inside if the ash marks have spread beyond recovery.

The Linea are loved by citizens in the abstract, feared in the corridor, begged for in catastrophe, blamed in scarcity, and forgotten during peace.

This is the correct order of things.

## STRUCTURE OF A LINEA HOLDING FORCE

A Linea holding force is organized according to district scale, threat maturity, logistical depth, and the degree to which local maps can be trusted. A properly formed Linea command contains infantry ranks, gun sections, engineers, ration officers, chapel-detail, medical orderlies, signal clerks, ash-watch sentries, execution witnesses, local guide auxiliaries, corpse-taggers, and at least one officer trained to recognize when an ordinary crime has ceased to be ordinary.

The smallest recognized Linea body is the **watch-file**, commonly five to nine soldiers under a file-sergeant. It is sufficient for corridor guard, checkpoint duty, convoy escort, prisoner movement, furnace-yard security, and the public posture of lawful force.

The **wall-section** is the standard defensive body assigned to fixed architecture: gates, bridges, locks, pump houses, tower stairs, power nodes, cableways, and spire-access lifts. Wall-sections are expected to become intimately familiar with their assigned terrain. A wall-section that does not know where water pools after a pressure failure is not considered trained. A wall-section that does not know which children throw stones at which blind corner is not considered awake.

The **district cohort** is the most common meaningful Linea command. It can hold a hab-zone, spire quarter, road interchange, ration complex, industrial strip, or breach perimeter. District cohorts are expected to maintain civil maps, casualty ledgers, bridge demolition charges, emergency ration keys, and lists of citizens useful during collapse.

The **planetary line command** coordinates all Linea bodies on a planet, moon, orbital stack, dead belt colony, or chained spire complex. Its commander is usually a mortal Legate-Subaltern, an old rejuvenate, or a minor immortal officer too useful to be wasted on ceremonial politics and too dull to be assassinated by nobles without embarrassment.

## RECRUITMENT AND THE LINE OATH

Linea recruitment is broad because walls are broad.

A Linea soldier may come from hereditary barracks clans, frontier settlements, spire labor families, ash-dike communities, factory dormitories, noble debt households, municipal levies, orphaned districts, redeemed criminal stocks, or battlefield absorption of surviving local defenders. The Legio prefers recruits who understand hunger, heat, crowd pressure, locked doors, and the sound a family makes when told evacuation capacity is exhausted.

The Line Oath is deliberately plain:

*I stand where placed. I hold until relieved. I spend my life according to measure. I do not abandon the living to the ash. I do not yield the dead their claim. I obey the line because beyond it there is no law.*

Every variant is inferior.

Certain noble academies attempt to embellish the oath with devotional flourishes. The Central Military Archive marks such embellishments as vanity contamination. A soldier does not need poetry to understand a gate. A soldier needs to know that the gate must remain closed until ordered otherwise, even when the voices outside know the soldier's childhood name.

## TRAINING OF THE ORDINARY SOLDIER

Linea training is not designed to produce heroes. It is designed to delay panic, standardize obedience, and make terror more expensive for the enemy.

The recruit learns rifle discipline, barricade construction, stairwell fighting, crowd control, ration convoy security, breach recognition, ash-mask fitting, casualty marking, water discipline, corpse distance, firebreak procedure, and the rules governing when a civilian becomes a threat, when a threat becomes a carrier, and when a carrier becomes terrain.

The recruit is taught never to mock superstition until the superstition has been audited.

The recruit is taught never to accept orders from a dead officer unless the officer's current legal status has been verified.

The recruit is taught that a mother carrying a child may be a mother, a carrier, a bait-saint, a mourning cultist, a Didam hunger vessel, a Nezram grief vector, an ash-split echo, a noble courier, a genuine refugee, or all of these in sequence. The correct response is not cruelty. The correct response is procedure.

Failure of procedure is how cities die while everyone involved insists they were being humane.

## ARMAMENT AND EQUIPMENT

Linea equipment is plain by the standards of the Legio and miraculous by the standards of most districts.

Standard issue includes sealed combat coats, layered ash-cloth, reinforced helmets, respirator masks, compact rifles, bayonets, heat tabs, ration strips, civic identification slates, flare capsules, wound-glue, corpse tags, breach chalk, and a small manual of emergency orders printed on material that can survive blood, rain, ash, and a soldier attempting to eat it during starvation psychosis.

Heavy sections receive crew guns, mortar tubes, light artillery crawlers, shield shutters, demolition charges, portable searchlights, and breach-staple launchers. Spire defense units receive vertical harnesses, lift-lock tools, bridge mines, cable cutters, and descent coffins. Industrial guards receive heat armor, chemical hoods, and legal permission to destroy machines that have begun operating without accepted command.

No Linea unit shall be issued distillate weaponry without higher seal.

No Linea unit shall be issued Cine-derived armor without Ferrum notation and Mortis witness.

No Linea unit shall be permitted unsupervised access to immortality infrastructure unless temporarily subordinated to the Aeteric Guard.

This is not distrust.

This is memory.

## RELATION TO OTHER LEGIO ARMS

The Cohortes Ferrata call the Linea mud-walls, boot-ranks, and gate furniture. The Linea call the Ferrata fuel gluttons, steel peacocks, and excellent cover when moving under fire. The hostility is old, useful, and suspended whenever the enemy is near.

The Custodia Cineris regard the Linea as both shield and hazard. A Linea cordon can save a district. An ignorant Linea cordon can also concentrate conceptual infection until the cordon becomes a choir of doom. Linea officers assigned to Cineric-threat theaters therefore receive custody primers and learn the first lesson of anti-Conceptual war: never repeat the enemy's phrase simply to prove one heard it.

The Praetoria Civica consider the Linea too blunt for civil enforcement. The Linea consider the Praetoria too interested in paperwork during shootings. Both are correct.

The Limes Wardens draw heavily from Linea veterans who have already learned to be lonely without becoming useless. A warden who has never held a gate is merely a wanderer with a rifle.

The Mortis Auxilia march behind the Linea after the line has held or failed. The relationship between the two arms is quiet. The Linea make corpses. The Mortis Auxilia move them, burn them, tag them, question them when permitted, and occasionally discover that a dead Linea soldier is still trying to hold the same wall.

The Aeteric Guard regard the Linea as population weather: too numerous to know individually, too important to ignore, and disastrous when moving in panic.

## DISCIPLINE AND PUNISHMENT

Linea discipline is severe because ordinary soldiers are numerous enough to become catastrophe if loosely held.

Desertion from ordinary post during ordinary threat is punished by firing squad.

Desertion during breach threat is punished by public hanging at the abandoned post, if the post still exists.

Unauthorized mercy that causes containment failure is prosecuted as mass negligent murder.

Unauthorized cruelty that causes rebellion is prosecuted as strategic vandalism.

The Linea are expected to be hard without becoming wasteful, obedient without becoming stupid, and afraid without becoming decorative. Officers who mistake brutality for discipline are removed when time permits and shot when time does not. Officers who mistake softness for virtue are treated similarly, though often after more paperwork because soft officers tend to write explanations.

## NOTABLE LINEA FORMATIONS

The Archive preserves the names of certain Linea bodies not because they were unique, but because they were ordinary under impossible conditions.

The **Seventh Bridgeward of Turris Vey** held a lower-spire transit bridge for thirty-one hours after both approaches had been eaten by ash-fire. Their final transmission consisted only of bridge load calculations and a request for boots in smaller sizes for the children being carried across.

The **Grey Ration Cohort of Old Marrow District** executed its own quartermaster, opened the grain vault under emergency seal, fed eighty thousand citizens, and then surrendered itself to Praetoria custody. The Null Court later ruled the execution unlawful, the vault opening lawful, the feeding necessary, and the surrender administratively elegant.

The **Ninety-Second Gate File** remained at its checkpoint for twelve years after the district evacuation order failed to reach them. When discovered, they had become a hereditary checkpoint household, maintained full salute discipline, and had collected tolls from three generations of smugglers in the name of an officer whose death certificate predated their grandchildren.

The **Ashmark Twenty-One** fired upon an approaching column wearing their own faces. Eleven survived. None asked to be relieved.

## FINAL DOCTRINAL NOTE

The Legio Linea do not preserve civilization by winning the grand wars.

They preserve civilization by ensuring that there remains a place from which grand wars may be fought, supplied, mourned, taxed, justified, and lied about afterward.

Where the citizen sees a checkpoint, the Archive sees a hinge upon which the continuity of law may turn.

Where the noble sees a garrison, the Archive sees the first obstacle between entitlement and panic.

Where the enemy sees ordinary soldiers, the Archive sees an error.

The ordinary soldier is the most dangerous soldier to underestimate, because the ordinary soldier is the one the world has taught to endure ordinary misery before the extraordinary arrives.

Let the line hold.

Let the line be fed.

Let the line be remembered before it must die to become visible.
