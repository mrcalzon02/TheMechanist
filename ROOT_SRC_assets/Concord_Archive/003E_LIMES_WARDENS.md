# 003E LIMES WARDENS
## Concordial Military Archive Record Concerning Frontier Troops, Dead-World Watches, Breach-Zone Garrisons, Outer-District Patrols, Boundary Stones, Long Silence Posts, and the Men and Women Assigned to Places Civilization Has Not Yet Reclaimed and Refuses to Admit It Has Lost

Filed beneath the parent seal of **003 Eternus Legio and Military**, cross-referenced with **002 The Cinerum and Ash Sea**, **014 Liminal Office and Void Travel**, the outer district ledgers of the Concordia Logistica, and all dead-world registers not yet eaten by their own dust.

This record concerns the **Limes Wardens**.

The word *limes* denotes the boundary.

Citizens imagine boundaries as walls.

Wardens know that most boundaries are habits maintained by exhausted people in bad weather.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Limes Wardens are the frontier troops of the Eternus Legio assigned to dead worlds, breach zones, outer districts, ash peripheries, abandoned infrastructure belts, failed settlements, quarantine borders, and remote holdings where the Concord's authority persists by patrol, signal, memory, and occasional artillery.

They serve where maps become apologetic.

They serve where the last census is older than the youngest ghost.

They serve where a road may lead to a district, a crater, a cult, a functioning pump maintained by one stubborn family, or a doorway that was not present yesterday.

Their duty is not conquest. Their duty is boundary maintenance. They mark what remains inside law, what has fallen outside it, what may be recovered, what must be watched, what must be burned if it moves, and what must be lied about until evacuation capacity exists.

---

## ORIGIN

The Limes Wardens descend from survey soldiers, ash-dike watchers, dead-world scouts, outer gate patrols, road wardens, quarantine pickets, and Legio remnants left behind when larger campaigns moved onward. The formal arm was created after the Third Periphery Retreat, when the Concord discovered that many places declared lost had not actually died, while many places declared stable had become graveyards with tax records.

Central authority required a class of soldier able to live amid uncertainty without immediately simplifying it into cowardice, optimism, or madness.

The Wardens became that class.

The first Warden tablets record five duties:

Mark the edge.

Watch the edge.

Feed what can still answer.

Kill what crosses wrongly.

Report even when no answer comes.

No superior doctrine has been produced.

---

## THE FRONTIER CONDITION

A frontier is not a place far away.

A frontier is any place where the Concord's claim is stronger than its presence.

A lower-spire maintenance void may be frontier if no civil office has entered it in thirty years. A dead agricultural moon may be frontier if one fortified grain convent still signals twice each winter. A noble-owned industrial valley may be frontier if its maps are false, its workers hereditary, and its furnaces whisper at night. An orbital stack may be frontier if its lower decks have become tribal and the elevators no longer stop.

The Limes Wardens are therefore found not only at the edge of empire but at the edge of attention.

Their patrols pass through forgotten water galleries, ash-choked streets, collapsed transit lines, outer hab belts, quarantine villages, dead manufactoria, feral shrine complexes, breach observation towers, frontier forts, listening posts, signal cairns, and those stretches of wasteland where even the Cinerum appear to have grown bored and left something worse behind.

---

## RECRUITMENT

Wardens are recruited from Linea veterans, frontier-born families, failed settlers, pardoned smugglers, ash guides, dead-world hunters, road clans, exiled nobles, quiet criminals, Liminal-adjacent scouts who did not rate formal office training, and soldiers whose commanders have noted an unusual ability to endure isolation without inventing doctrine.

The last trait is precious.

Isolation breeds prophets.

The Wardens require witnesses.

Before assignment, candidates undergo the **Long Silence Trial**, during which they are stationed in a sealed observation post with limited food, unreliable heat, and a false signal schedule. The trial is not meant to test bravery. It tests whether the candidate begins inventing messages when none arrive.

Those who invent messages are reassigned.

Those who report silence accurately are considered dangerous enough to trust.

---

## ORGANIZATION

The smallest Warden formation is the **marker pair**: two soldiers assigned to verify boundary stones, signal posts, road shrines, quarantine markers, and outer gates. They work in pairs because a lone Warden is a rumor waiting to become a cult.

A **road file** patrols routes between inhabited points. Road files carry maps that include several categories unknown to civilian cartography, including road present, road absent, road resentful, road repeats, and road leads to destination but not in useful order.

A **watch company** holds forts, towers, dead-world stations, breach observation posts, and frontier gates.

A **limes commandery** oversees a broader frontier region. Its commander may govern more empty territory than a noble duke and fewer living citizens than a market street. Such imbalances are considered normal.

---

## EQUIPMENT

Wardens are issued long-weather coats, respirator hoods, survival packs, signal rods, boundary nails, compact rifles, long rifles, flare prayers, ash compasses, water testers, corpse flags, heat stones, map slates, climbing gear, field furnaces, ration concentrate, and sealed letters to be opened only if command silence exceeds legal duration.

Most Wardens carry personal charms despite regulation. The Archive tolerates this where the charms remain private, non-repetitive, and do not acquire followers.

Wardens prefer reliable tools over impressive tools. A rifle that fires after three winters in ash fog is better than a noble plasma piece requiring a priest, a battery choir, and a mood. Their vehicles are ugly, overfiltered, underarmed by Ferrata standards, and beloved because they start.

---

## RELATION TO THE LIMINAL OFFICE

The Wardens and the Liminal Office share borders but not temperaments.

Liminal personnel sense routes through the Ashen Sea and the deep black of transit. Wardens walk the physical aftermath of routes that failed, thinned, leaked, or were opened by fools. Liminal choirs may guide ships between worlds. Wardens mark where something from between worlds touched soil and did not properly leave.

Wardens distrust Liminal personnel because ash-tuned minds weep black tears, seek heat compulsively, and sometimes describe frontier terrain using words that later become accurate.

Liminal personnel distrust Wardens because Wardens put fences across places that navigation wants left open.

Both distrust anyone who says the boundary is symbolic.

---

## DEAD WORLDS

A dead world is rarely entirely dead.

If it were entirely dead, the Limes Wardens would not be required.

Dead worlds contain sealed vaults, feral descendants, automated pumps, forgotten armies, frozen laws, noble claims, ash-storm migrations, salvage cults, Cineric scars, Mortis laboratories, machine shrines, illegal Liminal experiments, and occasionally one old woman with a shotgun insisting that taxes were paid before the sky burned.

Warden protocol on dead worlds is conservative. They do not promise rescue without capacity. They do not declare extinction without corpse or silence index. They do not drink local water until the water has failed to speak for three consecutive tests. They do not sleep in buildings where all doors face inward.

The Warden's task is to determine whether the dead world may be reclaimed, watched, harvested, quarantined, or forgotten more honestly.

---

## BREACH ZONES

Breach zones are not battlefields.

They are wounds in geography.

A breach zone may be stable for a century and then open because a child sings the wrong inherited song. It may appear closed while still altering dreams. It may produce no monsters and yet ruin every crop planted within sight of it. It may be physically small and legally enormous.

Wardens assigned to breach zones maintain markers, exclusion lines, listening posts, heat beacons, false trails, and kill corridors. They record animal behavior, ash drift, sound distortion, unauthorized pilgrimage, repeated graffiti, corpse movement, and weather that appears to be waiting.

Custodia Cineris cells are called when breach behavior becomes interpretive.

Mortis examiners are called when corpses become informative.

Ferrata support is called when the breach produces something rude enough to require mass.

---

## OUTER DISTRICTS

Outer districts are the frontiers inside cities.

Spire citizens prefer to imagine their cities as governed from crown to root. Wardens know many lower and outer districts are held by habit, road toll, old oath, gang treaty, furnace dependency, Praetoria raid, or the fact that the surrounding wastes are worse.

Limes Wardens assigned to outer districts behave more like patient invaders than guards. They map families, water points, illegal chapels, gang routes, elevator ghosts, old military bunkers, forgotten machine rooms, vent tribes, plague corridors, food fungus, and places where official architecture has become folklore.

They are often the first Concordial soldiers an outer child sees who are not arriving to arrest someone.

This makes them dangerous symbols.

The Custodia Cineris monitors Warden folk-hero formation closely.

---

## CULTURE OF THE WARDENS

Wardens are quiet by custom and necessity. Noise travels badly at boundaries and meaning travels worse.

They keep weather journals, road bones, signal tallies, private maps, and small grudges against central offices that demand daily reports from places where daily survival is already an administrative triumph.

They are infamous for answering elaborate orders with brief confirmations, and brief orders with long lists of reasons the order misunderstands the terrain.

Wardens do not enjoy parade. When forced to attend, they stand near exits and evaluate the building for defensibility. This has offended many nobles and saved two.

---

## FINAL DOCTRINAL NOTE

The Limes Wardens are the Concord's admission that civilization is not a solid thing.

It frays.

It thins.

It forgets its own edges.

The Wardens walk those edges and keep them from becoming invitations.

Let no court redraw a boundary without asking who will stand there.

Let no noble claim a frontier without paying for its watch.

Let no central office declare a place safe merely because no recent report has contradicted it.

Silence is not safety.

Silence is only silence until the Warden arrives to measure it.
