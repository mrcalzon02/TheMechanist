# 003B COHORTES FERRATA
## Concordial Military Archive Record Concerning the Iron Cohorts, Armored Lances, Mechanized Siege Bodies, Furnace-Drives, Road-Breaking Doctrines, and the Steel Discipline Required When Velocity Becomes Law

Issued under the parent seal of **003 Eternus Legio and Military**, machine-witnessed by the Ferrum Sanctum, ration-noted by the Concordia Logistica, casualty-crossed by the Collegia Mortis, and restricted from circulation among civilian guilds whose admiration of armored vehicles exceeds their comprehension of fuel expenditure.

This record concerns the **Cohortes Ferrata**.

The citizen hears engines and thinks rescue.

The enemy hears engines and thinks flight.

The Logistica hears engines and begins to count the ruin of its ledgers.

All three responses are correct.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Cohortes Ferrata are the armored and mechanized formations of the Eternus Legio. They include tank cohorts, armored infantry carriers, siege crawlers, assault walkers, breaching engines, self-propelled artillery, protected convoy bodies, road-forging machines, armored recovery temples, and the sworn crews who treat a vehicle not as property but as a moving oath with guns.

They exist because some gates cannot be held by men alone.

They exist because some cities must be entered through walls that refuse negotiation.

They exist because open ground under ashfall belongs to whatever can cross it while being shot, burned, cursed, lied to by the dead, and starved of clean air.

The Ferrata are not merely soldiers inside machines. They are the Legio's declaration that machinery, when properly fed and disciplined, may become a temporary civic geography. A tank is a wall that has decided to move. A carrier is a district corridor with armor. A siege crawler is a courthouse that renders judgment by pressure, flame, and collapse.

---

## ORIGIN OF THE IRON COHORTS

The first Ferrata were improvised from industrial haulers, ash-mining crawlers, noble hunting engines, distillery tractors, furnace hulls, and dead-world survey machines plated with scavenged armor. During the early Continuity Wars, the Legio discovered that courage could not carry heavy guns through ash dunes quickly enough to matter. It discovered also that many Cineric things were not impressed by banners, prayers, or infantry lines, but could still be delayed by mass, heat, and repeated impact.

The Ferrata were born from this impolite revelation.

The earliest armored lances were cursed by every office required to support them. The Logistica called them moving famine. The Ferrum Sanctum called them crude but promising. The Undying Lords called them inefficient until one of their manufactories was saved by six smoke-belching refinery crawlers mounting naval guns. After that, the Undying Lords called them sacred invoices.

By the Second Iron Codification, Ferrata doctrine had separated from ordinary Linea command. A machine that consumes a district's monthly fuel in an afternoon cannot be governed by the same habits as a wall-section with rifles. The Ferrata required their own ration law, recovery law, repair caste, crew oath, and execution discipline for officers who mistook armored speed for strategic intelligence.

## THE IRON OATH

A Ferrata crew does not swear only to command.

It swears to the hull.

The Iron Oath is spoken with one hand on the vehicle skin while the engine is cold:

*I enter iron by permission. I move only by measure. I spend fuel as blood and armor as bone. I do not abandon the hull while the hull can carry war. I do not burn the road for vanity. I do not confuse speed with victory. I do not mistake the machine's voice for my own will.*

The final line was added after the Karsic Engine Mutinies, when seven armored vehicles continued advancing for three days after their crews had died, broadcasting devotional hymns in the voices of their former commanders. The Ferrum Sanctum maintains that the incident was caused by damaged command relays. The Collegia Mortis maintains that three of the crews were still legally alive at the time of recovery. The Custodia Cineris recommends that no surviving transcript be read aloud.

## INTERNAL ORGANIZATION

The basic Ferrata body is the **iron lance**, composed of three to five armored vehicles and their support foot, typically bound to a Linea or independent theater command. Iron lances are used for counterattack, road seizure, breakthrough, evacuation extraction, and shock intervention where ordinary formations have begun to fold.

The **breach troop** specializes in wall-breaking, gate reduction, tunnel collapse, and fortified district entry. Its vehicles often carry ram engines, thermal cutters, drilling heads, pressure hammers, smoke projectors, and short-range weapons intended to make architecture reconsider its loyalty.

The **casket column** is an armored convoy body assigned to move persons or substances too valuable to expose and too dangerous to delay. Casket columns have carried living Legates, sealed Draught ampules, Mortis relics, condemned nobles, Liminal choirs, Null Court judgments, and once an entire chapel believed to contain one measurable particle of first distillate in its altar wax.

The **furnace wing** consists of heavy assault vehicles assigned to ash-threat zones and industrial hellscapes. Their crews are trained to operate under heat, smoke, breath poison, and intermittent conceptual intrusion. Furnace wing veterans often develop a habit of sleeping only near running engines.

The **siege body** is a major Ferrata formation capable of reducing cities, spire roots, dead manufactoria, rebel noble redoubts, and Cineric-held hard terrain. No siege body may be deployed without Logistica countersign unless the theater commander is prepared to explain what population shall starve to keep it moving.

## MACHINES OF THE FERRATA

The Ferrata divide their machines by function rather than by vanity.

**Line Breakers** are frontal assault tanks and heavy crawlers built to take fire while opening movement corridors.

**Cinder Hounds** are faster armored vehicles used for pursuit, road denial, reconnaissance in hostile districts, and the unpleasant task of chasing things that run like men but cast shadows like wells.

**Reliquary Carriers** are armored transports modified to move high-value persons, wounded immortals, legal witnesses, restricted archives, and sealed corpses. Their interiors contain more locks than seats.

**Mortar Crawlers** and **Gun Chapels** provide indirect fire. Their crews maintain target ledgers in both ballistic notation and moral notation, because the Legio has learned that a shell fired into the wrong shrine can create an enemy that artillery cannot kill.

**Recovery Engines** retrieve damaged vehicles under fire. Ferrata doctrine gives recovery crews unusual authority. A dead tank can block a road, poison a district, leak sanctified fuel, shelter enemies, or become an idol. Recovery is therefore not maintenance. It is battlefield law.

## RELATIONSHIP WITH THE FERRUM SANCTUM AND UNDYING LORDS

No Ferrata record is honest unless it acknowledges the machine houses.

The Cohortes Ferrata depend upon the Undying Lords for hulls, engines, optics, power relays, armor composites, replacement tracks, ammunition feeds, and the thousand obscure components without which an armored spear becomes a metal tomb. They depend also upon the Ferrum Sanctum to police the doctrinal purity of that machinery and to ensure that no prohibited intelligence, Heliun remnant, corrupt synodic experiment, or unauthorized continuance device has been hidden inside the command stack.

This dependence is a chain around the throat.

The Ferrata admire machines. The Undying Lords own many of them. The Ferrum Sanctum judges their souls. The Logistica counts their hunger. The Legio commands their movement. The crew sits inside the result and prays the argument was resolved before the shell lands.

Ferrata officers are therefore trained in machine politics. They know which house produces engines that overheat in ash fog, which synod overcharges for pressure seals, which Ferrum inspector can be bribed only by rare schematics, which noble patron has confused armored warfare with parade spectacle, and which maintenance prayer actually refers to a coolant valve.

---

## LOGISTICAL SIN

The Ferrata are the most beautiful way to ruin a supply plan.

Fuel, oil, shells, spare parts, road plates, bridge ratings, recovery teams, crew replacements, machine-seers, ammunition trains, smoke charges, air filters, engine seals, coolant, traction shoes, and sanctified repair compounds must all arrive before the armored order can become more than sculpture.

A Ferrata commander who outruns supply is not bold. He is a murderer of his own future.

A Logistica factor who underfeeds Ferrata formations is not frugal. He is donating armor to the enemy.

A noble who demands an armored parade during ration crisis is not patriotic. He is a thief wearing medals.

The Archive records several instances of Ferrata crews executing aristocratic officials who attempted to redirect fuel reserves for ceremonial illumination while outer districts were under breach pressure. In three cases the executions were later ruled unlawful. In all three, the executed officials remained dead, which the Ferrata considered an acceptable administrative compromise.

## BATTLEFIELD DOCTRINE

Ferrata doctrine is built around four principles.

First: armor is movement with consequence.

Second: a vehicle unsupported by infantry is an expensive coffin waiting for someone with patience.

Third: infantry unsupported by armor in open killing ground is a prayer pretending to be a tactic.

Fourth: no machine is immune to the ash.

The Ferrata break enemy lines, provide hardened movement, suppress breach concentrations, smash rebel fortifications, escort evacuation columns, open roads through dead districts, and serve as moving anchors around which Linea forces reorganize. Their greatest victories occur when no one notices them because the convoy arrived, the gate was reinforced, the refugees crossed, or the enemy never reached the wall.

Their most infamous failures occur when commanders mistake armored confidence for invulnerability. The ash loves sealed hulls. The dead love intercoms. Hunger cults love fuel depots. Nezram grief phenomena love crew compartments where voices echo. Didam vessels love men trapped inside warm metal.

---

## CREW CULTURE

Ferrata crews are clannish, filthy, superstitious, technically literate, rude to infantry, rude to nobles, obedient to mechanics, and emotionally attached to machines that are often older than their grandparents.

They name their vehicles with names too vulgar for archive preservation and too beloved to erase from hull plates. They keep crew-dead tokens wired beneath seats. They feed engines ceremonial heat before assaults. They paint kill marks, rescue marks, breach marks, and shame marks. The shame marks are more closely guarded than the kills.

The crew believes the hull listens.

Ferrum doctrine forbids declaring this belief literally true unless tested.

Mortis doctrine advises against testing.

---

## PUBLIC REPUTATION

To citizens, the Ferrata are thunder in streets too narrow for hope.

A Ferrata column entering a starving district may cause panic, relief, rage, theft, prayer, and spontaneous children attempting to touch the armor for luck. In lower spires, the arrival of armored carriers is often interpreted as proof that the Concord has remembered the district. In noble quarters, the same arrival is interpreted as either protection or accusation, depending upon where the barrels point.

The Ferrata are celebrated in sanctioned murals because armored vehicles are easier to paint than casualty statistics. The murals rarely show the fuel trains, the crushed homes, the burned-out crew compartments, or the Linea soldiers walking behind the machines checking gutters for survivors.

---

## FINAL DOCTRINAL NOTE

The Cohortes Ferrata are not the iron heart of the Legio.

They are the iron fist, the iron lung, the iron gate, and occasionally the iron mistake.

Used properly, they turn despair into road.

Used foolishly, they turn supply into smoke and courage into wreckage.

Let the engine be fed.

Let the hull be watched.

Let the machine move only where law is prepared to follow.
