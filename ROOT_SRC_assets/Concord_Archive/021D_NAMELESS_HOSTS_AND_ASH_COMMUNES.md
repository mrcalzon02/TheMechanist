The **Nameless Host** is what remains when an Unburdened cell survives long enough for legal identity to become ornamental. Members may still possess bodies, skills, habits, and affection, but their names have loosened from personhood. They answer for each other. They remember through each other. They confess in plural. They distribute guilt, grief, hunger, and sometimes wounds.

**Ash Communes** are territory stabilized around such Hosts. They are not always violent. Some maintain gardens, clinics, child care, and food stores. This makes them politically tempting to exhausted frontier governors and morally infuriating to enforcement offices.

### Administrative Crisis

The Concord is built upon registered persons. A Nameless Host is an attack on the registry without needing to burn a ledger. Taxes fail. Marriage fails. guilt fails. inheritance fails. military conscription fails. Corpse handling fails because no body belongs cleanly to the name attached to it.

The Collegia Mortis considers this an existential hazard. The Logistica considers it a distribution nightmare. The Custodia considers it a door with voting habits.

### Field Behavior

Hosts frequently say, *we are lighter now*. They may surrender peacefully if addressed collectively but become distressed if one member is named too specifically. Children raised inside Ash Communes often understand individual names as tools used by outsiders to isolate pain.

Some Hosts become stable enough to negotiate. These negotiations are forbidden without Custodia, Mortis, and Null Court presence. A Host that negotiates successfully may spread by seeming reasonable.

### Countermeasures

Restore individual stakes. Use private memories, debts, injuries, skills, craft marks, personal tools, and kinship disputes. The goal is not cruelty. It is re-particularization. Persons can be saved. Weather cannot.

Field maxim: **A crowd that owns no name may still own a door.**
