# 005B WATER MEASURE OFFICE AND CISTERN WRITS
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Water Allotment, Pump Law, Cistern Seals, Contamination Schedules, Drought Courts, and the Liquid Arithmetic by Which Spires Continue Pretending They Are Not Built Above Thirst

Filed beneath the parent seal of **005 Concordia Logistica**, in joint custody with the Turris infrastructure ledgers, Praetoria Civica riot-prevention office, Collegia Mortis contamination desk, and Null Court miswording review, after repeated incidents in which careless language, broken pumps, noble pressure, or unauthorized compassion converted water into rebellion.

This record concerns the **Water Measure Office**, its cistern courts, pump registries, drought writs, and pipe-claim tribunals.

Water is the first ration and the last blackmail.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Water Measure Office controls lawful access to potable, tolerable, industrial, ritual, military, medical, agricultural, coolant, corpse-processing, and contaminated water within Concordial authority. It is older than the current ration system because bodies can survive insult longer than thirst.

The Office does not merely distribute water. It defines water.

That definition matters. Liquid drawn from a noble reservoir, ash-filter sump, battlefield condenser, Mortis sluice, lower-stack pipe, temple font, industrial coolant line, or Liminal heat bath may all appear wet to the foolish. To the Office each belongs to a different legal reality. A citizen who drinks the wrong category may be guilty of theft, contamination, sacrilege, self-harm, sabotage, or treason against infrastructure.

The Office's oldest maxim states:

*No empire falls from lack of water. It falls from uncertainty over who may drink first.*

---

## CISTERN WRITS

A Cistern Writ is the binding document by which a reservoir, pump chain, fog condenser, deep well, runoff vault, ice import, purification engine, or emergency bladder is attached to a jurisdiction. The writ specifies ownership, access, flow rate, contamination tolerance, class priority, repair authority, shutoff hierarchy, and sacrificial reduction schedule.

The lay citizen imagines pipes as neutral tubes.

There is no neutral pipe.

Every pipe carries precedence. Every valve has a social theory behind it. Every pump schedule is a political treaty between height, money, force, habit, and the willingness of those below to continue being below.

Cistern Writs are written in a dense form because clarity invites litigation. They are also written densely because most old cities have inherited water systems from prior ruins, dead noble houses, extinct machine synods, emergency war governments, refugee occupations, cult seizures, plague quarantines, and desperate repairs performed by people too dead to explain why they welded one district's drinking line through another district's latrine wall.

The Office therefore treats water maps as sacred lies.

They are necessary. They are wrong. They must still be obeyed until corrected under seal.

---

## DROUGHT COURTS AND SHUTOFF AUTHORITY

Drought Courts are convened when supply falls below registered demand or when the Office requires authority to make visible the deprivation that has already begun in secret. Their judgments can redirect flow between floors, revoke industrial usage, suspend bathing, drain private reservoirs, flood furnace coolers, close public fountains, prioritize troop columns, or seal entire lower districts under non-essential consumption penalty.

To those unfamiliar with Logistica doctrine, the Drought Court appears cruel because it may forbid drinking while water remains physically present nearby. This is not contradiction. It is allocation.

The most infamous near-collapse involved a high judge's phrase preserved in Null Court cautionary training: *All unauthorized H2O consumption below the Ninth Register is hereby interdicted until contamination audit conclusion.* The phrase was intended to prevent drinking from a poisoned line. Interpreted by a frightened district office without timely clarification, it criminalized ordinary drinking across several lower habitation bands for nine days. By the time correction arrived, citizens had died, gangs had seized condensers, and a shrine had declared tears exempt from interdiction.

Since that case, Water Measure judges are forbidden to use chemical notation in public decrees unless accompanied by three plain-language drinking clauses and a Mortis projection of likely stupidity.

The rule is observed inconsistently.

## CONTAMINATION DOCTRINE

The Water Measure Office maintains distinct contamination categories: ash-taint, corpse-taint, metal bloom, fungal bloom, conceptual taint, Draught-adjacent residue, industrial solvent intrusion, noble pharmaceutical runoff, Liminal breach condensation, and ordinary sewage. Each category has different permitted uses and different lies told to the public.

Ash-tainted water may be used in certain machines before it may be used on skin.

Corpse-tainted water may be rendered for Mortis work before it may be returned to fields.

Conceptually tainted water may look, taste, and measure as pure while producing dreams in which citizens learn songs they were never taught. Such water must be burned, sealed, or given to test rats that are not permitted to become interesting.

Draught-adjacent residue requires immediate chain-of-custody transfer. Unauthorized drinking of such liquid is both medical hazard and noble theft.

Temple fonts are audited discreetly. The Cultus Vitae dislikes this. The Office replies that holiness has never prevented algae.

## THE OFFICE AS BUREAUCRATIC CORPSE

Water records outlive water. The Office has entire sub-vaults filled with cistern rights belonging to houses that no longer exist, districts buried under newer districts, wells that were bricked shut before the Heliun Divide, pipes that have been dry for centuries, and reservoirs whose names are used in tax law but whose locations are unknown.

Because old rights can be revived, no dead water claim is fully dead.

This creates the swollen corpse quality common to Concordia Logistica. Every drought awakens documents. Every repair threatens inheritance. Every pipe dug from a wall may carry an ownership seal older than the building surrounding it. Engineers call for cutting. Lawyers call for review. Citizens call for water. The Office calls for forms.

Sometimes the forms arrive with water behind them.

Sometimes they arrive alone.

---

## PUBLIC TERROR

The citizen fears hunger openly. Thirst is feared in quieter fashion. Hunger riots shout. Water riots listen for pump noise.

An announced ration reduction may fill streets with protest. A rumored cistern shutoff causes families to fill pots, bathtubs, helmets, toolboxes, machine casings, infant cradles, funeral urns, and every illicit tank they can hide from neighbors. The Office studies these behaviors and pretends not to know several of them are sensible.

Among lower stacks the Water Measure Office is called the Dry Hand. Among pump workers it is called the Valve Church. Among nobles it is called intolerable until their ornamental lakes require emergency exemption. Among the Praetoria Civica it is called Tuesday.

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

Water is not soft.

It cuts cities by pressure, privilege, rust, miswording, and thirst. The factor who measures water measures obedience more directly than the magistrate who sentences bodies after disobedience has already begun.

Guard the pumps. Audit the seals. Write plainly when lives depend upon it.

Then expect to be misunderstood anyway.
