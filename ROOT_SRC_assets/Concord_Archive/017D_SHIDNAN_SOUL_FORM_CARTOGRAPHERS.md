# 017D SHIDNAN SOUL-FORM CARTOGRAPHERS
## Restricted Archive Record Concerning the Mapping of the Person Beneath Flesh, the Diagramming of Continuity Traces, and the Dangerous Comfort of Believing That the Soul Leaves Engineering Marks

Filed beneath the parent seal of **017 Undying Lords and Mechanical Continuance**, referenced by Collegia Mortis necrological examiners, disputed by Ferrum Sanctum material doctrinists, watched by Cultus Vitae contradiction-priests, and sealed from popular distribution lest grieving citizens begin tracing their dead on walls.

This record concerns the **Shidnan Soul-Form Cartographers**.

The Shidnan schools are among the strangest of the Undying continuance bodies because they do not begin with the machine. They begin with the outline left by the person. They insist that before one may preserve a lord, one must determine what the lord is when stripped of posture, appetite, vanity, organ habit, reflex, family theater, pain memory, and the lies told by a face.

They call this outline the **soul-form**.

The Ferrum Sanctum permits the term only in quotations.

The Cultus Vitae objects that soul is not a measurement.

The Shidnans answer that nothing becomes unmeasurable merely because priests become nervous.

---

## I. The Shidnan Proposition

The Shidnan doctrine teaches that every enduring person generates a pattern of continuity across flesh, memory, oath, fear, wound, desire, decision, and ash-resistance. This pattern can be mapped indirectly through dream echo, stress response, grief residue, nerve conduction, decision repetition, bloodline recurrence, voice fracture, heat preference, deathbed utterance, and the reactions of animals, children, and certain Liminal sensitives who should not be brought into noble laboratories without separate authorization.

The resulting map is not the soul, they insist.

It is the boundary of the soul’s pressure upon reality.

This is either profound metaphysical caution or a phrase invented to survive Ferrum review.

Possibly both.

---

## II. Instruments and Practices

Shidnan cartographers use machines that appear less like surgical devices than legal instruments built by mourners. Silvered nerve compasses, breath plumb-lines, memory prisms, ash-shadow screens, scar choirs, mirrored pressure wells, oath harmonic readers, and candle arrays soaked in trace distillate are arranged around the subject while the cartographer records deviations in bodily response and environmental silence.

The subject may be living, dying, preserved, recently dead, ceremonially reconstructed, or represented by relics.

The best maps are obtained from those who nearly die but return angry enough to describe the inconvenience.

The worst maps are obtained from ambitious heirs who have practiced sounding profound.

A completed Shidnan map is used to determine which continuance method is least likely to destroy the subject while preserving an excellent imitation. Mechanical Excision may suit one soul-form. Wet-core preservation may suit another. Electro-Hollow echo may be sufficient for a weak political ancestor but inadequate for a true lord. Electroquant tapping may preserve decision cadence while annihilating grief, which some houses consider acceptable and others consider proof of murder.

---

## III. House Politics

Shidnan cartographers are valued because they can tell a noble house that its chosen heir is unsuitable for preservation. They are hated for the same reason.

A negative soul-form ruling may declare that an heir lacks cohesion, oath-density, ash-resistance, ancestral resonance, or sufficient command continuity. Such findings can ruin marriages, trigger duels, redirect organ harvest plans, invalidate succession expectations, and produce sudden theological interest in murdering the cartographer.

A favorable ruling can elevate an obscure cousin, a technical bastard, a vat-sustained child, a disgraced veteran, or a living daughter previously ignored because the house preferred heirs with more ceremonial jawline.

For this reason Shidnan maps are locked, copied, disputed, forged, blessed, stolen, burned, and rediscovered whenever succession becomes expensive.

The Null Court accepts Shidnan evidence only when authenticated by three seals, unless the judge is personally impressed, terrified, bribed, or related.

---

## IV. Relationship With the Cinerum

The Shidnan schools maintain old theories that the ash recognizes incompletion. They argue that some persons resist Cineric dissolution because their soul-form possesses unusual closure, density, or refusal. This claim has not been proven. It has, however, inspired many irresponsible noble experiments involving condemned prisoners, dying continuants, ash exposure, and expensive apologies afterward.

The Collegia Mortis records several cases in which Shidnan instruments predicted post-mortem instability before breach events. The Ferrum Sanctum records several more in which Shidnan instruments produced beautiful diagrams immediately before everyone in the room died.

The value of the school remains disputed.

It is retained because sometimes its warnings are correct.

In Concordial practice, a method that is wrong nine times and correct before catastrophe is not useless. It is merely expensive.

---

## V. Heresies

The most dangerous Shidnan heresy is the doctrine of **transferable outline**: the belief that if a soul-form can be mapped well enough, it can be imposed upon another body or machine. The official schools deny this doctrine. The unofficial schools deny it more loudly.

Ferrum examiners treat any attempt to stamp a mapped soul-form into a hollow, cogitation lattice, vat-grown heir, penal body, or captured Liminal as a near-Heliun offense with additional metaphysical contamination charges.

The Shidnan defense is that a map is not a traveler.

The Ferrum reply is that enough maps eventually become roads.

---

## VI. Closing Instruction

Officers dealing with Shidnan houses must not dismiss them as mystics. Their instruments have preserved useful data. Their maps have prevented failed transformations. Their warnings have saved manufactories from inheriting a lord-shaped absence. They have also justified abductions, inheritance fraud, soul experiments, and murders committed in the name of finding the “true outline” beneath an inconvenient living person.

The Shidnan cartographer is most dangerous when gentle.

A butcher tells the victim he will cut.

A Shidnan tells the victim he will discover what must remain.

Sometimes the victim thanks him.
