**Crown-Beasts** are Bestiae displaying organized symbolic authority. They may serve a dead sovereignty, contain a Conceptual rider, or represent a local apex phenotype that has learned command. They are rare enough to become legends and common enough that legends get people killed.

### Morphology

Crown-Beasts often bear antlers, halos, crowns of bone, banners grown into hide, rank marks carved from living ash, or human-made objects embedded in carbon plate. Eye clusters may arrange like court audiences. Some walk as if processional, forcing even enemies to perceive ceremony.

### Behavior

They do not merely attack. They receive tribute, issue gestures, arrange lesser Bestiae, spare messengers, punish deserters, and mark territory with symbols. Soldiers exposed to Crown-Beasts report impulses to salute, kneel, challenge formally, or await orders. Nobles are especially vulnerable where bloodline symbolism is invoked.

### Countermeasures

Break ceremony immediately. Use irregular fire, crude language, nonstandard formations, and anonymous weapons. Do not duel. Do not issue proclamations. Do not display banners unless countersigned. The Crown-Beast feeds on recognition; insult is often safer than honor.

Custodia Cineris presence is mandatory if the beast appears to understand office, rank, or law.

Field maxim: **Never crown the thing by opposing it beautifully.**
