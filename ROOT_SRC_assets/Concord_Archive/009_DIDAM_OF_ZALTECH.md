## DOCTRINAL NOTICE OF CLASSIFICATION

The following record concerns the hostile faith commonly classified as the Didam of Zaltech.

The Custodia Veritatis uses the designation **Didam** because the word has survived in every captured sect, corrupted confession, battlefield sermon, hunger chant, and marrow-script so far recovered.

The word is unstable.

In seized glossaries it has been rendered as:

- the Mouth
- the Taking
- the Consuming Law
- the Feast That Remembers
- the First Hunger
- the Red Inheritance
- the Opened Gate of Flesh
- the Sacrament of Teeth
- the Doctrine Which Eats Witnesses

No translation is considered complete.

This incompleteness is not a weakness of Concordial scholarship. It is part of the hostile doctrine. The Didam prefers words that remain hungry after being spoken. Every definition leaves an edge unfiled. Every translation preserves a bite.

The name **Zaltech** is even less stable.

Zaltech may have been a war-god. Zaltech may have been an extinct alien philosopher whose species devoured its own dead to preserve civic memory. Zaltech may have been an ancient flesh-engine, a cannibal saint, a dead planet, a predatory Conceptual, a ritual grammar, a famine demon, or merely the title given to the first creature that discovered memory could be stolen through properly prepared meat.

No single sect agrees.

The Red Table names Zaltech as the First Guest and Last Host.

The Marrow Choir names Zaltech as the Bone Beneath All Songs.

The Gut-Saints name Zaltech as the Holy Stomach of the World.

The Butchered Communion names Zaltech as the Promise That Kings Can Bleed.

The Zaltechi Pure insist Zaltech was never a being at all, but the first law older than law: what eats, inherits.

The Ninth Appetite, when captured among noble houses, denies worship entirely and calls its rites hereditary therapeutic consumption, then attempts to bribe the interrogator, then requests a physician, then screams when presented with cooked ration-meat.

Disagreement does not weaken the Didam.

It feeds it.

Every sect claims the others have misunderstood the Feast. Every failure is attributed to impurity. Every survival is proof. Every atrocity becomes precedent if one witness lives long enough to describe it with red around the mouth.

For this reason, the Didam must not be catalogued as a mere cannibal cult.

Cannibalism is a method.

The Didam is a theology of transfer.

Its central claim is simple enough for the starving to understand and deep enough to corrode an empire:

**flesh is not merely meat.**

Flesh is a vessel of strength, fear, memory, lineage, spiritual heat, desire, humiliation, courage, rank, madness, and soul-residue.

To consume an enemy is to take what the world denied you.

To consume the brave is to become brave.

To consume the beloved is to prevent abandonment.

To consume the noble is to repossess stolen time.

To consume the saint is to inherit a fragment of authorized miracle.

To consume the officer is to drink command.

To consume the clerk is to digest the ledger.

To consume the priest is to seize absolution before it can be withheld.

The doctrine is false in most ordinary circumstances.

It is not false in all of them.

That is the root of the danger.

Most rites produce only disease, madness, shock, legal contamination, and the old sorrow of men doing obscene things because hunger has made philosophy from need.

Yet under certain Cineric conditions, with certain victims, during certain psychic weather, near certain breaches, after certain funerary failures, or upon flesh altered by Draught, Resonance, sanctity, battle trauma, or proximity to the Deadward, the Didam’s central proposition may produce measurable effects.

A cultist who consumes the heart of a brave enemy may display temporary courage beyond sanity.

A priest who eats the tongue of a prophet may speak with stolen cadence and be obeyed by those who should know better.

A butcher-saint who drinks spinal fluid from a dying Resonant may briefly hear the Cinerum through another soul’s wound.

A famine mother who eats the ashes of her child may survive the night and thereafter remember a dream the child never told her.

A noble who practices controlled Didamic ingestion may show an ugly strengthening of already illegal Draught patterns.

These cases are rare.

They are also enough.

One working miracle can recruit a district.

One impossible inheritance can turn a prison block into a chapel.

One dead officer’s courage blooming inside the murderer who ate him can make a regiment afraid of its own dead.

The Didam is a religion built around predatory proof.

It does not need to work often.

It needs only to work when someone desperate is watching.

## SUMMARY FOR AUTHORIZED OFFICERS

The Didam of Zaltech arises where hunger, humiliation, corpse proximity, class resentment, Cineric contamination, and visible elite hypocrisy overlap.

It thrives most reliably in:

- lower-spire famine districts
- abandoned hab-stacks
- penal kitchens
- corpse-processing zones
- battlefield stripping grounds
- failed ration halls
- ash reclamation camps
- underfunded hospitals
- debt-prisons
- refugee corridors
- labor gangs assigned to mortuary salvage
- military units exposed to repeated atrocity
- noble service corridors where servants see too much
- black clinics connected to unauthorized Draught refinement

The Didam should be understood as both an external enemy faith and an internal civic symptom.

It is not imported into misery from outside.

It condenses from misery when misery discovers a sacrament shaped like revenge.

Official doctrine describes the Didam as degenerate appetite, and this is true. It is also inadequate. A man does not usually begin by believing that the soul can be seized through the jaw. He begins by believing that no one with a clean hand will feed his children.

He sees noble houses hoard centuries.

He sees Concordia officials convert shortage into arithmetic.

He sees Legio officers requisition the last protein stores for troops marching through his district.

He sees the Cultus Vitae bless hunger as burden and discipline while private chapels pour clear medicine into ancient veins.

He hears that the dead are rendered for the good of all.

He sees that the good of all is delivered upward.

Then a red-mouthed preacher tells him the ruling class already consumes the dead. The only difference is that the rulers use sealed glass, purified channels, and legal permission.

The Didam does not invent this accusation.

It weaponizes it.

The poor notice hypocrisy.

The Didam gives hypocrisy teeth.

## ON THE SOCIAL ORIGIN OF THE HUNGER-REVELATION

The Concordial state prefers causes that can be assigned to agencies.

Famine belongs to the Concordia Logistica.

Riot belongs to the Praetoria Civica.

Heresy belongs to the Custodia Veritatis.

Grief belongs to the Cultus Vitae.

Necrological contamination belongs to the Collegia Mortis.

Battle trauma belongs to the Eternus Legio unless it becomes politically inconvenient, at which point battle trauma belongs to no one.

The Didam belongs to all of them.

Every institution produces some portion of the conditions in which the Feast awakens.

A shortage becomes a sermon when it is explained badly.

A corpse becomes scripture when the family is denied lawful mourning.

A noble procession becomes a recruitment event when it passes over ration dead without slowing.

A battlefield becomes a red chapel when soldiers are left with the bodies of enemies and comrades for too long under Cineric pressure.

A prison becomes a monastery when the guards discover that starvation is cheaper than discipline.

A hospital becomes a cult hatchery when the poor hear that ash-grade material is being extracted below the dispensary while their own wounds are sewn with recycled thread.

The Didam is therefore not an interruption of Concordial order.

It is one of the shapes Concordial order makes when it fails in public.

This is not an absolution.

It is a warning.

The faith spreads because it tells the hungry that their hunger was not weakness but vocation.

It tells the beaten that the mouth is a throne no creditor can repossess.

It tells the nameless that names may be taken from the blood.

It tells the dying that death is only surrender if one leaves the strength inside the body for someone else to claim.

It tells the lower stack what the lower stack has already suspected: that civilization is a meal served above them, and that they have mistaken their own bodies for the table.

The Didam’s genius is not savagery.

Savages can be suppressed.

Its genius is moral inversion.

It takes the accusation made against the poor — that they are base, bodily, driven by appetite, unfit for high stewardship — and makes appetite holy.

It says:

You were called meat because your rulers feared what meat remembers.

You were called hungry because hunger is the first law.

You were told to wait for ration because patience is how the powerful season their victims.

To a full citizen this is madness.

To a starving one, it is almost an argument.

## THE FIRST PRINCIPLE: TO EAT IS TO INHERIT

Captured Didamic doctrine returns again and again to a fourfold sentence:

**To eat is to inherit.**

**To inherit is to become.**

**To become is to rise.**

**To rise is to open the Feast.**

This sentence appears scratched into wall-fat, carved into ration benches, inked on stripped skin, sung in penal kitchens, and bitten into the edges of communion tables assembled from doors stolen out of condemned habs.

The Custodia Veritatis has issued six prohibited-phrase circulars concerning the sentence. Suppression has reduced its open use in middle districts and increased its prestige in lower ones.

The sentence is attractive because it provides a metaphysical ladder requiring no permission.

The Concordia Logistica teaches advancement through service classification, ration reliability, labor proof, debt reduction, family compliance, and decades of recordable utility.

The Cultus Vitae teaches spiritual ascent through endurance, correct mourning, sanctioned labor, reproductive duty, funerary submission, and faith in continuity.

The Eternus Legio teaches honor through obedience unto death.

The Ashbourne teach nothing at all unless one is born close enough to listen.

The Didam teaches that every hierarchy is edible.

This doctrine has obvious insurgent applications.

It also has intimate ones.

A wife may eat a dead husband’s finger to keep his work-hardened resolve.

A child may drink broth made from a gang-lord’s knuckle to stop being afraid.

A prisoner may swallow a guard’s eye and claim he now sees the keys.

A soldier may consume a fallen comrade because grief becomes unbearable if the beloved is simply gone.

A clerk may bite paper soaked in his supervisor’s blood and believe the ledger will answer him differently.

In each case, the Didam turns deprivation into agency.

It is a terrible agency.

It is still agency.

This is why simple denunciation rarely works. The state says the rites are filthy. The convert already knows. Filth is part of the accusation against the world. The state says the rites are dangerous. The convert has spent a life in danger administered by cleaner hands. The state says the rites are illegal. The convert believes legality is the porcelain mask worn by predation when predation has a seal.

Counter-doctrine must therefore address not merely the act, but the grievance into which the act has inserted itself.

Failure to understand this distinction has cost three district prefects, twelve correctional cantors, two Legio detachments, one famine tribunal, and an entire mortuary audit board their lives.

Some of those lives were subsequently redistributed by cultic procedure.

## THE SECOND PRINCIPLE: FLESH REMEMBERS

The Didam’s second major principle is that flesh remembers.

This claim overlaps dangerously with approved Mortis Collegia research.

The Collegia admits, under restricted vocabulary, that human remains may retain emotional imprint, identity residue, trauma structure, familial resonance, and post-vital conceptual adhesion for limited periods after death. The Cultus Vitae embeds this truth in funerary practice. The Concordia Logistica embeds it in corpse-routing protocols. The Legio embeds it in battlefield disposal. The Praetoria embeds it in riot sanitation. None of these institutions publicly teach that a citizen should eat the dead.

The Didam asks why not.

If ash can carry memory, why not marrow?

If grief can attract entities, why not meat?

If a saint’s relic may comfort a district, why may a saint’s heart not strengthen the one who consumes it?

If the Draught may be refined from slain Cineric substance to extend noble life, why is the poor man’s crude sacrament called abomination while the noble’s purified sacrament is called continuity?

The official answer is technical, legal, theological, and very long.

The hungry answer is shorter.

The Didam thrives in that gap.

Captured priests teach that different tissues contain different inheritances.

Heart carries courage, fear, loyalty, and final resolve.

Tongue carries authority, lies, oaths, curses, remembered names, and the right to be heard.

Eyes carry witness, suspicion, shame, and the last image before death.

Hands carry craft, violence, debt, habit, and touch.

Liver carries endurance, poison, appetite, and hidden bitterness.

Blood carries heat, kinship, speed, and battle mood.

Marrow carries ancestry, bone-song, and the deep pattern by which a lineage continues pretending it is one thing across many bodies.

Brain carries memory but is unstable, dangerous, and avoided by sects with any remaining practical discipline unless the intended rite concerns madness, prophecy, or deliberate possession.

Skin carries identity at the threshold where the world once recognized the person.

Fat carries stored ease, status, softness, and, among noble victims, signs of long chemical privilege.

This taxonomy is medically obscene.

It is also operationally useful.

When a Didamic cell steals only tongues, it is preparing for false proclamation, extorted confession, or command-theft rites.

When it steals marrow from many lineages, it is constructing choir-vats or ancestral broth.

When it steals hands, expect craft sabotage, workshop infiltration, or attempts to grant labor skill to untrained cultists.

When it steals noble liver and blood, suspect Draught-directed experimentation.

When it steals eyes from witnesses, prepare for intimidation doctrine: the cult wishes the district to know that seeing is no protection.

When it steals a child entire, there is no stable prediction. Seal the district, summon correctional clergy, search for sympathetic adults, and assume someone believed innocence could be eaten before the world consumed it.

## ON CIRCUMSTANCES UNDER WHICH THE RITES WORK

Most Didamic rites fail.

They fail because the priest is lying.

They fail because the victim carried nothing the eater could use.

They fail because the flesh was prepared incorrectly.

They fail because the eater was too sick, too frightened, too intoxicated, or too full of expectation.

They fail because the Cinerum did not lean close enough.

They fail because the world is still merciful on some days.

Nevertheless, several circumstances increase the likelihood of measurable effect:

First, proximity to Cineric breach activity.

The thinner the boundary between matter and the Cinerum, the more readily emotional and identity residue behaves as if transferable. This does not make transfer safe. A breached district often produces false miracles, parasitic inheritances, misassigned memories, hunger hallucinations, and conceptual infections that mimic power long enough to spread.

Second, consumption of Resonant victims.

Persons with unusual psychic sensitivity, grief-density, saint-contact, Cineric exposure, or hereditary Mortis response may leave stronger residues. Didamic cells actively hunt such persons once identified. Protection protocols must be updated whenever a Resonant appears in lower-stack rumor.

Third, consumption of the recently dead under heightened emotional conditions.

Battlefield death, public execution, betrayal, oath-break, martyrdom, and death during unfinished speech all appear to produce stronger residues. This is consistent with approved funerary doctrine and therefore must never be discussed outside sealed instruction.

Fourth, consumption of flesh altered by Cineric Vitae.

Draught-fed noble bodies, long-lived officers, heavily treated Ashbourne retainers, and certain black-clinic patients may contain stabilized patterns attractive to Didamic experimentation. Cults do not merely hate the Ashbourne. They study them with teeth.

Fifth, ritual consensus.

A lone eater may gain shock, mania, or food poisoning. A gathered circle chanting the same inheritance, naming the same desired quality, and consuming from the same victim may generate a stronger conceptual channel. This is the basis of the Red Table and Butchered Communion threat profiles.

Sixth, repetition.

The first rite may do nothing. The fifth may produce craving. The ninth may produce behavioral alignment. The thirteenth may produce a stable cult identity. Didamic conversion is often less a single belief event than an alimentary apprenticeship.

Seventh, victim recognition.

The eater must know what is being taken. Eating a stranger rarely produces more than pathology unless the stranger is symbolically loaded. Eating the officer who beat the district, the clerk who denied ration appeals, the noble whose carriage crushed a child, the singer whose voice held a mourning house together, or the prisoner who remained brave under torture gives the rite a target. Hatred is inaccurate but powerful.

Eighth, hunger.

The Collegia has repeatedly attempted to separate physical starvation from metaphysical receptivity. The results are classified because they are politically inconvenient. A starving body appears more willing to make a treaty with meanings a fed body would reject.

The Cultus Vitae notes this as evidence that ration stability is doctrinal defense.

The Concordia Logistica notes this as evidence that ration stability should receive funding after military commitments, noble allocations, emergency reserves, administrative continuity, and authorized export obligations.

This ordering remains under review.

## SECTARIAN ARCHITECTURE OF THE DIDAM

The Didam does not possess one church.

It possesses mouths.

Each mouth claims to speak the Feast. Each mouth bites the others when doctrine requires it or food runs low. The absence of central authority makes eradication difficult and infiltration dangerous. A cell destroyed in one district may be denounced as impure by a neighboring cell whose doctrine is nearly identical except for one cooking method, one organ priority, or one translation of Zaltech’s name.

The following sects are confirmed recurring structures rather than temporary gangs.

---

## THE RED TABLE

The Red Table treats every killing as a sacrament and every shared meal as a court of inheritance.

Its cells construct literal tables whenever possible. These may be made from stolen doors, mortuary slabs, stacked crates, stripped pews, overturned ration counters, or the shield panels of dead Legio vehicles. The table is not decoration. It is jurisdiction.

A killing performed away from the table may be necessary.

A killing brought to the table becomes law.

Red Table doctrine divides participants into Host, Guests, Witnesses, Knives, and Remainders.

The Host presides over distribution.

The Guests receive selected portions according to role, grievance, and desired inheritance.

The Witnesses record the victim’s name, title, crime, strength, and final words.

The Knives conduct butchery and are forbidden from swallowing until authorized, a rule frequently violated and punished by having fingers removed for later corrective ingestion.

The Remainders are children, recruits, prisoners, or doubters forced to watch until hunger or fear brings them closer.

The Red Table is especially dangerous in districts where public courts are perceived as corrupt. It offers immediate trial, visible sentence, communal participation, and a meal.

Its justice is monstrous.

It is also legible.

A Concordial tribunal may take months to determine whether a ration-prefect’s negligence caused starvation.

The Red Table can drag the prefect into a room, hear testimony, cut out his tongue, boil his hands, and distribute him before midnight.

This is why the Red Table must be countered not only with force but with visible lawful correction of the abuses it exploits. Suppressing the Table while leaving its chosen victims publicly unpunished teaches the district that the state protects its own meat.

Red Table signs include:

- doors removed from many homes and replaced with hanging cloth
- tables stained but repeatedly scrubbed in spiral patterns
- missing tongues among murdered officials
- witness-marks written in rendered fat
- phrases such as “seat him properly” or “the table remembers”
- communal silence around sudden abundance of meat
- children chanting titles while striking knives against cups

Recommended response: isolate the kitchen network, seize salt stores, identify those who control fire, and protect potential symbolic victims before they become courses.

## THE MARROW CHOIR

The Marrow Choir believes bone contains ancestral resonance.

Its rites are sung, drilled, tapped, scraped, and boiled. Where the Red Table is juridical and immediate, the Choir is patient. It collects long bones from entire families. It hollows them. It strings them. It flutes them. It grinds them into powder. It makes broth in which names are sung until the steam carries genealogies no archive approved.

The Choir’s central claim is that bloodlines are cages built by law, but marrow is the true record. A noble house may possess charters, seals, physicians, heralds, and vaults. The bone beneath the titles still remembers famine, cowardice, incest, theft, betrayal, and every peasant ancestor erased by marriage policy.

This makes the Choir uniquely attractive to dispossessed lineages, bastard branches, orphan gangs, and household servants who know noble genealogies from the inside.

The Choir does not always kill. It robs graves, mausoleums, battlefield ossuaries, disaster pits, hospital amputary bins, and unauthorized family shrines. It prefers bones with names attached but can work with rumor. The Choir will steal a femur from a noble ancestor not to gain strength but to make the house hear itself sung as meat.

Marrow Choir ceremonies have produced documented cases of shared memory hallucination. Participants report seeing ancestral kitchens, forgotten wars, birth rooms, debts, sealed murders, and migration routes through ash storms. Some reports are false. Several have been confirmed against sealed Concordia records.

This has created a quiet panic among certain Ashbourne houses.

A house can protect a living lord.

It is harder to protect every buried compromise in the bone.

The Choir’s music is often low, repetitive, and built around percussion on rib, cup, pipe, and furnace railing. It may pass initially as mourning song. Correctional cantors must therefore learn the difference between authorized grief cadence and marrow-meter.

Signs include:

- opened graves with flesh left behind but bones removed
- thin flutes hidden inside heating ducts
- children taught genealogies not found in civic school registers
- repeated tapping during ration queues
- broth shared after funerals without visible meat
- household servants humming noble names in descending intervals
- powdered bone mixed into candle wax

Recommended response: deploy Cultus Vitae choirs before Praetoria raids where possible. The Choir competes in sound as much as doctrine. A district that can mourn lawfully is less easily recruited into bone-song.

## THE GUT-SAINTS

The Gut-Saints are mutated prophets who claim digestion is revelation.

They are the least structurally consistent and among the most dangerous at close range.

A Gut-Saint may be a single charismatic cannibal with Cineric lesions and a talent for surviving poison. It may be a line of frauds preserving one title through murder. It may be a genuinely altered organism whose digestive tract has become a crude interpretive engine for post-vital residue. It may be all three across time.

Gut-Saints do not merely eat to inherit. They eat to interpret.

They claim the stomach is the first altar because it judges without language. What the mind excuses, the gut rejects. What the law conceals, bile reveals. What priests bless falsely, vomiting exposes.

Captured sermons refer to “the lower oracle,” “the belly that has no etiquette,” and “the warm court below the ribs.”

Their revelations are disgusting, theatrical, and occasionally operationally accurate.

A Gut-Saint may consume a scrap of uniform and identify the soldier’s unit.

A Gut-Saint may eat a dead clerk’s finger and name a hidden ledger.

A Gut-Saint may swallow ash from a breach and predict where a Cineric pressure wave will next stain the wall.

A Gut-Saint may devour a poisoned offering, survive, and declare the name of the assassin while everyone present learns the wrong lesson.

Such successes make them nearly impossible to discredit among followers. Failures are explained as indigestion caused by unbelief, impurity, or insufficient suffering.

Physically, Gut-Saints often show distended abdomens, ritual scars around the navel, tooth replacement with metal or bone, chronic fever, altered saliva, and tolerance for spoiled meat. Advanced cases may exhibit internal Cineric colonization, secondary voices from the abdomen, or impossible hunger cycles unrelated to caloric intake.

Do not allow captured Gut-Saints to fast without medical supervision. Several have turned self-consumption into martyr doctrine. Do not allow them unrestricted access to their own waste. Do not permit correctional clergy to debate them in rooms containing food.

Signs include:

- jars of vomit treated as scripture
- abdominal icons scratched into walls
- followers waiting for a prophet’s belch, retch, or cramp before acting
- rumors of a saint who can “taste lies”
- missing organs from victims but discarded muscle meat
- cult kitchens built near sewer heat and corpse vents

Recommended response: separation from followers, controlled nutrition, anti-emetic restraint if medically approved, and immediate doctrinal correction focused on false revelation rather than gluttony. Calling a Gut-Saint disgusting is useless. He agrees. He has made disgust his throne.

## THE BUTCHERED COMMUNION

The Butchered Communion is the Didam’s most politically explosive form.

It emerges when class resentment, ration collapse, and visible administrative cruelty combine with a preacher capable of explaining murder as civic restitution.

The Communion does not frame itself as mere cult. It frames itself as the lower stack finally conducting the audit no office would perform.

Its victims are rarely random at first.

Debt-lords.

Ration clerks.

Rent collectors.

Private security captains.

Abusive overseers.

Minor nobles passing through districts without proper escort.

Physicians accused of selling poor patients into ash clinics.

Priests accused of blessing hunger while eating from secure stores.

Praetoria informants.

Concordia surveyors carrying maps of future clearance zones.

The Communion kills them, tries them posthumously, distributes selected flesh, and declares the district fed by justice.

This is not metaphor to the believers.

The body of the oppressor becomes public resource.

The crime becomes food.

The food becomes oath.

The oath becomes organization.

The organization becomes insurgency.

The Butchered Communion is thus a bridge between cult and revolt. It can transform ordinary grievance networks into sacramental cells difficult to penetrate because every participant is bound by shared crime, shared meal, and the fear that the state will never distinguish between the starving, the coerced, and the devout.

The Communion is particularly skilled at exploiting official overreaction. If the Praetoria punishes an entire district after one Communion killing, the cult preaches that the state has confirmed collective guilt and therefore collective inheritance. If the state punishes only the killers, the cult preaches martyrdom. If the state does nothing, the cult preaches fear. There is no response without cost.

Best practice requires speed, precision, and visible parallel relief.

The killers must be separated from the hungry.

The victims’ crimes, if real, must be publicly acknowledged.

The district must be fed through channels not controlled by suspected collaborators.

Cult kitchens must be seized, but lawful kitchens must open before sunset.

A district cannot be beaten out of Communion while its children smell meat for the first time in weeks.

Signs include:

- slogans equating eating with audit
- stolen ledgers nailed beside butchered officials
- victims posed at ration counters
- selective distribution of organs to grievance groups
- witness lists burned into table surfaces
- use of phrases such as “pay in flesh,” “render the debt,” and “the office has been consumed”

Recommended response: joint Praetoria-Cultus-Concordia action with Mortis oversight. A purely military response turns the Communion into legend. A purely charitable response gives it time. A purely theological response sounds like etiquette advice delivered during a fire.

## THE ZALTECHI PURE

The Zaltechi Pure reject cooked meat, tools, civilized food, preserved rations, utensils, tables, contracts, rank, and, in their more extreme cells, language itself after initiation.

They argue that every mediation between tooth and flesh is cowardice.

Cooking weakens inheritance.

Knives introduce hierarchy.

Salt imitates law.

Plates imitate class.

Prayer imitates delay.

Words imitate permission.

The Pure are smaller than the Red Table and less politically articulate than the Butchered Communion, but their violence is immediate and spiritually severe. They do not seek to expose hypocrisy. They seek to abolish the distance between hunger and act.

They often appear first as feral packs in collapsed sectors, but this appearance can mislead officers. The Zaltechi Pure maintain sophisticated deprivation discipline. They train recruits to endure disgust, cold, wound pain, and social horror until ordinary human hesitation breaks. They use nakedness, scarification, tooth modification, and refusal of shelter to sever ties with civic identity.

They are not animals.

They are people attempting to prove that becoming animal is more honest than remaining citizen.

Their rites produce fewer documented transferable effects because they reject controlled conditions. However, they are more likely to generate Cineric predation events. The Pure’s refusal of funerary boundaries, combined with intense emotional violence, makes their territories conceptually porous. Bestiae Cineris, corpse echoes, and hunger-shaped Conceptuals have repeatedly followed in their wake.

The Pure also function as shock auxiliaries for more organized Didamic sects. A Red Table cell may secretly direct a Pure pack toward a convoy. The Butchered Communion may use Pure violence to make its own court-killings appear disciplined by comparison. A Gut-Saint may bless a Pure pack, then interpret whatever remains.

Signs include:

- raw bite wounds on corpses without tool marks
- discarded utensils arranged as renunciations
- recruits with filed or broken teeth
- slogans reduced to bite symbols rather than words
- ritual destruction of ration stoves
- refusal to cross lines of salt
- sleeping piles around fresh kills

Recommended response: treat as armed hostile force with contamination risk. Do not attempt negotiation through food offers unless directed by an approved psychological operations officer; unstructured feeding may be interpreted as submission, insult, or invitation.

## THE NINTH APPETITE

The Ninth Appetite is the most politically sensitive Didamic form because it occurs among those who have no need to be hungry.

It is found among nobles, physicians, heir-candidates, private scholars, Ashbourne degenerates, forbidden duel societies, and certain old retainers who have spent too long watching their masters survive by sanctioned consumption.

The Ninth Appetite does not resemble lower-stack cannibalism in presentation. It uses clean rooms, surgical language, inherited silver, private chapels, controlled harvest schedules, coded menus, and physicians who insist that all biological material has been ethically procured from condemned persons, failed clones, enemy cultists, legally dead servants, or non-person stock.

The paperwork is always immaculate.

This is how one knows to be suspicious.

Ninth Appetite doctrine claims that the lower cults are crude because they waste the sacrament through panic. True ingestion must be measured, graded, purified, paired with Draught cycles, and aligned with genealogy. The noble does not eat to survive the week. The noble eats to steer the century.

Its rites focus on selective enhancement:

- courage from decorated soldiers
- fertility from proven mothers
- cunning from rivals
- voice from beloved singers
- memory from old clerks
- obedience from lifelong servants
- resilience from laborers who survived impossible work
- sanctity from unauthorized relic-bearers
- novelty from Resonants and Cineric survivors

The Ninth Appetite is less likely to produce open cult violence and more likely to produce serial disappearances, black-clinic procurement chains, falsified death records, inheritance manipulation, and quiet doctrinal convergence between Draught medicine and Didamic sacrament.

It is also the form most likely to reveal the essential terror of the Ashbourne.

The noble house publicly condemns the flesh-cult because the flesh-cult makes visible what the noble house hides behind law.

Both believe life, strength, and time can be extracted from another being.

The difference is technique, class, and permission.

This resemblance drives Ashbourne hatred of Zaltech beyond ordinary piety. The lower cultist is an enemy. The noble Didamist is a mirror.

Signs include:

- unusually high mortality among servants with desirable qualities
- private kitchens attached to medical suites
- physician-led menu codes in household ledgers
- small sealed freezers guarded more heavily than jewel rooms
- heirs developing mannerisms of recently dead tutors
- “therapeutic ancestry tastings” hidden inside succession rituals
- Draught schedules synchronized with illegal biological ingestion

Recommended response: do not initiate arrest without Null Court warrant or equivalent Continuity authorization. Noble Didam cases are capable of collapsing district politics, exposing Draught irregularities, and triggering private military intervention. Evidence must be copied beyond local jurisdiction before approach. The first officer to find such evidence is rarely the first officer allowed to live with it.

## THE DIDAM AND THE DRAUGHT

The Draught is the Didam’s greatest recruitment tool.

Not because most cultists have seen it.

They have not.

The Draught recruits by rumor.

Among the lower classes, Cineric Vitae is not simply an elixir. It is accusation in liquid form.

They call it noble blood, god-fat, dead honey, saint oil, grave milk, time in a bottle, the long sip, the sacred fluid, and the rich man’s afterbirth. Some names are jokes. Some are curses. Some are prayers that have lost their way.

The official doctrine says the Draught is a tragic tool of continuity. Certain lives must be extended because their service preserves millions. A commander who remembers five centuries of breach war may save districts a younger officer would lose. A physician who has studied Reversion across generations may protect noble and common patients alike. A legal custodian who remembers the first settlement charters may prevent civil collapse. A ruler may be preserved because replacing him would trigger succession slaughter.

This doctrine comforts those receiving doses.

It is less persuasive to those rendered, requisitioned, taxed, conscripted, starved, and buried beneath the machinery that keeps the dose line moving.

The Didam’s argument is direct:

The nobles drink what they kill.

The Concord calls this law.

The poor eat what they defeat.

The Concord calls this heresy.

There is enough falsehood in this statement to condemn it.

There is enough truth in it to make condemnation dangerous.

The Draught and the Didam are not the same. The Draught is refined from Cineric entities, necrological residues, carefully stabilized death-substrates, and restricted procedures. Didamic rites are crude, biological, spiritually volatile, and usually fatal over time. The Draught extends selected lives through controlled theft from the Deadward and from the war economy surrounding it. The Didam attempts direct inheritance through flesh and hunger.

Yet both occupy the same moral wound.

Both ask whether life may be prolonged by consuming what should have remained other.

Both produce hierarchies of permitted and forbidden predation.

Both depend upon the claim that survival justifies horror when the correct mouth receives the result.

The Cultus Vitae condemns unauthorized Didamic ingestion while blessing authorized continuity medicine.

The Custodia Veritatis explains the legal distinction.

The Collegia Mortis explains the technical distinction.

The Ashbourne prefer that no one ask for the moral distinction in a room with poor acoustics.

Didamic cells raid corpse convoys, noble clinics, battlefield recovery teams, and suspected Draught distribution routes not merely to acquire supplies but to prove theological equivalence. A cult that captures a Draught-fed noble corpse gains both material and propaganda. If the flesh produces any abnormal effect, the district will hear of it before the Praetoria arrives.

If the effect fails, the cult will claim the noble was spiritually empty.

Either outcome feeds doctrine.

## ON REVERSION AND DIDAMIC MISINTERPRETATION

The Ashbourne fear Reversion: the sudden return of postponed age, accumulated decay, deferred illness, and all the years held back by Draught dependency.

The Didam interprets Reversion as evidence that stolen time remains edible until it spoils.

This is false.

It is also difficult to disprove in public without explaining too much.

Didamic preachers describe Reversion as the noble body disgorging its hoard. They claim an ancient lord who collapses into ash, lesions, and centuries of debt has not died but opened. They instruct followers to gather the fluids, teeth, hair, blackened marrow, and final breath of reverting nobles before state agents can seal the site.

Several outbreaks began after unauthorized access to Reversion remains.

Most participants died of toxin exposure, necrological shock, infection, or Cineric backlash.

A few did not.

Those few founded sects.

The Ninth Appetite has pursued Reversion material for the opposite reason. Its physicians hope to understand the failure boundary of noble immortality and possibly redirect a collapsing elder’s remaining aeteric pattern into an heir. This is not formally Didamic theology, but the mouth does not care what legal theory surrounds it.

The Butchered Communion uses rumors of Reversion to recruit. It tells the lower stack that nobles are not immortal but constipated with stolen years. It promises that when the people finally open them, time will pour out.

The Red Table has developed rites in which aging officials are seated as “overdue meat.”

The Marrow Choir seeks bones from reverted nobles because it believes the years ring differently in them.

The Zaltechi Pure attempt to eat Reversion remains raw and usually become dead lessons.

Recommended protocol: any noble Reversion event in or near lower-civic space must be treated as a religious containment emergency. Remove remains under visual blackout. Provide alternative explanation within three hours. Deploy Cultus Vitae grief-control before Praetoria rumor suppression. Audit all medical staff for Ninth Appetite exposure. Assume corpse-market bounty has already been posted.

## IMMOG AETER IN DIDAMIC DOCTRINE

The Concord names Immog Aeter as founder, commander, distiller, grave-defier, chainmaker, or necessary monster depending on the mouth speaking and the room in which it speaks.

The Didam names him **the Thief of the Feast**.

This title appears in nearly every major sect.

According to Didamic doctrine, Immog discovered that the dead, the slain, the Cineric, the divine, the monstrous, and the brave could be rendered into continuance. Instead of opening the Feast to all humanity, he stole it, sealed it in vaults, built law around it, fed it to commanders and noble lines, and taught the hungry to call their own mouths unclean.

This version of history is heresy.

It is also a distorted reflection of restricted truths, which makes it more durable than pure invention.

Some captured priests claim Immog was the first Didamist and betrayed Zaltech by choosing glass over teeth.

Others claim he defeated Zaltech and imprisoned the Consuming Law inside the Draught economy.

The Marrow Choir claims Immog’s bones still sing below the first Turris foundation and that the Ashbourne drink to avoid hearing them.

The Butchered Communion claims Immog promised the Feast to the common soldier before the Concordia stole the ration.

The Zaltechi Pure reject Immog as the first coward because he built tools between hunger and flesh.

The Ninth Appetite occasionally reveres Immog privately as the patron of disciplined consumption, which is one of many reasons noble archives require periodic fire.

The Concordial state must avoid strengthening Didamic myth by over-centralizing denial. A public sermon insisting that Immog never consumed forbidden substances invites exactly the question that cannot be answered simply. Better doctrine emphasizes that continuity exists to preserve humanity, while the Didam reduces humanity to appetite and inheritance theft.

This distinction is true.

It is also more fragile than official confidence admits.

## RELATION TO THE CULTUS VITAE

The Cultus Vitae is both the Didam’s enemy and its unwilling supplier of symbols.

The Cult teaches that life is sacred because it must be defended against dissolution.

The Didam replies that life is sacred because it can be taken into the worthy.

The Cult teaches disciplined mourning.

The Didam teaches incorporation of grief through consumption.

The Cult teaches that the dead must be processed correctly to protect the living.

The Didam asks who decided that burning, sealing, rendering, and vaulting are clean while eating is unclean.

The Cult teaches that labor is continuity.

The Didam teaches that laborers are the continuity being eaten.

Correctional clergy report that ordinary denunciations fail when they rely on disgust. The lower stack already lives amid industrial fluids, corpse carts, ash bins, sewer heat, infected wounds, ration mold, and the smell of bodies burned in public furnaces. A doctrine that says “this is disgusting” may be received as class commentary rather than moral instruction.

More effective counter-doctrine emphasizes theft of personhood.

The Didam promises inheritance but usually produces erasure. The consumed dead are not honored. They are broken into properties, reduced to courage, strength, voice, lineage, or heat, and stripped of the mystery by which a person was more than useful qualities. The eater does not preserve the victim. The eater edits the victim into a tool.

This argument works best in districts where lawful funerary practice has remained reliable.

It works poorly where the Cultus has failed to bury children, lost records, blessed abusive officials, or surrendered bodies to suspicious clinics.

The Cult cannot outpreach the Didam while standing beside uncollected dead.

Therefore, the first anti-Didamic sermon is logistical.

Find the bodies.

Name them.

Return them.

Feed the living.

Then speak.

## RELATION TO THE CONCORDIA LOGISTICA

The Concordia Logistica appears, to Didamic eyes, as the priesthood of denial.

It decides who eats without admitting that this is power.

It classifies hunger as shortage, shortage as allocation, allocation as necessity, necessity as doctrine, doctrine as obedience, and obedience as virtue.

The Didam reverses the chain.

Virtue is the courage to eat.

Obedience is domesticated starvation.

Doctrine is appetite wearing a badge.

Necessity is what the strong call their preference.

Allocation is theft arranged in columns.

Shortage is a weapon.

Hunger is truth.

Concordia facilities are frequent targets because they contain ledgers, ration stamps, protein stores, death records, corpse routing schedules, and personnel blamed for impersonal cruelty. A ration office need not be truly corrupt to become a Didamic target. It need only be the visible face of arithmetic during hunger.

The Butchered Communion especially enjoys converting offices into kitchens. Filing cabinets become smoking racks. Counters become butcher blocks. Queue rails become hooks. Ledgers are nailed open beside rendered officials so citizens may see which line item became meat.

This is propaganda theater.

It is also a direct attack on the Concordia’s claim that abstraction can govern bodily need.

Operational guidance: secure ration offices not only with guards but with transparency rituals. Public audit boards, emergency grievance hearings, visible food testing, and rapid correction of abusive clerks reduce Didamic recruitment more effectively than silent fortification. A locked office during famine looks like a pantry refusing confession.

The Concordia dislikes this recommendation because transparency slows distribution.

The Custodia notes that cult occupation slows distribution more.

## RELATION TO THE ETERNUS LEGIO

The Eternus Legio gives the Didam both enemies and converts.

Soldiers are trained to obey, endure, kill, and carry the dead. These virtues protect civilization. Under Cineric pressure, repeated atrocity, supply collapse, and abandonment, the same virtues can be inverted into Didamic practice.

A soldier already understands that bodies decide history.

A soldier already knows that courage may live in meat for a few terrible moments after death because he has watched comrades die with expressions that command the living more strongly than officers do.

A soldier already knows hunger, blood, hierarchy, loyalty, and the practical difficulty of keeping the dead intact during retreat.

This makes the Legio resistant to soft cult language and vulnerable to hard proof.

If a squad consumes the heart of a fallen hero and then holds a breach line beyond expectation, the Didam has acquired a sermon no correctional pamphlet can easily erase.

If a unit trapped behind enemy pressure eats enemy dead to survive, then later hears a priest explain that survival was sacrament, shame becomes recruitment.

If commanders conceal starvation incidents to preserve morale, Didamic chaplains will find the survivors first.

The Legio officially treats cannibalism as battlefield degeneracy, morale collapse, and spiritual contamination unless conducted under no circumstances whatsoever, which remains the legally safest formulation.

Privately, sealed military histories contain survival incidents, vengeance incidents, and controlled atrocity experiments that should never have been approved and therefore officially were not.

Didamic insurgents seek veterans because veterans bring discipline to hunger.

The Red Table recruits former quartermasters.

The Butchered Communion recruits mutineers and abandoned auxiliaries.

The Zaltechi Pure recruits shock survivors.

The Marrow Choir recruits casualty clerks and grave registration staff.

The Gut-Saints recruit medics who have seen too much of the inside of citizens.

The Ninth Appetite hires retired surgeons.

Recommended Legio protocol: after siege starvation, corpse exposure, retreat through breach fields, or noble Reversion battle events, units must receive not only medical care but food abundance under supervision, confession without automatic execution, and funerary closure for consumed or missing dead. Men punished before being heard become silent. Silent men become kitchens in the dark.

## RELATION TO THE PRAETORIA CIVICA

The Praetoria Civica tends to misunderstand the Didam because it first encounters the cult as crime.

Murder.

Corpse theft.

Illegal butchery.

Riot.

Desecration.

Conspiracy.

Trafficking.

Unauthorized assembly.

Assault upon officers.

All correct.

All insufficient.

A Praetoria squad entering a Didamic district is not merely raiding criminals. It is entering an alternative court, pantry, chapel, theater, and family structure built around shared transgression. Every arrest risks pulling on several loyalties at once.

The convert may fear the cult.

The convert may love the cult.

The convert may have eaten with the cult under duress and now believe no lawful life remains possible.

The convert may have consumed a hated official and felt, for the first time, that justice entered the body instead of departing through paperwork.

The convert may be a child who was fed without being told what the stew contained.

The convert may be a witness deliberately given one mouthful so the state would condemn him with the rest.

Praetoria doctrine must therefore distinguish:

- active feast-priests
- recruiters
- butchers
- coercive cooks
- procurement gangs
- doctrinal singers
- corpse-market suppliers
- terrified participants
- famine participants
- child participants
- witnesses contaminated by forced ingestion
- opportunistic criminals using Didamic signs to conceal ordinary murder

Failure to distinguish strengthens cult claims that the state sees the lower stack as one guilty stomach.

At the same time, excessive hesitation allows rites to complete.

Recommended formation: food-control officer, Mortis examiner, correctional cantor, child extraction team, forensic butcher, and rumor clerk embedded with strike command. Standard riot formations are too blunt. A shield line can clear a street. It cannot tell which kitchen is the church.

## RELATION TO THE COLLEGIA MORTIS

The Collegia Mortis is the institution the Didam most wants to rob and most wants to be.

The Collegia studies death as law.

The Didam studies death as meal.

Both understand that the dead are not inert in the Cineric Concord.

Both know that residue matters.

Both know that improper handling produces consequences.

Both have taxonomies of tissue, ash, echo, heat, and transition.

The difference is discipline, consent, authorization, and purpose. The Collegia seeks containment, knowledge, continuity, and controlled utility under state seal. The Didam seeks inheritance through violation.

This distinction is clear inside Collegia halls.

It is less clear to a lower-stack mother whose child’s body disappeared into a sealed Mortis facility and returned as a number.

Didamic cells target Mortis staff for two reasons. First, they possess technical knowledge useful to ritual refinement. Second, they are symbolically potent: the death-scholar eaten by the hunger-priest proves that official necrology can be overthrown by mouth.

The Gut-Saints especially prize Mortis examiners.

The Marrow Choir steals Collegia sample bones.

The Ninth Appetite bribes junior researchers.

The Red Table publicly executes field pathologists accused of stealing the dead from families.

The Zaltechi Pure tend to die attacking sealed laboratories and are therefore sometimes useful as early warning alarms.

The Collegia has proposed controlled misinformation campaigns to poison Didamic tissue taxonomies. The Custodia rejected several drafts because false doctrine about corpse handling tends to become real practice among undertrained officials. In matters of death, even lies may grow teeth.

Recommended protocol: maintain transparent chain-of-remains where security permits; provide family-readable records; do not allow Mortis vocabulary to become so abstract that the bereaved conclude only cultists speak plainly about bodies.

## RELATION TO THE ASHBOURNE

The Ashbourne publicly despise the Didam because it is barbaric, filthy, illegal, riotous, and offensive to all civilized hierarchy.

Privately, they fear it because it understands them.

Not completely.

Not accurately.

But closely enough.

A Didamic preacher can stand in a lower-market with blood on his teeth and explain the noble secret in words the poor understand:

They drink what they kill.

Why should you not eat what you defeat?

A century of Ashbourne public doctrine can be damaged by one sentence if the district is hungry enough.

The noble houses are not Didamic by nature. Their authorized immortality depends on refined Cineric Vitae, legal continuity, medical discipline, guarded supply, and social distance from the materials that sustain them. They are trained to think of themselves not as consumers but as stewards who endure for the common good.

Some believe this sincerely.

Some even make it true.

The Didam does not care.

It points to the old lord’s young hands and asks whose death paid for them.

It points to the guarded clinic and asks why the poor may not enter.

It points to the war beyond the walls and asks why every slain horror becomes another season of noble breath.

It points to household staff who vanish and asks what the kitchen below the private chapel prepares.

The Ashbourne respond with disgust because disgust preserves distance.

They call the Didam animal.

The Didam replies that animals at least admit they eat.

This exchange has destabilized entire service populations inside noble estates. Servants who witness Draught dependence, Reversion symptoms, illegal procurement, or Ninth Appetite rites become high-risk vectors for Didamic recruitment. The danger is not merely that they will rebel. It is that they will reinterpret everything they have seen through the Consuming Law.

A servant who once thought his master fragile may begin to think him ripe.

A guard who once protected a vault may begin to think of the vault as pantry.

A physician who once administered Draught may begin to wonder whether glass is only an aristocratic tooth.

Ashbourne houses therefore spend heavily on silence, rotation, memory correction, private confession, servant marriage control, funerary oversight, and quiet disappearance. These measures reduce leaks and increase resentment. This is the usual efficiency of fear.

Recommended protocol: any noble request for anti-Didamic assistance must be audited for household abuses. Suppressing cult symptoms while preserving the conditions that produced them only teaches the servants that the Feast was correct.

## THE PROMISE OF STOLEN IMMORTALITY

The deeper Didamic cults do not merely promise strength.

They promise a route to deathless existence without noble permission.

Their missionaries whisper across breach dreams, hunger fevers, corpse smoke, famine riots, labor strikes, penal pits, battlefield hospitals, and the private nightmares of men who have watched immortal lords glide above ration lines.

They say:

You were born meat because the weak taught you shame.

They say:

The soul is not freed by death. It is claimed by whoever has the courage to take it.

They say:

The noble drinks from a glass and calls it law. You drink from the wound and call it truth.

They say:

Let us in, and we will show you how to keep what you kill.

That is the bargain.

Service for appetite.

Servitude for strength.

Possession disguised as empowerment.

The promise is usually fraudulent. Most Didamic bodies decay quickly. Chronic cannibal practice produces infection, toxin load, social exposure, mental destabilization, Cineric parasitism, and the simple statistical problem that a cultist who kills symbolically important victims invites symbolically serious retaliation.

Yet the promise does not need to be medically sound.

The Ashbourne have made immortality the central visible injustice of Concordial life. Once centuries can be hoarded, every forbidden path to more years becomes plausible to someone denied even a clean forty.

The Didam tells the poor that immortality was never the property of bloodlines. It was appetite monopolized by law.

This is why confiscated sermons dwell obsessively on noble youth, preserved commanders, ancient physicians, hereditary courts, and Draught rumors. The Feast sells itself as democratized predation.

The cult does not abolish hierarchy.

It offers a more immediate one.

At the top stands the mouth that has eaten most successfully.

## RELATION TO NEZRAM AND THE MOURNING COURT

Where Zaltech offers hunger, Nezram offers revelation.

The distinction must be preserved by all officers, clergy, analysts, and interrogators.

The Didam says: consume, and become strong.

Nezram says: listen, and be remade.

Zaltech spreads through hunger, resentment, gang violence, corpse theft, famine, battlefield trauma, black-market medicine, and the rumor that flesh can carry power.

Nezram spreads through grief, music, dreams, psychic sensitivity, disaster zones, mourning houses, artistic obsession, veterans’ nightmares, and the unbearable suspicion that reality itself is screaming.

Zaltech cultists become butchers, predators, feast-priests, marrow prophets, flesh kings, red-handed revolutionaries, and noble gourmands with locked rooms.

Nezram cultists become singers, listeners, dreamers, scream-oracles, grief mathematicians, ecstatic duelists, and servants of the Mourning Court.

The Didam wants the body opened.

Nezram wants the mind tuned.

The Didam uses teeth.

Nezram uses sound.

The Didam promises inheritance.

Nezram promises meaning.

The Didam treats death as a storehouse.

Nezram treats death as a wound still making noise.

The two faiths sometimes cooperate. A Didamic butcher may provide bodies for a Nezramite chorus. A Nezramite singer may bless a Feast so the victim’s final scream becomes stronger seasoning. Joint cells arise most often after disasters in which hunger and grief cannot be separated.

They also destroy one another.

To the Didam, Nezramites waste edible strength by dissolving into sound.

To Nezramites, Didamists are deaf animals gnawing on instruments instead of hearing the music inside them.

A district showing both bite marks and organized mourning-song requires immediate escalation. Dual contamination means the people have begun to believe both that bodies contain power and that the universe approves their opening.

## RELATION TO THE UNDYING LORDS

The Undying Lords despise the Didam because it is inefficient.

To consume flesh directly is crude. To trust hunger as selection mechanism is barbarous. To inherit through blood and organ is imprecise. To let meaning slosh inside a stomach is intolerable to minds that prefer archive, engine, lattice, and sealed continuity.

The Didam despises the Undying Lords even more.

To Zaltech, the machine immortal is an insult: a sealed meal, a creature that refuses the sacred vulnerability of flesh, a hoarder of strength without blood, a thing that has attempted to make itself inedible and therefore outside the law of the Feast.

Didamic cults especially prize the cracking of casketed lords, armored continuants, reliquary bodies, and machine-preserved aristocrats. They believe that beneath every plate, tube, and engine there remains something that can be eaten, and that proving this restores the first law.

This has strategic implications.

An Undying Lord traveling through a lower district may attract Didamic violence disproportionate even to noble presence. The cult may not expect to survive. It may only need to demonstrate that the metal saint contains meat.

The Marrow Choir seeks preserved bones from machine lords to hear whether memory survives replacement.

The Red Table seats captured augmetic magnates as “hard courses.”

The Zaltechi Pure attempt to bite through metal and die on the lesson.

The Gut-Saints claim mechanical parts induce constipation of the soul and perform grotesque purges after consuming augmented tissue.

The Ninth Appetite is divided. Some noble Didamists consider machine-preserved tissue a delicacy of continuity. Others fear contamination by nonhuman process.

The Undying Lords have responded with anti-ingestion protocols: caustic preservative floods, post-mortem self-incineration, bone denaturation, decoy organs, and legal classification of their remains as hazardous industrial components rather than bodies.

The Didam calls these measures cowardice.

The Custodia calls them helpful.

## THE THREE FALSE IMMORTALITIES AND THE FOURTH SCREAM

The Didam must be understood within the Concord’s broader crisis of immortality.

The Ashbourne seek immortality through stolen life.

The Didam of Zaltech seeks immortality through consumed strength.

The Undying Lords seek immortality through mechanical continuity.

Nezram offers a fourth and worse promise: immortality through surrender to the scream, becoming part of a dead god’s endless dying.

Each path answers the same terror.

The Cinerum waits.

Death burns.

The soul is not safe.

Every faction knows this.

They differ only in what they are willing to become to escape it.

The Cultus Vitae teaches that humanity must endure without surrendering the human meaning of life.

The Concordia teaches that systems must survive even when persons fail.

The Legio teaches that some must die so all are not harvested.

The Ashbourne teach that continuity requires preserved rulers.

The Undying Lords teach that flesh is a design flaw.

Nezram teaches that sanity is deafness.

The Didam teaches that every answer except appetite is a lie told by those already fed.

This is why the Didam cannot be eradicated only by killing its priests. The question it asks remains alive wherever the poor can see the old preserved above them and the dead processed below them.

The question is:

Who is allowed to consume whom?

The Concord’s official answer is law.

The Didam’s answer is hunger.

History has not yet judged which answer frightens the world more.

## DIDAMIC LANGUAGE AND NAMING STRUCTURE

Didamic speech corrupts existing Concordial terminology rather than replacing it entirely. This allows the cult to move through familiar words like infection through plumbing.

**Concordia** becomes the Measured Hunger, the Ledger-Mouth, or the Office That Eats Without Teeth.

**Legio** becomes the Long Knife, the Marching Meat, or the Iron Herd.

**Civitas** becomes the Pen of the Waiting Flesh.

**Turris** becomes the Stacked Table.

**Limes** becomes the Butcher’s Edge.

**Custodia** becomes the Locked Jaw.

**Praetoria** becomes the Club That Guards the Pantry.

**Collegia** becomes the Cold Tongues.

**Domus** becomes the Fat House.

**Vitae** becomes stolen heat.

**Mortis** becomes unopened inheritance.

**Cinerum** becomes the Ashen Stomach, the Dead Pantry, the Feast Beyond Smoke, or the Place Where Meat Remembers Without Skin.

**Aeteric** becomes hoarded years.

**Ashbourne** becomes glass-eaters, vial-mouths, old children, polished cannibals, and those who chew through servants.

**Immog Aeter** becomes the Thief of the Feast, the First Sealer, the Glass-Toothed King, or the One Who Locked the Mouth.

**Draught** becomes noble blood, dead honey, saint oil, grave milk, the long sip, time in a bottle, and the stolen broth.

**Cultus Vitae** becomes the Choir That Blesses the Pantry, though some Didamic cells retain fear of Cantors whose mourning practice is strong.

These corruptions are not mere slang. They are recruitment tools. A citizen who adopts Didamic renaming begins to perceive institutions through appetite. Once the ration office becomes the Ledger-Mouth, burning it feels less like crime and more like dentistry.

Custodia language teams must therefore monitor not only overt cult slogans but metaphor drift in districts under stress. When children begin calling noble elevators “food lifts,” when workers call payroll “meat sorting,” when hospital rumors speak of “kitchens under the beds,” doctrine has already entered the pipes.

## OBSOLETE INFORMAL INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION RESTORED AS INTERNAL SCENARIO INDEX

Older training bundles contained informal scenario language unsuitable for classified civic education. The substance of those examples remains operationally valid and is restored here under proper internal classification.

A noble patron requesting recovery of a stolen medical ampoule may in fact be attempting to conceal a Ninth Appetite procurement chain.

A district riot following rumors of secret ash extraction beneath a hospital may become Didamic if lawful investigators fail to produce bodies, records, and visible accountability quickly enough.

A corpse-processing plant raided by Zaltech cultists may not be searching for food but for bodies believed to have carried Draught, Resonance, sanctity, or official guilt.

A Concordia official falsifying death records may be hiding illegal Draught refinement, Didamic blackmail, noble procurement, or the sale of bodies to feast-priests.

A noble appearing young in public while showing private Reversion symptoms may become a target for Red Table prophecy, Marrow Choir theft, Ninth Appetite inheritance rites, or servant revolt.

A black-market physician offering weak Gray Draught may also be feeding patients trace human material under Didamic assumptions of compatibility.

An apparently simple cannibal murder may be a failed attempt at skill theft, oath transfer, courage acquisition, prophetic tongue seizure, revenge communion, or false-flag intimidation.

A feast-priest captured too easily may be bait intended to contaminate a prison kitchen, interrogation staff, or evidence morgue.

A district suddenly receiving meat from unknown donors during shortage must be treated as a possible recruitment phase. Hunger remembers who fed it.

A Legio veteran preaching survival cannibalism as necessity rather than doctrine may still become the hinge through which doctrine enters a barracks.

A child repeating “to eat is to inherit” may have heard a song, eaten a meal, witnessed a rite, or been marked for future use. Do not assume innocence protects the child from being made symbol.

This index is not exhaustive.

It exists to remind authorized personnel that the Didam rarely announces itself first as religion.

It appears as food, justice, medicine, grief, rumor, revenge, survival, or a joke repeated too often near the dead.

## DIDAMIC MATERIAL CULTURE

The Didam prefers tools that blur domestic and sacramental function.

A kitchen knife is not suspicious until the handle is wrapped in prayer skin.

A stew pot is not suspicious until the stirring pattern matches Red Table witness cadence.

A bone flute is not suspicious until the district stops using metal whistles.

A ledger is not suspicious until debts are crossed out in blood and replaced with cut weights.

A child’s cup is not suspicious until it is passed around a circle after sentencing.

The cult survives by hiding horror inside familiar objects. This makes evidence collection difficult and emotionally corrosive. Officers may find that an entire neighborhood has participated in small acts whose meaning only becomes visible when assembled.

Common objects include:

- table planks with underside names
- knives with notches marking inheritances rather than kills
- hooks made from bent ration rails
- marrow pipes hidden in ventilation shafts
- broth stones used to weight organs during boiling
- salt circles used by moderate sects to distinguish lawful meal from raw inheritance
- teeth kept in matchboxes as proof of participation
- fat candles burned during testimony
- red cloths used to cover portions not yet assigned
- child-sized bowls marked with future names
- prayer strips soaked in blood then dried into bookmarks
- false ration stamps bearing bite marks instead of seals

Material analysis should include smell, heat staining, fat residues, microscopic bone powder, ash composition, and inscription pattern. Do not discard ordinary utensils found at a suspected site. The Didam’s holiest instruments are often those stolen from civic kitchens.

## THE ECONOMY OF THE FEAST

No cult survives on doctrine alone.

The Didam has markets.

Not open markets, except during full district collapse, but networks of procurement, concealment, preparation, rumor, and exchange.

Corpse thieves supply bodies from hospitals, battlefields, morgues, pauper pits, and collapsed habs.

Kitchen cells prepare material under cover of charity feeding, gang feasts, funeral meals, or emergency ration substitution.

Butchers separate symbolic organs from bulk meat.

Bone-workers supply the Marrow Choir.

False physicians identify desirable victims.

Ration clerks redirect hunger toward targeted neighborhoods.

Servants provide noble medical waste, hair, blood cloths, discarded tissue, and sometimes living access.

Children carry messages because adults do not want to search lunch tins too closely.

Corpse-market brokers sell authenticity: this was a brave man, this was a noble cousin, this was a priest, this was a Resonant, this was a murderer, this was a virgin, this was a traitor, this was a man who never broke under torture.

Most such claims are false.

Fraud does not weaken the market. It broadens participation. A cheap fake saint-bone allows a poor convert to feel near the doctrine. Later, when the convert learns he was cheated, he does not abandon the Feast. He seeks purer meat.

The Didam also trades in services:

revenge meals, courage rites, grief incorporation, enemy humiliation, silence binding, gang initiation, false immortality treatments, fertility inheritances, and oath feasts.

Currency may be scrip, food, medicine, protection, names, access, or future body-rights. In some Communion districts, citizens pledge portions of themselves after death in exchange for current protection. These contracts are illegal, unenforceable, and spiritually catastrophic. They are also often more understandable to the poor than Concordia debt forms.

Recommended response: target authenticity brokers. A cult can survive without one preacher. It struggles when no one believes the heart being sold belonged to a hero.

## CHILDREN OF THE DIDAM

Children enter Didamic structures in five primary ways.

First, as dependents fed by cult kitchens before they understand the source.

Second, as witnesses forced to participate so they cannot safely return to civic life.

Third, as orphans adopted by feast-priests and raised with appetite as family law.

Fourth, as symbolic innocents whose consumption, threatened consumption, or protection becomes doctrine.

Fifth, as genuine believers because children learn the moral shape of the world from whoever feeds them consistently.

The Didam’s treatment of children varies by sect.

The Red Table often uses children as Remainders, seated at the edge of rites until they are old enough to receive assigned portions. This creates terror, loyalty, and future witnesses.

The Marrow Choir trains children in bone-song because young voices carry ancestral cadence with unsettling clarity.

The Gut-Saints may treat children as interpreters of appetite, believing untrained disgust is purer revelation.

The Butchered Communion often frames itself as feeding children with the flesh of those who starved them, making child welfare central to propaganda.

The Zaltechi Pure may abandon weak children, exalt feral survivors, or use them as proof that civic nurture is a lie.

The Ninth Appetite hides child involvement behind adoption papers, tutoring contracts, hereditary treatments, and obscene ideas about transferring talent early.

Child extraction must proceed with caution. A child who has eaten under Didamic conditions may fear that rescue means execution. A child who has been fed by cultists may love the feeder more than the state. A child who has been forced to watch may not be able to explain what happened without using cult language.

Correctional doctrine emphasizes replacement meals: immediate lawful food, warmth, names, and non-punitive confession before interrogation. Do not ask first what the child ate. Ask when the child last ate without fear.

## DIDAMIC PROPAGANDA AGAINST THE CONCORD

Captured slogans demonstrate recurring lines of attack.

**The nobles hoard time.**

This slogan links the Didam to Draught resentment and Ashbourne paranoia. It is effective because it is visibly plausible.

**The Concordia hoards names.**

This slogan targets bureaucracy. To be named in a ledger is to receive ration, work, residence, death handling, and legal existence. The Didam promises names can be taken through flesh when records fail.

**The Legio hoards weapons.**

This slogan targets monopoly of organized violence. The Didam answers with the body as weapon, kitchen as armory, hunger as ammunition.

**The Collegia hoard knowledge.**

This slogan targets sealed necrology. The Didam offers crude public experimentation anyone can understand too quickly.

**The Cultus hoards forgiveness.**

This slogan targets priestly gatekeeping. The Didam offers absolution through participation: once all have eaten, all are guilty, and guilt becomes family.

**The Ashbourne hoard years.**

This slogan is the strongest. It requires little explanation.

**Take power into your own mouth.**

This slogan is the core recruitment phrase. It should be suppressed in public signage, but suppression alone may glamorize it. Counter-phrasing should emphasize that the mouth which eats a person becomes the chain by which Zaltech leads it.

**They use cleaner knives.**

This phrase is dangerous because it compresses the entire critique of noble hypocrisy into five words. Do not repeat casually.

**The Feast remembers who refused you bread.**

This phrase often precedes targeted killings of ration officials.

**No one starves at the Red Table.**

This phrase is false, but it kills slowly because a cult meal today may outweigh a civic promise tomorrow.

## INTERROGATION NOTES

Didamic captives are difficult because shame does not reliably function.

Many are proud of the acts others find unspeakable.

Others are terrified but believe they are already beyond pardon.

Some want to confess in detail to spread doctrine through the interrogator’s imagination.

Some lie about rites to make themselves seem powerful.

Some tell the truth because they believe the truth itself will recruit.

General principles:

Do not display disgust as primary response. It rewards the captive and confirms his belief that he has crossed into power.

Do not debate the morality of hunger before establishing the specific chain of acts. Abstract hunger is the cult’s chosen ground.

Do not allow the captive to name victims only by desired qualities, such as courage, sweetness, rank, or heat. Restore proper names whenever known. The Didam reduces persons to harvestable traits; interrogation must reverse this.

Do not accept “we all ate” as sufficient. Determine who killed, who cooked, who coerced, who named portions, who provided access, who watched, who profited, who was forced, and who was fed unknowingly.

Do not let feast-priests control pacing through dramatic silence. Their doctrine is theatrical. Interrupt liturgy with procedural detail.

Offer lawful food during long interrogation. This is not kindness alone. It denies the cult’s frame that all power in the room flows through hunger.

When appropriate, bring correctional clergy trained in personhood restoration, not merely condemnation. A captive may resist being told the rite was evil. He may break when forced to speak the victim’s full life aloud.

Beware inherited mannerisms. Some captives who have consumed specific victims unconsciously imitate voice, posture, gestures, or phrases. Most cases are psychological. A few are not. In all cases, do not allow present family members of the victim to witness without preparation.

## MEDICAL AND NECROLOGICAL HAZARDS

Didamic scenes present overlapping hazards:

ordinary biological infection, prion-like contamination, industrial toxin exposure, rot gases, bloodborne disease, Cineric residue, unresolved grief density, identity echo, ritual suggestion, hunger trauma, and hostile human resistance.

Medical officers should not assume that cultists are immune to what they consume. Many are actively dying and interpret symptoms as transformation. Fever may be called inheritance heat. Tremor may be called courage entering the hand. Vomiting may be called false self leaving. Memory confusion may be called ancestral arrival. Parasitic infection may be called Zaltech’s child.

This reinterpretation complicates treatment. Saving the patient may be perceived as stealing revelation.

Necrological examiners must distinguish between:

- normal post-mortem mutilation
- symbolic organ selection
- emergency starvation cannibalism later ritualized
- Cineric predation disguised as Didamic violence
- Didamic violence disguised as animal attack
- noble procurement disguised as cult activity
- cult staging intended to frame a rival sect
- Reversion scavenging
- Marrow Choir bone theft
- Gut-Saint interpretive consumption

Incorrect classification causes bad doctrine. If a district believes the state falsely blamed a cult to conceal noble procurement, every future denial will be weaker. If a cult successfully disguises itself as ordinary crime, it gains time. If a Cineric entity learns to imitate Didamic signs, the problem has ceased being primarily human and all personnel should regret their career paths privately.

## THE DIDAM AND Cineric ENTITIES

The Didam attracts entities of appetite, inheritance, mimicry, grief, and unfinished violence.

Not all such entities serve Zaltech. Some exploit the cult. Some are created by it. Some merely follow the smell of conceptual blood.

Known manifestations include:

**Mouth-Echoes**, which repeat the last words of victims from drains, pots, and vents until someone answers.

**Inheritance Flies**, ash-winged swarms that gather around contested remains and induce possessive hunger.

**Table Shadows**, flat dark presences beneath communal eating surfaces where illegal rites have occurred repeatedly.

**Bone-Witnesses**, articulated remains that tap out names not recorded in official ledgers.

**The Warm Guest**, a presence reported by Red Table survivors as the sensation of someone beloved sitting beside them during the first forbidden meal.

**False Courage Fevers**, which grant reckless bravery for several hours before leaving the subject hollow, suggestible, and hungry for whatever trait was invoked.

**Marrow Static**, a low vibration in bones recovered from Choir sites, sometimes carrying genealogical fragments or deliberate lies.

**The Unchewed**, victims whose remains resist cutting, rotting, burning, or naming after a failed rite. Their presence causes escalating shame and violence among cultists.

Approved doctrine does not confirm Zaltech as an independent entity.

Approved doctrine does confirm that repeated organized belief in predatory inheritance creates pathways through which predatory things may enter.

Whether the cult found Zaltech or made a mouth large enough for Zaltech to speak remains classified as unknown.

Operationally, the distinction matters less than officers hope.

## CASE RECORD: THE HOLLOW RATION HALL

In the Forty-Second Shortage of Turris Vey, a ration hall in Stack Twelve failed to receive protein allotments for nine consecutive days due to a routing conflict between military emergency requisition and noble household reserve protection.

The Concordia record used the term “temporary distribution variance.”

The district used the term “the week the children stopped crying.”

On the tenth day, Assistant Ration Prefect Merauld Hest was seized by residents after announcing further delay. Hest was not the official responsible for the routing conflict. He was the official present.

A former kitchen supervisor named Salla Vorn convened what witnesses later identified as a Red Table proceeding. Hest’s ledger was read aloud. Citizens testified regarding denied appeals, spoiled flour, missing infant supplements, and the visible passage of sealed noble food lifts through the district spine.

Hest attempted to explain allocation hierarchy.

His tongue was removed as “the part that had learned numbers instead of hunger.”

His hands were boiled and distributed to those whose ration cards he had stamped invalid.

His heart was reserved for Vorn, who claimed courage enough to feed the hall without permission.

Within three days the hall became a Communion site. Within six days three additional officials were dead. Within eight days Praetoria intervention killed forty-one residents, including fifteen who had eaten unknowingly from the first stew.

The district remained unstable for eleven years.

Post-action review concluded that a visible correction of the ration failure within the first forty-eight hours would likely have prevented Didamic consolidation.

The review was sealed because the responsible noble reserve order remained politically protected.

The Didam’s surviving slogan from the incident remains prohibited:

The office had hands. We made them useful.

## CASE RECORD: THE SINGER'S TONGUE

In a mourning house attached to the East Ash Furnace, Cantor-Associate Heliane Sest was beloved for the steadiness of her funeral voice. During a breach winter, she conducted three hundred and seventeen child rites in twenty-nine days. Her voice became, to the district, proof that grief could pass through a human body and emerge disciplined.

A Marrow Choir cell, infiltrated by a Gut-Saint, abducted her after evening liturgy.

They did not kill her immediately.

They demanded she speak the names of all children she had sung to ash. She refused. They removed her tongue and divided it among five choir leaders, each believing voice could be inherited through the organ that had carried sanctioned grief.

For several nights afterward, illegal mourning songs spread through the district in a cadence resembling Sest’s voice. Witnesses reported hearing her tone from mouths that had never trained under the Cultus Vitae.

The Custodia initially classified the incident as mimicry and grief hysteria.

Mortis review found trace Cineric resonance attached to the consumed tissue.

The five choir leaders were later captured. Two could reproduce Sest’s funeral cadence with impossible precision. One wept continuously and begged to have the names removed from his mouth. One had bitten off his own tongue after claiming she was still using it. One escaped by convincing a guard that the guard’s dead sister was singing from inside the wall.

This case remains required study for correctional cantors assigned to Didamic districts.

The lesson is not that tongues contain souls.

The lesson is that grief will use any available instrument if lawful instruments are broken.

## CASE RECORD: THE GLASS-EATER SALON

The Ninth Appetite was confirmed in House Morvain’s eastern cadet branch after a servant delivered frozen tissue ledgers to a Null Court courier, believing them to be evidence of ordinary murder.

They were worse than ordinary.

The cadet branch had conducted “virtue tastings” for thirty-two years. Guests consumed prepared portions of selected servants, tutors, duelists, musicians, debtors, and political rivals under the supervision of a physician trained in unauthorized Mortis tissue preservation. The events were recorded as private dinners, medical retreats, or inheritance meditations.

House language avoided Didamic terms. Participants spoke of refinement, continuity alignment, elective incorporation, excellence transfer, and hereditary palate calibration.

One heir had consumed portions of four rival claimants before succession vote.

One matriarch believed she had preserved the loyalty of a dead bodyguard by consuming his heart annually in preserved slivers.

One physician had attempted to synchronize Draught dosing with neural tissue ingestion from a condemned prodigy.

When confronted with the term Didam, several participants expressed genuine offense. They considered themselves above hunger.

The Null Court judgment noted that contempt for the lower form of a crime does not absolve the higher form.

The public record states that the cadet branch died in a furnace accident.

The private record states that their remains were denatured beyond ceremonial recovery because too many parties wanted to taste what they had become.

## CASE RECORD: THE BONE PIPE REVOLT

During reconstruction of a sewer-adjacent maintenance corridor beneath Turris Halvek, workers discovered that hundreds of missing pauper bones had been fitted into ventilation channels, drainage knocks, and steam-echo cavities. When pressure shifted, the corridor sang.

At first the sound was dismissed as pipe strain.

Then names emerged.

Not clearly. Not loudly. Enough.

The names belonged to laborers recorded as transferred, not dead. Their families had never received Procession of Ash documentation. The Marrow Choir had acquired their remains through a mortuary subcontractor, hollowed selected bones, and built an instrument large enough that normal infrastructure became choir.

The song spread through adjacent blocks. Workers refused shifts. Families gathered to hear whether their dead were present. The Concordia attempted to seal the corridor as structural hazard. This nearly caused revolt.

A correctional cantor broke protocol by reading every recovered name aloud in the public square before the Cultus had confirmed identities. The Custodia reprimanded her. The district stabilized.

The mortuary subcontractor was eaten before arrest by a Communion cell that formed during the investigation.

The incident demonstrates the Didam’s ability to exploit record failure. The Choir did not merely steal bones. It returned names through criminal sound when the state had failed to return them through lawful paperwork.

This is why missing-person ledgers are anti-cult infrastructure.

## COUNTER-DOCTRINE: THE PERSON IS NOT A PORTION

The authorized correctional formulation against Didamic inheritance is:

**The person is not a portion.**

This sentence should be used carefully, repeatedly, and with material support.

The Didam divides the person into harvestable traits. Courage. Voice. Beauty. Rank. Fertility. Skill. Memory. Years. Heat. Obedience. The cult then claims that consuming the trait honors the whole.

This is false.

A person is not courage alone.

A person is not voice alone.

A person is not a heart, hand, eye, tongue, marrow, or name rendered into broth.

To eat a brave man does not preserve his bravery. It steals the story from the one who lived it and gives the thief a fever.

To eat a singer does not preserve her song. It turns mourning into burglary.

To eat an oppressor does not undo oppression. It may satisfy revenge, but it also teaches the district that power is proven by who may be reduced to meat.

This last point must be handled with care. Many victims of Didamic revenge are themselves guilty of cruelty. The state damages its own doctrine when it pretends otherwise. Correctional clergy should not sanctify abusive officials to condemn their consumption. They should condemn both the abuse and the Feast.

The state must say:

He wronged you.

He should have faced lawful judgment.

Eating him made his wrongness your altar.

The person is not a portion.

This doctrine has shown measurable success when accompanied by food relief, body return, and prosecution of genuine official crimes.

Without those measures, the sentence sounds like etiquette from a full stomach.

## COUNTER-OPERATIONS: FEED, NAME, SEPARATE, BURN CLEANLY

Anti-Didamic response is summarized in four imperatives.

**Feed.**

Hunger is not the sole cause of the Didam, but hunger is the easiest door for it to open. Emergency food distribution must be visible, lawful, and not controlled by suspected abusers. Protein matters symbolically as well as nutritionally. A district surviving on starch paste while cultists provide meat is a district already halfway recruited.

**Name.**

The Didam thrives where people become categories: deficient citizens, unclaimed bodies, shortage variance, labor surplus, unidentified dead, condemned material. Restoring names restores personhood against portioning. Public rolls, funerary postings, witness correction, and family notification are not administrative luxuries. They are spiritual barricades.

**Separate.**

Separate priest from participant, cook from child, butcher from coerced eater, supplier from starving neighbor, noble procurer from ordinary servant, rumor from verified rite. Collective punishment confirms cult doctrine. Indiscriminate mercy permits cult survival. Separation is difficult, expensive, and morally tiring. This is why civilization employs officers instead of slogans.

**Burn cleanly.**

Contaminated remains must be processed with technical and ritual precision. Hidden disposal feeds rumor. Public spectacle feeds fascination. Clean burning requires witnesses trusted by the district, Cultus liturgy, Mortis control, and Concordia records that do not vanish. The dead must not be left for cult recovery, but neither may they be erased into administrative smoke.

Failure in any one imperative weakens the other three.

A fed district without names remains vulnerable to grief cults.

A named district without food remains vulnerable to hunger cults.

A separated district without clean funerary handling remains vulnerable to corpse rumor.

A burned district without justice remains vulnerable to the next mouth that promises memory.

## FIELD SIGNS BY DISTRICT TYPE

In famine districts, watch for sudden meat, red jokes, children with greasy mouths during shortage, missing pets, vanished debt collectors, and ration halls that smell of broth after closure.

In corpse-processing zones, watch for misrouted remains, workers carrying covered lunch tins into restricted areas, marrow dust near drains, false incineration weights, and prayer strips tied to hooks.

In noble service corridors, watch for servants sharing rumors of medicine rooms, private kitchens active after midnight, tissue waste not matching medical logs, household songs using lower-stack Didamic metaphors, and unusual fear around certain family dinners.

In military barracks, watch for survival jokes becoming oaths, heart-eating rumors after heroic death, enemy trophies kept in salt, cooks gaining influence beyond rank, and soldiers refusing ordinary meat but accepting “honor portions.”

In prisons, watch for missing weak inmates, guards selling kitchen access, gangs using bite scars as rank, chaplains excluded from meals, and prisoners referring to execution dates as harvest.

In hospitals, watch for poor patients transferred without records, tissue samples requested by unauthorized physicians, rumors of noble medicine, morgue staff with unexplained wealth, and families denied viewing under vague contamination claims.

In schools, watch for children playing Red Table, chanting inheritance formulas, drawing towers as stacked plates, biting names into bread, or calling teachers “tongues.”

In markets, watch for sellers claiming meat carries qualities: brave, clean, noble, old, fertile, clever, obedient, sainted. Most such sellers are frauds. Fraud still recruits.

In churches, watch for unauthorized communion metaphors, excessive focus on incorporation of the dead, funeral meals not supervised by clergy, and cantors whose correctional sermons are answered by laughter rather than anger. Laughter means the cult has made the official words sound naive.

## ON FRAUD, FAILURE, AND WHY THEY DO NOT SAVE US

Many Didamic miracles are fake.

Many priests are opportunists.

Many sacred meats are misidentified.

Many courage rites produce only intoxication.

Many inherited voices are mimicry.

Many bone songs are staged with hidden pipes.

Many noble body parts sold in corpse markets are pig, vat-grown scrap, or the unfortunate remains of someone poor enough that no buyer cared to verify.

These facts should comfort no one.

Fraud is not external to the Didam.

Fraud is how marginal believers buy proximity to atrocity before committing fully. Fraud spreads vocabulary, ritual expectation, procurement channels, and appetite. Fraud allows citizens to rehearse belief at lower cost. Fraud teaches them which symbols matter. Fraud makes the first real rite feel like fulfillment rather than rupture.

Moreover, failed rites strengthen doctrine when interpreted correctly by priests.

The victim was impure.

The eater lacked hunger.

The knife was cowardly.

The table was not witnessed.

The Concord poisoned the flesh.

Zaltech withheld inheritance to test devotion.

The noble had no soul to steal.

The dead resisted because their name was not spoken.

Every failure is a door to another rite.

Rational exposure helps only when paired with alternative meaning. Showing a starving district that its purchased saint-bone was false may discredit one seller. It may also create rage at having been denied even authentic blasphemy.

The correct response is not mockery.

It is replacement: lawful food, lawful mourning, lawful justice, lawful truth, and enough visible consequence for predatory officials that the Red Table does not appear to be the only court that ever reaches the guilty.

## SEALED NOTE ON POSSIBLE ORIGIN OF ZALTECH

No approved doctrine identifies Zaltech with certainty.

Several hypotheses remain under restricted study.

**The War-God Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech was a martial Conceptual formed from the repeated consumption of enemies across pre-Concordian wars. This explains courage transfer phenomena but not marrow-song complexity.

**The Extinct Philosopher Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech originated in an alien culture that preserved civic continuity through ritual anthropophagy or xenophagy, later entering the Cinerum as a predatory idea. This explains translation instability and doctrinal sophistication.

**The Flesh-Engine Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech was a biological machine designed to extract memory and skill from bodies, worshipped by survivors after its collapse. This explains tissue taxonomy but risks flattering cult technical claims.

**The Cannibal Saint Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech was once human or near-human: a famine survivor whose impossible inheritance became myth under Cineric pressure. This hypothesis is popular among correctional clergy because it makes the cult a human sin rather than cosmic law.

**The Dead Planet Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech refers to an entire consumed world whose biosphere entered the Cinerum as appetite. This explains scale, recurrence, and the cult’s indifference to individual identity.

**The Predation Conceptual Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech is not a being but the name given when predation becomes self-aware through repeated ritual meaning. This is the most flexible and therefore the least comforting.

**The Concordial Shadow Hypothesis** holds that Zaltech condensed in response to the Draught economy itself: a metaphysical counter-argument formed by centuries of authorized consumption, corpse rendering, noble extension, and public denial. This hypothesis is politically toxic and has been sealed repeatedly under different names.

The Custodia Veritatis takes no position in general circulation.

Operationally, all hypotheses require the same immediate behavior:

Do not feed it.

## FINAL CONTINUITY ASSESSMENT

The Didam of Zaltech is not the greatest military threat facing the Cineric Concord.

It does not command the largest armies.

It does not possess the oldest vaults.

It does not hold the highest offices.

It does not build the engines, chart the Cinerum, regulate the Draught, maintain the spires, or command the visible war.

It simply asks a question every hungry citizen can understand.

If power may live in what is consumed, why are only the high permitted sacred appetite?

The official answer is that the Concord does not consume for pleasure, revenge, or personal ascent. It renders death into continuity under law because without continuity the living would fall to the Deadward, the Cinerum, the Bestiae, the old gods, the false immortalities, and the ash beyond every wall.

This answer is true enough to preserve civilization.

It is not clean enough to silence the Feast.

The Didam grows in the stain between necessity and hypocrisy.

It grows where ration lines fail beneath immortal balconies.

It grows where the dead disappear into systems that return neither body nor name.

It grows where abusive officials are protected because their signatures remain useful.

It grows where noble medicine is glimpsed by servants whose children die of ordinary fever.

It grows where soldiers survive by acts no priest helps them name.

It grows where hunger becomes the only honest sensation left.

Against such a faith, the Concord must use force.

It must also use food.

It must use doctrine.

It must also use justice.

It must burn contaminated remains.

It must also return names.

It must expose false saints.

It must also admit true wounds.

It must kill feast-priests.

It must also ask why the district believed them before the first body was opened.

The Didam teaches that civilization is a lie because all things eat.

The Concord teaches that civilization is the discipline by which eating does not become the only law.

The difference between those claims is the width of a table.

Guard it.
