# 002C — THE BLACK VATS OF KHEPRION-VI

**Cineric Concord Central Mortis Archive**  
**Record Family:** Expanded Incident Dossier / Distillate Memory Reef Contamination  
**Seal:** Ash-Black / Collegia Mortis / Noble Medical Contracts / Custodia Veritatis Suppression Writ  
**Circulation:** Mortis distillers, noble medical factors, battlefield restorative inspectors, Custodia evidence burners, and Ferrum containment engineers assigned to chemical-memory hazards.  
**Penalty for unauthorized recitation:** formula erasure, sensory cauterization, or rendering into blind witness stock.

This record is not maintained for comfort. It is maintained because the dead remember poorly, the living remember conveniently, and the Concord survives only when enough officers know which kind of memory is trying to enter the room.
## I. PURPOSE OF THE WORKS

Kheprion-VI housed sanctioned distillate stabilization vats under Collegia Mortis license and noble funding. Its official production included battlefield restorative gels, ash-suppressive reagents, rejuvenant intermediates, and medical amplifiers intended to stretch fractional traces of distillate into useful treatment without feeding noble hunger directly.

Its unofficial production has never been fully established. Some ledgers dissolved into black fluid. Others wrote new inventories over themselves while locked in evidence vaults. The surviving investigators agreed only that the works did not begin as a cult site. They began as industry with a tolerated appetite.

## II. THE USEFUL ANOMALY

The first suspect batches improved memory. Wounded soldiers treated with low-index restorative gel recalled battlefield details with extraordinary precision. They remembered enemy positions hidden from sight, shell trajectories heard only in fragments, the last words of comrades drowned by bombardment, and the location of ammunition caches buried under rubble. Mortis procurement saw a potential revolution in after-action intelligence. Testing expanded.

The gel did not improve memory. It installed proximity to a memory reef in the Ash Sea.

At first the reef supplied plausible recollection. Then it supplied nearby casualty memory. Then enemy death memory. Then nonhuman strata. Treated soldiers recalled wars fought by extinct species, architectural plans from civilizations never indexed, and the final weather of planets that had no surviving names. Surgeons recited repair manuals for machines the Ferrum had never built. A medic correctly named every patient who would die in the ward over the next forty minutes before drowning in ash with dry lungs.

## III. THE VATS REMEMBER THEIR CONTENTS

The catastrophe became irreversible when the vats began remembering contents before processing. Batches emerged labeled with casualties not yet delivered. Workers recognized their own future residue signatures in lots scheduled for the following week. Several vats demanded ingredients by name. One vat produced a perfect restorative gel compounded from deaths that had not yet occurred and screamed when denied them.

Mortis theory holds that rendered Cineric material, stabilized for healing, can be tuned toward remembering when exposed to repeated trauma inquiry. The treatment question—what happened to this body—became a summons. Enough questions asked through distillate became a door.

## IV. MORTIS-ON-MORTIS VIOLENCE

Containment failed first because the Collegia argued with itself. Senior examiners ordered destruction of notes. Junior examiners refused, claiming the knowledge could restore lost histories and improve battlefield survival. Noble sponsors demanded retrieval of proprietary samples. Custodia Veritatis declared all three parties contaminated by desire. The first executions were not of vat workers but of scholars trying to save notebooks.

The Custodia destroyed preservationists and thieves alike. Ferrum teams dismantled stabilizers under gunpoint. Legio units burned warehouses whose contents whispered casualty lists. The lower works were flooded with caustic ash binders, burned, buried in glass, and then declared absent from public maps.

## V. THE SURVIVING BLACK DROPS

No official sample remains. This statement is repeated in every sealed register and believed by no competent examiner. Noble houses still trade rumors of black restorative ampules capable of returning lost memory, seeing through the dead, or retrieving a loved one’s final thought. Mortis agents periodically intercept counterfeit Kheprion lots, most harmless, some fatal, some worse than genuine.

The true danger is not that Kheprion’s formula survived. The true danger is that the question survived: if memory can be healed by touching the Ash Sea, who decides which memories are worth the wound?

## VI. DOCTRINAL CONCLUSION

Kheprion-VI teaches that medicine may become interrogation and interrogation may become invitation. A wound wants closure. A soldier wants to remember. A physician wants to know. The Ash Sea answers generously until the answer learns to hunger.
