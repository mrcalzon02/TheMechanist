# 003F MORTIS AUXILIA
## Concordial Military Archive Record Concerning Penal Auxiliaries, Corpse-Handling Formations, Contaminated Labor Bodies, Ash-Burial Gangs, Furnace Lines, Dead-Carriers, and Those Whose Punishment Is to Make Survival Possible After Battle

Filed beneath the parent seal of **003 Eternus Legio and Military**, corpse-crossed by **011 Collegia Mortis**, ration-noted by the Concordia Logistica, discipline-marked by the Praetoria Civica, and restricted from sentimental civic circulation.

This record concerns the **Mortis Auxilia**.

They are not honored.

They are necessary.

The distinction is one of the foundations of civilization.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Mortis Auxilia are penal, corpse-handling, contaminated labor, battlefield clearance, furnace-support, quarantine-digging, corpse-tagging, plague-hauling, and ash-exposure auxiliary units operating under Legio authority and Collegia Mortis technical oversight.

They move the dead.

They burn the dead.

They separate the useful dead from the dangerous dead, the dangerous dead from the speaking dead, the speaking dead from the legally dead, and the legally dead from those still inconveniently alive inside corpse piles.

They perform the labor that follows violence: trenching, body retrieval, mass cremation, contaminated rubble clearing, latrine burning, corpse-road construction, plague pit sealing, battlefield salvage, bone sorting, armor stripping, ash-bag hauling, and the miserable work of ensuring that victory does not rot into a second enemy.

If the Linea hold the wall, the Mortis Auxilia clean what the wall cost.

---

## CIVIC DISCOMFORT

Citizens prefer not to think about the Mortis Auxilia until smell forces philosophy upon them.

Armies produce corpses. Spires produce corpses. Famines produce corpses. Riots produce corpses. Plagues produce corpses. Noble punishments produce corpses. Cults produce corpses with enthusiasm and poor labeling. The Cinerum produce corpses that may object to the term.

A society that cannot process its dead becomes food, infection, haunting, legal dispute, inheritance fraud, grief cult, hunger rite, or road blockage.

The Mortis Auxilia prevent this.

They do so with hooks, carts, masks, furnace tongs, tags, shovels, acid, lime, ropes, hymns they are not supposed to enjoy, and a humor so black even the Collegia Mortis occasionally asks them to lower their voices.

---

## PENAL ORIGIN

The Mortis Auxilia began as punishment detail. Early Legio commands assigned deserters, thieves, camp followers, condemned prisoners, captured rebels, failed ration officials, and cowards to corpse trenches because the work was disgusting, dangerous, and immediately necessary.

The practice endured because it worked.

It was formalized after the Siege of Gant Ashfall, when unprocessed dead behind the victorious Legio line produced plague, revenant confusion, false casualty registers, inheritance collapse, and a Nezram mourning event that killed more soldiers after the battle than during it. The Collegia Mortis petitioned for a permanent labor arm trained in corpse discipline. The Legio petitioned for punishment labor that would save soldiers from wasting time with shovels. The Null Court combined both petitions into one order and, by clerical accident, sentenced an entire minor tax college to the first formal Mortis Auxilia. Their descendants still claim seniority.

---

## COMPOSITION

The Mortis Auxilia include penal companies, bonded corpse clans, contaminated labor drafts, plague survivors, debt-ash workers, condemned soldiers, battlefield scavengers under sentence, hereditary furnace families, volunteer sin-eaters, and certain citizens whose crimes are too useful to waste on execution.

Not all Mortis Auxilia are criminals.

This is important.

Some are born into the work because their families have performed it for centuries and no one else knows how to identify a corpse that is lying. Some volunteer because the dead took their district and they prefer useful proximity to helpless mourning. Some are assigned after surviving contamination that makes ordinary civic return impossible. Some are Legio soldiers punished for offenses not grave enough for death but too grave for trust.

All wear the ash-grey mark.

All carry hooks.

All learn to count bodies twice.

---

## ORGANIZATION

The smallest Mortis Auxilia body is the **hook file**, used for body retrieval, trench clearing, furnace feeding, and contaminated debris movement.

A **tag gang** identifies, labels, separates, and records corpses. Tag gangs are trained in recognizing noble marks, Legio implants, cult mutilations, Cineric alteration, Draught exposure, plague swelling, false death, and the peculiar stillness of a corpse waiting for someone to lean close.

A **furnace line** operates crematoria, mobile burners, ash pits, and emergency thermal reduction engines. Furnace lines are prized and feared because they can prevent plague or destroy evidence depending on who holds the order.

A **black labor cohort** performs contaminated engineering: trenching through corpse fields, sealing plague drains, clearing collapsed hab-blocks, building burn roads, and restoring access to infrastructure no clean worker will approach.

A **Mortis command gang** coordinates large clearances under Collegia oversight. Its officers are often old, scarred, and impossible to impress.

---

## TRAINING

Mortis Auxilia training begins with smell.

The recruit is made to identify age of death, heat exposure, plague risk, ash contact, chemical burn, hunger cult preparation, and improper stillness by smell before instruments are introduced. The lesson is not cruelty. Instruments fail. Smell rarely lies, though it may wish it could.

Training includes hook use, corpse leverage, mask discipline, furnace procedure, plague spacing, body stacking, grave math, ash disposal, rapid tagging, field amputation, contaminated salvage, denial of mourning interference, and the legal distinction between remains, person, evidence, hazard, witness, and material.

The hardest lesson is not vomiting.

The hardest lesson is not apologizing to every corpse.

Apology slows the line. The dead do not require apology from the worker. They require correct handling from the living.

---

## RELATION TO THE COLLEGIA MORTIS

The Collegia Mortis claims technical authority over the dead.

The Mortis Auxilia carry the dead to where technical authority can reach them.

This relationship is intimate, unequal, resentful, and indispensable. Mortis scholars may speak of necrological classifications, revenant indices, ash morphology, and posthumous legal states. Auxilia workers speak of heavy ones, leakers, singers, grabbers, warmers, liars, and don't-touch-those.

Both vocabularies save lives.

A Collegia examiner who ignores Auxilia slang is a fool.

An Auxilia worker who ignores Collegia seal marks is usually dead.

---

## CONTAMINATION DUTY

The Mortis Auxilia are assigned where contamination risk makes ordinary labor impossible: plague districts, ash-breached trenches, corpse-flooded lower spires, battlefields under heat, Cineric residue zones, Nezram mourning sites, Didam feast halls, failed hospitals, burned noble estates, dead manufactoria, and places where the floor moves for reasons not listed on architectural plans.

They are issued masks, hooks, layered coats, seal gloves, bone saws, lime, black wax, furnace tickets, chalk marks, and permission to refuse unsafe orders once. The second refusal is reviewed. The third refusal is often irrelevant because the worker is either correct or dead.

Contamination duty produces strange loyalties. A condemned thief and a hereditary corpse-worker may trust one another more deeply after a week in a plague trench than siblings who have shared forty years of clean air.

The Legio exploits this.

So does the dead.

---

## PENAL DISCIPLINE AND REDEMPTION

Mortis Auxilia service may reduce sentence, commute execution, erase debt, restore limited citizenship, or provide posthumous civic credit to a worker's family. The terms are complex, harsh, and frequently altered by local need.

A penal worker who completes ten furnace cycles without infraction may receive improved rations.

A contaminated worker who survives a full quarantine term may receive legal cleansing if the Collegia agrees the word cleansing is medically, spiritually, and administratively defensible.

A condemned soldier who retrieves the bodies of those he abandoned may be restored to Linea service or assigned permanently to hooks, depending on whether the retrieved bodies forgive him. This last clause is not metaphorical in Mortis jurisdictions.

The Mortis Auxilia do not promise redemption.

They offer work valuable enough that redemption may become bureaucratically convenient.

---

## PUBLIC REPUTATION

Citizens fear the Mortis Auxilia because they arrive after screaming stops.

They are associated with plague bells, corpse carts, furnace smoke, quarantine chalk, hooks at doors, family bodies taken before mourning, and the dreadful efficiency of workers who no longer flinch from ruin.

Children dare one another to touch Auxilia hooks.

Adults pretend not to see Auxilia workers in markets.

Nobles employ perfumes when Auxilia processions pass.

Veterans buy them drinks.

This is among the few reliable methods for distinguishing veterans from decorated fools.

---

## CASES PRESERVED FOR INSTRUCTION

At **Morrow Gate**, a Mortis Auxilia tag gang discovered forty-two living children beneath a corpse ramp because one worker noticed the pile was breathing in wrong sequence. The Linea commander credited discipline. The worker credited laziness, claiming he did not want to move the same bodies twice.

During the **Seventh Grey Plague**, black labor cohorts dug counter-trenches through infected burial pits to reach a sealed water valve. Six hundred workers died. Three million citizens received clean water. The Concord awarded the cohort a posthumous ash-banner and charged surviving families for replacement hooks. The charge was later waived after someone with shame read the ledger.

At **House Varn's Feast Hall**, Mortis Auxilia workers refused to burn the bodies immediately despite noble command, identifying Didam preparation marks under ceremonial clothing. Their refusal enabled Custodia Cineris action that prevented a lineage hunger compact from spreading into three districts. The noble steward who ordered the burning was reassigned to the Auxilia. He lasted four days.

---

## FINAL DOCTRINAL NOTE

The Mortis Auxilia stand beneath honor and above oblivion.

They are the hands that close the battle.

They are the hooks in the ash, the masks in the plague fog, the carts in the alley, the furnace line at dawn, the condemned who become useful, the useful who become contaminated, and the contaminated who keep working because someone must.

Let no commander plan a battle without planning for the bodies.

Let no city speak of victory while its dead remain uncounted.

Let no noble complain of smell before paying the furnace tithe.

The dead are a population.

The Mortis Auxilia are their first and roughest government.
