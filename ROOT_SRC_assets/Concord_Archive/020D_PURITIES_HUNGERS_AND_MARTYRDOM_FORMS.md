This record groups Conceptuals that form around the most intoxicating civic poisons: purity, hunger, and martyrdom. They are grouped because each offers a population a way to make suffering feel chosen.

**Purity Forms** promise that pain comes from contamination. If the impure are removed, the world will become light. They may attach to blood, class, species, doctrine, ash exposure, machine augmentation, poverty, nobility, language, or mourning practice. They rarely begin by demanding massacre. They begin by offering cleanliness.

**Hunger Forms** arise when appetite becomes identity. They differ from Didam doctrine, though the Didam may shelter them. A Hunger Form may begin in famine trauma, noble Draught excess, siege rationing, corpse-render scandal, or taboo feasts. It teaches that to consume is to continue, that all law is garnish, and that the eater is more honest than the citizen.

**Martyrdom Forms** arise when death for a cause becomes more socially useful than survival for a cause. The Legio is dangerously attractive to this class. So are the Cultus Vitae and all noble houses with more banners than grain. A Martyrdom Form does not need soldiers to lose. It needs survivors to envy the dead.

### Shared Signs

All three classes produce simplification. Purity simplifies blame. Hunger simplifies need. Martyrdom simplifies courage. In each case, complexity is cast as cowardice. The impure must be removed. The meal must be taken. The glorious death must be embraced. Questions become symptoms of corruption.

The body responds accordingly. Purity-infected subjects over-wash, self-cut, burn possessions, denounce kin, and obsess over invisible marks. Hunger-infected subjects lick ash from walls, bite their own tongues to taste blood, hoard spoiled food, or describe other citizens in culinary language. Martyrdom-infected subjects become serene before suicidal orders, arrange farewell objects too beautifully, and speak of their deaths as if already witnessed by history.

### Incidents

The Red Lent of Savr-Kesh began as a sanitation campaign and ended with citizens flensing their neighbors for ash freckles. The Nine Banquet Siege began as noble survival provisioning and ended with a district whose survivors could not describe faces except by cuts of meat. The Brass Stair Last Stand produced no Cineric breach during the battle itself; the breach opened decades later through schoolchildren reenacting the charge with unacceptable accuracy.

### Countermeasures

Against Purity, introduce beloved contamination under guard. Against Hunger, restore measured food before preaching restraint. Against Martyrdom, honor survivors with more ceremony than the dead. The last measure is unpopular with old soldiers and absolutely necessary.

Required countersign: **The living cause is not proven by a dead witness.**

### Closing Note

The Cinerum does not need to invent humanity's worst ideas. It waits for humanity to polish them smooth enough to swallow.
