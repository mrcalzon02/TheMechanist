# THE AGE OF CONTINUITY
## Preserved Historical and Doctrinal Record of the Known Ages

Compiled under Continuity Seal by request of the Ninth Civic Archive Synod of Turris-Vey,
with corrections and annotations permitted under Mortis Collegia Advisory Decree 77-A.

Unauthorized duplication punishable under Concordial Continuity Statutes.

---

## ON THE COUNTING OF AGES

Modern Concordial civilization exists within what is commonly designated:
The Eighteenth Age,
or more formally:
The Age of Continuity.

The classification remains disputed.

Several Collegia Mortis historical schools insist humanity emerged only during the latter
portion of the Eleventh Age and that the current epoch should therefore be designated
the Twelfth Great Continuity Cycle.

Others argue the earliest Ages cannot be reliably counted because causality itself behaved
differently in pre-human eras before the stabilization of observed material continuity.

The Ferrum Sanctum rejects several officially recognized Ages entirely,
claiming the sequence was corrupted during the Heliun Divide and subsequent archive purges.

Lower-stack labor populations generally ignore these disputes and refer to all pre-Concord
history simply as:
"The Before Ash."

---

## THE LOST AGES BEFORE HUMANITY

Very little reliable information survives regarding the earliest Ages.

No human civilization existed during the first known epochs.
Knowledge regarding them derives from:
- alien ruins
- dead machine vaults
- pre-human orbital remains
- conceptual fossilization
- impossible archaeological strata
- extinct linguistic structures
- recovered cognition archives
- and partially translated nonhuman memory-engines

The oldest records are fragmentary and frequently contradictory.

Some may not describe chronological history at all,
but states of reality itself.

---

## THE FIRST AGE — THE AGE OF OPEN LIGHT

The First Age is believed to have existed before the current metaphysical instability
between material existence and the Cinerum.

Several recovered nonhuman archives describe reality during this period as:
unpartitioned,
continuous,
and unwounded.

No evidence of breach phenomena appears in surviving records from this epoch.

The Continuity Historians of Zal-Mereth propose that death itself may once have behaved
differently.

This theory remains prohibited for public dissemination.

---

## THE SECOND AGE — THE AGE OF GREAT SILENCE

The Second Age appears associated with the extinction of at least one interstellar civilization.

Recovered ruins suggest:
- vanished stellar engineering systems
- abandoned cognition lattices
- impossible megastructures
- and evidence of sudden synchronized societal collapse

Some Mortis scholars believe this Age ended when the first large-scale conceptual entities
emerged within the proto-Cinerum.

---

## THE THIRD THROUGH SIXTH AGES

These periods are poorly understood and collectively referred to within the Collegia Mortis as:
The Fragment Epochs.

Known surviving evidence includes:
- machine ruins predating known chemistry
- gravitationally impossible architecture
- biological fossil fields exhibiting intentional alteration
- extinct mathematical systems
- evidence of wars fought using manipulated memory itself

One partially translated alien archive repeatedly references:
"The Burning of Thought."

Interpretation remains ongoing.

---

## THE SEVENTH AGE — THE FIRST RECORDED CONTACT ERA

The Seventh Age marks the earliest known evidence of proto-human existence.

Humanity during this era appears fragmented, pre-industrial, and regionally isolated.

Archaeological evidence suggests mankind arose long after numerous older civilizations
had already collapsed.

This realization continues to produce periodic philosophical crises among Ashbourne scholars.

---

## THE EIGHTH THROUGH ELEVENTH AGES

During these Ages humanity expanded gradually through unstable stellar territories while
encountering:
- dead civilizations
- abandoned machine ecologies
- conceptual contamination zones
- and increasingly aggressive breach phenomena

The earliest precursor forms of:
- the Concordia Logistica
- Mortis Collegia
- Ferrum Sanctum
- and Continuity Houses

likely emerged during this period.

Most records were lost during the Ash Wars.

---

## THE TWELFTH AGE — THE NECROLOGICAL REVELATION

The Twelfth Age marks the first confirmed discovery that the dead did not remain dead
in any stable metaphysical sense.

Exploratory voidcraft returned with:
- impossible passengers
- dead relatives speaking through sealed hull plating
- contradictory clocks
- and evidence of environmental conceptual infection

Several early worlds collapsed entirely after uncontrolled exposure to Deadward phenomena.

This period is considered the beginning of the Long Horror.

---

## THE THIRTEENTH AGE — THE AGE OF DISTILLATION

During this era Immog Aeter emerged.

The historical truth regarding Immog remains violently disputed.

What is known with relative certainty is:
- he unified multiple collapsing human polities
- he pioneered necrological distillation processes
- he weaponized rendered Cineric matter
- and he survived conditions previously believed universally fatal

Whether Immog conquered death,
postponed it,
or merely transformed it into another problem
remains unknown.

---

## THE FOURTEENTH AGE — THE FIRST CONTINUITY WARS

The Continuity Wars erupted after the spread of immortality distillation among ruling powers.

Entire civilizations collapsed fighting over:
- access to vitae compounds
- continuity technologies
- deadward extraction zones
- and control of breach corridors

The first Ashbourne dynasties emerged during this period.

---

## THE FIFTEENTH AGE — THE GREAT STACKING

As conditions worsened across many planetary surfaces,
humanity increasingly consolidated into immense vertical fortress-cities.

The Turris-Spireholds were born.

Civilization ceased spreading outward.
Instead it began compressing inward and upward.

This process fundamentally altered:
- social organization
- labor structures
- architecture
- class division
- religion
- and military doctrine

Many modern Concordial systems originate from this period.

---

## THE SIXTEENTH AGE — THE HELIUN DIVIDE

The Sixteenth Age was dominated by the Heliun Divide,
the greatest doctrinal-technological civil war in known human history.

The central question was deceptively simple:

Can continuity survive interruption?

Synthetic Continuity factions claimed identity could be duplicated.
The Continuance Orders declared this metaphysical murder.

Entire worlds burned.

Machine cathedrals split into armed theological blocs.
Synthetic cognition vaults were sterilized.
Planetary populations vanished under continuity sanctions.

The scars of the Divide remain visible throughout the Concord.

---

## THE SEVENTEENTH AGE — THE ASHEN CONSOLIDATION

Following the Divide,
the surviving institutions of humanity reorganized into the modern Cineric Concord.

The Concordia Logistica expanded massively.
The Custodia Veritatis emerged in secret form.
The Eternus Legio standardized military doctrine.
The Ferrum Sanctum accepted restricted oversight.
The Ashbourne consolidated dynastic control.

The modern structure of civilization dates largely from this era.

---

## THE EIGHTEENTH AGE — THE AGE OF CONTINUITY

The current Age is one of exhausted survival.

The manufactories still roar.
The furnaces still burn.
The Legio still marches.
The void fleets still cross the Deadward.

But much of civilization now operates through systems older than living memory.

Entire districts no longer understand the machinery sustaining them.
Ancient cognition engines operate beneath Turris foundations.
The Ashbourne preserve themselves through stolen years.
The Undying Lords pursue continuity without surrendering the self.
The dead continue pressing against reality.

And still humanity endures.

Not because victory is assured.

But because extinction has not yet succeeded.

It remains official Concordial position that humanity persists within the final viable
continuity state currently achievable under observed cosmological conditions.

The Mortis Collegia disputes the word:
final.

The Ferrum Sanctum disputes the word:
viable.

The lower Stack disputes the word:
persists.

## THE AGE OF SHATTERED BANNERS
### On the Disappearance of Immog Aeter

The precise era in which Immog Aeter vanished remains among the most disputed
subjects in all Concordial historiography.

Official Concordial doctrine maintains that the First Distiller withdrew from
public authority during the late Continuity Wars after the stabilization of the
first major Deadward containment corridors.

The Ferrum Sanctum disputes this timeline.

Several hidden Mortis Collegia records instead suggest Immog vanished only after
directly entering the deeper regions of the Ash Sea during what later became
known as:
The Blackward Crusades.

According to these records, Immog claimed:
"The war cannot be won from this side of the wound."

No verified sighting exists after this period.

Yet the rumors persist.

Across the Deadward frontier void-crews still report:
- black warfleets operating beyond mapped transit routes
- impossible breach suppressions
- dead worlds found mysteriously sterilized
- abandoned battlefields filled with entities butchered by unknown weapons
- and sightings of a solitary figure wearing archaic continuity armor older than the Concord itself

Among lower-stack populations Immog is no longer viewed merely as founder.

He has become:
- wandering conqueror
- death-defier
- the Ash King
- the Black Legate
- the Last Continuant
- the Warden of the Outer Dark

The Cultus Vitae officially condemns many of these stories as destabilizing myth.

This has done nothing to suppress them.

## THE FRACTURING OF THE LEGIOS

The disappearance of Immog shattered the unified command structure of the early
Legios.

Without the authority of the First Distiller, command gradually fragmented among:
- the surviving Conclave Houses
- Continuity Generals
- Ferrum Sanctum industrial powers
- Mortis Collegia advisory blocs
- and the emerging Ashbourne dynasties

This period saw the rise of the Concorde Conclave:
a constantly shifting political body composed of military houses, industrial
dynasts, continuity officials, Mortis scholars, and aristocratic immortals.

Officially the Conclave exists to preserve continuity.

In practice it survives through compromise, threat balancing, dynastic maneuvering,
political marriages, economic leverage, military coercion, archive suppression,
and occasionally assassination.

Several historians of the Lower Archives refer to this period as:
The Rule of Bitter Survivors.

---

## THE CONCLAVE DYNASTIES

Many of the great dynastic rivalries of the modern Concord originate during this era.

The Ashbourne competed for:
- vitae monopolies
- Deadward extraction rights
- industrial inheritance chains
- machine-cathedral ownership
- military patronage
- and access to surviving Continuity relics attributed to Immog himself

Entire sectors burned over inheritance law.

Some dynasties collapsed after coordinated Custodia investigations revealed
unauthorized synthetic continuity research hidden beneath their estates.

Others vanished after Ferrum Sanctum continuity audits uncovered impossible
discrepancies in their historical records.

Among the Ashbourne there exists an old saying:

"The throne survives only because every claimant fears the ash more than each other."

## THE XENOFORM WARS

Humanity did not inherit a dead universe quietly.

Even during the rise of the Concord the void beyond known space remained occupied
by ancient nonhuman powers whose origins often predated mankind itself.

Many such civilizations were already wounded by the Cinerum.

Others adapted to it.

Some worshipped it.

---

## THE IRAQ ARACHNOCHUNIDITES
### The Sepulcher Concords

The Iraq Arachnochunidites are among the oldest known surviving nonhuman powers.

Human terminology for the species remains inconsistent because direct visual
observation often induces severe psychological distress.

Most descriptions reference:
- immense segmented forms
- cathedral-scale arachnoid limbs
- membrane-wrapped feeding appendages
- clustered luminous eyes
- and impossible internal movement beneath semi-transparent biological armor

Their civilization, known in surviving records as the Sepulcher Concords,
exists within vast tomb-realms orbiting dead stellar systems where all light
has been intentionally suppressed.

The Arachnochunidites possess deep familiarity with the Cinerum and appear capable
of limited conceptual engineering.

Several Deadward scholars fear they may predate the current metaphysical structure
of reality itself.

The First Sepulcher War began after Concordial explorers attempted to reclaim
a silent necrological relay station near the Veiled Ossuary Systems.

Only one vessel returned.

Its crew had surgically removed their own eyes.

Subsequent wars against the Sepulcher Concords remain among the bloodiest known
Deadward campaigns.

## THE METAL MEN OF THE DEAD STARS

The Metal Men are not confirmed to be biological in any conventional sense.

Recovered Ferrum Sanctum records describe them as:
post-civilizational autonomous continuity remnants.

They inhabit:
- collapsed stellar systems
- dead manufactories
- abandoned cognition worlds
- and machine ecologies older than recorded human history

Some resemble humanoid metallic forms.

Others manifest as:
- distributed machine swarms
- orbital lattice intelligences
- continent-scale cognition structures
- or self-repairing war forms constructed from unknown alloys

The greatest terror surrounding the Metal Men is not their hostility.

It is the possibility that they are what remains after a civilization survives
itself for too long.

The Heliun Divide intensified after several synthetic continuity factions attempted
communication with Dead Star machine ecologies.

Most of those worlds were later sterilized by Continuity decree.

---

## THE HERSCHELAT LEAGUE

The Herschelat League is a fungal macro-colonial civilization exhibiting
extreme hostility toward warm-blooded and oxygen-breathing life.

The League spreads through:
- spore flotillas
- planetary rot blooms
- corpse forests
- atmosphere contamination
- and biological assimilation zones

Entire worlds consumed by Herschelat expansion eventually resemble:
rotting ecosystems attempting to digest themselves.

The Herschelat appear to consider decay sacred.

Recovered translation fragments repeatedly reference:
"The Great Softening."

Several Mortis scholars argue the League may perceive decomposition as a holy
liberation from rigid biological identity.

This interpretation remains controversial.

The Rot Crusades against the Herschelat lasted nearly two centuries and permanently
altered multiple frontier sectors.

Entire Legio formations were lost after fungal infiltration breached environmental seals.

Many surviving veterans voluntarily entered isolation colonies rather than risk
further contamination.

---

## THE AGARON DOMINIONS

The Agaron are reptiloid empire-fragments possessing a profound and ancient hatred
for humanity.

No surviving Concordial archive explains the origin of this hostility.

Several theories exist:
- forgotten prehistoric conflict
- human resemblance to extinct enemies
- conceptual contamination
- disputed precursor territories
- or events predating recorded human civilization entirely

The Agaron refuse all sustained diplomatic normalization.

Their military doctrine emphasizes:
- precision extermination
- rapid orbital assault
- ritualized warfare
- and psychological terror deployment

Captured Agaron records repeatedly refer to humanity using terms roughly translatable as:
"The Unfinished Error."

This phrase remains a subject of ongoing Mortis linguistic study.

The Scale Wars against the Agaron continue intermittently across frontier sectors.

Entire colonies have vanished during Agaron reprisals.

In response the Legio increasingly adopted fortress-world doctrine across contested systems.

## IMPORTANT WORLDS OF THE AGES

### Turris-Vey
Seat of multiple Continuity Synods and among the oldest continuously inhabited
Spireholds in known space.

### Zal-Mereth
Center of pre-Continuity archaeological study and repeated site of Custodia purges.

### Heliun Prime
Destroyed during the final stages of the Heliun Divide after synthetic continuity
containment failed across multiple cognition vaults.

### The Veiled Ossuary Systems
Frontier dead-star region bordering Sepulcher Concord territory.

### Kheprion-VI
Primary manufactorial world of the Ashen Consolidation period.

### The Blackward Corridors
Unstable Deadward transit routes associated with the disappearance of Immog Aeter.

## FINAL HISTORICAL COMMENTARY

The current Age persists only because prior Ages failed slowly enough to leave
machinery behind.

Humanity survives inside systems inherited from catastrophes it barely understands.

The towers endure.
The furnaces burn.
The void fleets still travel.

And somewhere beyond the mapped dark,
if the stories are true,
the First Distiller still wages war against things older than memory itself.
