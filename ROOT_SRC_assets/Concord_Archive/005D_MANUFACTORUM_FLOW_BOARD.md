# 005D MANUFACTORUM FLOW BOARD
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Industrial Scheduling, Raw Material Sequence, Machine-Time Apportionment, Worker Quotas, Furnace Inputs, Output Truth, and the Slow Conversion of Production Into Paperwork Wearing Soot

Filed beneath **005 Concordia Logistica**, jointly reviewed by Ferrum Sanctum industrial auditors, Undying Lord estate factors, Legio procurement officers, Turris maintenance inspectors, and Null Court production-liability clerks. Retained for all manufactorum flow factors assigned to factories whose owners insist that output is a sacred mystery until taxation begins.

This record concerns the **Manufactorum Flow Board**.

A factory is not a building.

A factory is an argument between matter, time, hunger, doctrine, ownership, and the lie everyone tells about capacity.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Manufactorum Flow Board measures, schedules, disputes, and compels the movement of industrial input through Concordial production systems. It tracks ore, ash glass, bone resin, machine oil, ration starch, copper, vat medium, labor shifts, repair pauses, furnace heat, sacred downtime, illegal uptime, sanctioned inefficiency, and everything a machine noble house tries to hide under the phrase proprietary process.

The Board does not own the factories. The Undying Lords, foundry patricians, municipal compacts, military estates, temple works, and emergency state authorities own them in tangled patterns old enough to require specialists in inheritance pathology.

The Board owns the count.

That is often more dangerous.

A factory claiming to produce ten thousand rifle locks but delivering six thousand may be incompetent, corrupt, sabotaged, underfed, underpowered, cursed, mismeasured, doctrinally obstructed, or telling the truth while the receiving warehouse lies. The Board exists to determine which explanation preserves continuity with the least bloodshed and then recommend the bloodshed if required.

---

## MACHINE-TIME

Machine-time is the central currency of the Board. Raw material without machine-time is inventory. Labor without machine-time is crowding. Demand without machine-time is fantasy. A machine hour assigned to rail couplings cannot simultaneously make water valves, ammunition casings, coffin hinges, lift teeth, ration press plates, or noble surgical brackets, though every requesting office will explain why its own need is uniquely existential.

The Board grades machine-time by reliability, contamination risk, operator competence, Ferrum sanction, maintenance debt, energy stability, and likelihood of hidden machine intelligence residue. After the Heliun Divide, machine-time became not merely industrial but moral. To automate too much is suspect. To automate too little is waste. To automate badly is common.

Senior factors are trained to ask the unfashionable question: does the machine actually make what the ledger says it makes?

Many careers end there.

## RELATIONS WITH THE UNDYING LORDS

The Undying Lords describe their manufactoria as continuance estates, sacred process houses, mechanical inheritance bodies, or embodied ancestral engines. The Board describes them as production nodes with owners attached.

This difference of language has caused duels, lawsuits, sabotage, industrial fasts, and one famous incident in which a Foundry Baron mailed a Board auditor three replacement hands with a note suggesting future inspections would require them. The auditor logged the hands as unrequested prosthetic bribe, retained two as evidence, and requisitioned the third after losing his original in a press accident during the inspection.

Relations improved thereafter.

The Board depends on Undying expertise. The Undying depend on Board routing. Each side believes the other would destroy civilization if left unsupervised. Both are correct.

Ferrum Sanctum observers sit in many Board proceedings. Their presence reminds all parties that inefficiency may be sin, but certain efficiencies are heresy.

## OUTPUT TRUTH AND FALSE CAPACITY

Output Truth is the Board's term for the difference between what a factory can produce, what it claims to produce, what it is scheduled to produce, what it actually produces, what it delivers, and what the receiving office acknowledges receiving.

No large manufactorum has a single Output Truth.

It has layers.

The public layer declares success. The noble layer protects prestige. The worker layer hides survival practices. The machine layer hides failure. The Logistica layer attempts arithmetic. The Ferrum layer searches for doctrinal rot. The Legio layer asks why guns are late. The Null Court layer waits until someone important needs blame.

Board inspectors are therefore not merely accountants. They are industrial interrogators. They smell hot bearings, read shift scars, count missing fingers, test rivet batches, compare scrap against declared yield, interview furnace crews, audit prayer pauses, and listen for the peculiar silence that indicates a machine is doing more thinking than permitted.

---

## THE BOARD AS BUREAUCRATIC CORPSE

The Manufactorum Flow Board is a graveyard of old production systems still filing reports. It contains standards for tools no longer made, quotas for wars no longer fought, machine categories banned after the Heliun Divide, emergency forms for factories lost to the Cinerum, and output tables copied so many times that decimal errors have become regional industrial traditions.

The Board rarely deletes obsolete process law because obsolete law becomes useful when modern systems fail. A tool made by hand according to an eight-hundred-year-old pattern may save a pump whose automated replacement line was scrapped after digital contamination. A forgotten furnace schedule may restore production when a noble machine estate falls into succession dispute. A clay-tablet alloy note may matter more than a sealed data vault if the vault whispers back.

This conservatism preserves civilization.

It also preserves nonsense with equal tenderness.

---

## WORKER QUOTAS AND HUMAN MATERIAL

The Board counts workers as labor, risk, knowledge, consumption, morale, and future corpse liability. It does not admit this in public because citizens dislike being described accurately.

Worker quotas must balance production against exhaustion. Exhausted workers produce flawed goods, accidents, sabotage, cult receptivity, fertility collapse, and sometimes art. The last category is not formally tracked unless it blocks aisles or criticizes management.

The Board's more humane factors argue that a rested worker is efficient. Its colder factors argue that a replaceable worker is efficient. Its oldest factors know both statements become false under different emergencies.

The Board's shame is that it has tables for those emergencies.

---

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

Do not worship output.

Output without truth is decoration. Output without maintenance is theft from the future. Output without workers is impossible unless one has recreated the forbidden sins of the Heliun. Output without distribution is warehouse rot.

The factory is only alive when matter enters, work occurs, goods leave, and the lie between those events remains small enough for civilization to survive.
