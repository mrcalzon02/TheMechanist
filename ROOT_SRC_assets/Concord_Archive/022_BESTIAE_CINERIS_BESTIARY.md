**Penalty for unauthorized full recitation:** identity review, furnace reassignment, or delivery to the nearest conceptual contamination desk for language inspection.


This file is not a hunter's romance, soldier's boast, scholar's cabinet, or noble trophy ledger. It is a survival bestiary compiled because the living mind insists on making animals out of things that only borrow animal shape.

The **Bestiae Cineris** are predatory Cineric amalgams that enter material perception through stolen memory, corpse-residue, symbolic expectation, and afterfield hunger. They are not beasts in the biological sense. They do not belong to stable breeding lines known to natural philosophy. They do not require ordinary ecology, though they can infest one. They are hunger arranged into recognizable threat.

Common witnesses describe burned and raw flesh beneath carbonized plates, red wet interiors, multiple heads formed without embryological discipline, limbs borrowed from incompatible animals, eye clusters showing dead weather, mouths that speak in prey voices, hides flickering with eaten memories, and silhouettes assembled from whatever a local population has been trained to fear.

The resemblance to beasts is a courtesy they do not deserve.

A wolf is alive.

A Bestiae Cineris is the memory of being eaten given legs.

## I. TAXONOMIC CAUTION

The bestiary uses phenotype names for operational convenience. These names do not imply species purity, fixed anatomy, reproductive stability, or reliable boundaries. One specimen may present as Render during breach impact, as Mourner during evacuation, as Red Shepherd during withdrawal, and as Crown-Beast when confronted by symbols of authority. The Collegia Mortis therefore classifies by observed function first, tissue second, and appearance last.

Young officers love silhouettes. Old officers love behavior.

The first group sketches horns and counts claws. The second asks where the civilians are being driven, whose voices the thing has learned, whether the wounded are too easy to recover, why the beast avoids the furnace line, and whether the dead soldiers are beginning to answer questions they were not asked.

This is why the present document is arranged by known phenotype and operational role rather than beauty of horror.

## II. GENERAL EFFECTS

Bestiae contact disrupts living systems beyond ordinary trauma. Wounds remember being open. Blood may flow upward. Nerves may receive panic from extinct prey. Hearts may attempt rhythms suitable for other anatomies. Soldiers report instincts that do not belong to humankind: burrowing fear, sky-predator fear, clutch-protection fear, spawning-ground panic, and recognition of scents not present in material air.

Machine systems also degrade near major specimens. The Ferrum Sanctum denies that this proves machines possess souls vulnerable to predation. The Undying Lords insist it proves machines possess continuity fields worthy of reverence. The Collegia records only that pattern stability collapses under intense afterfield pressure and that doctrinal argument has never stopped teeth.

Agriculture rots in track shapes. Doors sweat ash. Dogs bite their handlers. Infants stare at empty corners. Heat behaves strangely around carbon-plate specimens. Mirrors show prey that has not yet been taken. Units ordered into pursuit may find they have been herded rather than hunting.

## IV. LEGIO RESPONSE PRINCIPLES

Against Bestiae, courage is useful only after geometry has been respected.

Maintain heat. Maintain height. Maintain silence where voice mimicry is present. Maintain name discipline. Do not pursue a wounded beast through a route it chose. Do not recover a crying child from a breach margin until three observers agree the child casts a lawful shadow. Do not trust animals that flee toward safety; some have been taught to lead gates behind them. Do not allow soldiers to keep teeth, claws, ash-scales, hide fragments, eye stones, or other trophy matter. The beast may continue using what vanity preserves.

Cohortes Ferrata doctrine favors thermal lances, cinder-shell saturation, mass-driver percussion, distillate-edged containment weapons, and denial of soft corridors. Custodia Cineris doctrine favors civilian silence, controlled evacuation, language inspection, and execution of compromised returnees when salvage fails. The Collegia demands samples. The Legio obeys when alive enough to resent the demand.

## V. FINAL INSTRUCTION

Bestiae Cineris are not the enemy because they are ugly. They are ugly because the mind needs a warning shape before it can justify fear.

The true enemy is hunger from a place where death has not finished dying.

Anything that looks like an animal in the Ash Sea has chosen a shape smaller than its appetite.
