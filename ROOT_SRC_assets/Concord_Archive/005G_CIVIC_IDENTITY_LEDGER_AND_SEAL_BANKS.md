# 005G CIVIC IDENTITY LEDGER AND SEAL BANKS
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Names, Ration Entitlement, Citizenship Marks, Seal Authority, Permit Debt, Duplicate Persons, Dead Persons Still Receiving Bread, and Living Persons Whom the Ledger Has Not Yet Forgiven Into Existence

Filed beneath **005 Concordia Logistica**, cross-indexed with Null Court identity rulings, Custodia Veritatis verification failures, Praetoria Civica arrest protocols, Ration Directorate entitlement tables, and Turris habitation registries. Retained for identity clerks, seal bankers, district factors, and all officials who must remember that a person without record may still be hungry, dangerous, taxable, or all three.

This record concerns the **Civic Identity Ledger** and the **Seal Banks**.

A name is not identity.

A name is where identity begins causing paperwork.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Civic Identity Ledger binds persons, houses, crews, labor blocks, penal cohorts, noble dependents, temple wards, military families, Mortis registrants, and other recognized bodies to ration, housing, tax, movement, work, medicine, inheritance, punishment, and burial entitlement. The Seal Banks issue, validate, revoke, duplicate, encrypt, misplace, rediscover, and occasionally worship the seals by which these entitlements become administratively visible.

The Ledger does not decide whether a person is real.

It decides whether reality has been filed.

The distinction is fatal often enough to support entire legal professions.

---

## PERSONHOOD AS LOGISTICAL CATEGORY

In Concordial practice, personhood is moral, legal, biological, spiritual, military, and logistical. The Civic Identity Ledger concerns the logistical portion and trespasses upon the rest whenever necessary.

A citizen with valid identity may receive food and be arrested efficiently. A citizen with invalid identity may starve in front of a full ration square while clerks attempt to determine whether feeding them creates precedent. A corpse with identity may be returned, burned, studied, taxed posthumously, or inherited from. A corpse without identity becomes Mortis burden and sometimes raw politics.

The Ledger recognizes several identity states:

**Confirmed Living** — eligible for ordinary entitlement and ordinary punishment.

**Presumed Living** — absent, delayed, routed, imprisoned, lost in transit, or inconvenient to declare dead.

**Administratively Dead** — deceased in record, regardless of bodily protest.

**Biologically Present, Legally Suspended** — used for disputed heirs, identity fraud, suspected cult replacements, and citizens caught inside rulings too stupid to explain publicly.

**Duplicated** — more than one claimant under one identity.

**Multiplied by Court Error** — a category expanded after the Del Mondo ruling, when obscure legal phrasing caused masses of lower-hive citizens to share a civic designation to the horror of everyone who enjoyed receiving unambiguous requests.

## SEAL BANKS

Seal Banks maintain the tokens, stamps, codes, strings, chips, waxes, bones, signatures, blood checks, voice prints, witness marks, machine-readable tags, and archaic ceremonial impressions by which authority validates claims. They are called banks because seals accrue value, debt, interest, fraud, and robbery.

A seal may open a warehouse, pass a lift, authorize a ration, redirect a convoy, release a prisoner, prove noble descent, or condemn a counterfeit official to a cage full of other counterfeit officials. Seal authority is therefore protected by paranoia.

The Custodia Veritatis has sealed ident rosarias whose codes may not be present in local systems for decades. The Seal Banks maintain advisories on such failures and are widely ignored until someone important is either freed too late or shot too early.

The Banks' private motto is:

*The seal is valid even when the world is not prepared to admit it.*

This motto is not comforting.

## DUPLICATE NAMES AND IDENTITY DISASTERS

Duplicate identity is an old danger. Families repeat names. Cults steal names. Nobles preserve names. Penal units erase names. Mortis sometimes returns names with insufficient person attached. The Ledger manages these through lineage strings, district anchors, body marks, ration histories, and cross-office confirmation.

Then there are legal disasters.

The Del Mondo ruling remains the most cited caution. A local Null Court order, intended to regularize the citizenship status of unregistered lower-stack residents below a specified floor band, used inherited code language that assigned a standard civic name-marker derived from Armand del Monto across a vast population. The administrative aftermath was not merely comic. Rations duplicated, arrests misfiled, debts merged, marriages became suspect, warrants multiplied, inheritance claims collapsed, and several cult cells obtained lawful meal access under the same name as their victims.

The Ledger spent years separating persons from a name that had become a district.

The case is taught to junior clerks as proof that language is infrastructure.

## THE LEDGER AS BUREAUCRATIC CORPSE

The Civic Identity Ledger is perhaps the purest corpse within the Logistica because it contains everyone and therefore cannot shed its dead without losing part of the living. It has citizens registered to demolished floors, soldiers still drawing pay after becoming memorial statues, noble bastards hidden under servant codes, penal dead counted as labor reserves, entire refugee columns marked provisional for generations, and family lines whose original biological members are extinct but whose legal estates continue consuming ration classes through trust mechanisms.

Every reform attempt creates ghosts. Every purge destroys someone real. Every merge benefits a thief. Every correction angers an ancestor, a house, a gang, a widow, a machine estate, or a child who has only ever known the wrong name.

The Ledger is bloated because the empire is bloated.

It is necrotic because history is not removed when inconvenient.

It is necessary because without it the ration square becomes a knife market.

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

Do not laugh at names.

Names carry bread, debt, law, grief, permits, bodies, doors, and bullets. A spelling error can starve a family. A duplicated seal can arm a rebellion. A dead citizen can vote in a house council if the ledger has not accepted the body's opinion.

When correcting identity, proceed as if touching a loaded weapon.

Because you are.
