# 017B SYNOD OF MECHANICAL EXCISION
## Concordial Restricted Record Concerning the Cutting Away of Mortal Weakness, the Preservation of Knowledge Through Reduction, and the Doctrine That a Man May Become More Himself by Losing Almost Everything That Once Proved He Was One

Filed beneath the parent seal of **017 Undying Lords and Mechanical Continuance**, monitored by the Ferrum Sanctum, cross-noted by the Collegia Mortis for voluntary mutilation law, and restricted from circulation among apprentice surgeons, devotional mechanics, despairing heirs, and any noble child whose tutor has already described the flesh as temporary.

This record concerns the **Synod of Mechanical Excision**.

The Synod of Mechanical Excision is among the oldest, most respectable, most feared, and most predictably horrifying continuance schools maintained by the Undying Lords. Its central doctrine is simple enough for a child to understand and cruel enough for an adult to spend nine centuries elaborating:

Flesh fails.

Cut away failure.

What remains may continue.

The excisionists do not hate the body as a vulgar ascetic hates pleasure. They hate the body as an engineer hates an unserviceable component placed in a critical system by an incompetent predecessor. Skin tears. Stomachs crave. Glands mislead. Teeth rot. Hearts panic. Eyes cloud. Bones snap. Blood betrays. Sleep steals hours. Hunger makes fools of strategists. Desire makes fools of heirs. Pain makes liars of saints.

The machine does not redeem these weaknesses by comforting them.

It redeems them by replacing them.

---

## I. Core Doctrine

The excisionist holds that continuity of mind and duty may be strengthened by the deliberate removal of biological distractions. They do not seek death. They seek narrowing. They believe the human person is not enlarged by flesh but diluted by it. The proper continuant, they argue, should be refined until memory, command, calculation, oath, and will remain unburdened by unnecessary appetite.

Critics describe this as suicide performed slowly with expensive tools.

The synod describes it as maintenance.

Ferrum examiners describe it as permissible until the patient ceases to retain legal self-recognition, begins outsourcing consent to pre-written doctrine, or replaces so much neural tissue that the preserved lord can no longer be distinguished from a habit engine.

The excisionists consider Ferrum caution sentimental.

The Ferrum considers excisionist enthusiasm evidence.

---

## II. Approved Stages of Reduction

The formal pathway varies between houses, but most excisionist lineages recognize seven broad stages.

The **First Shearing** removes cosmetic vanity. Hair, unnecessary teeth, reproductive pride, ceremonial scarring, and redundant flesh are modified or disciplined. Many heirs survive this stage with only arrogance and a better posture.

The **Second Shearing** replaces laboring limbs, damaged joints, sensory defects, and organs deemed inefficient. This stage is widely accepted in noble and military circles.

The **Third Shearing** reduces dependency on sleep, digestion, pain response, and hormonal interruption. Collegia Mortis observers report increased productivity and decreased mercy.

The **Fourth Shearing** places memory aids, command relays, sensory crowns, and interface jacks into direct neural service. Ferrum observers become attentive at this stage.

The **Fifth Shearing** begins true anatomical surrender: heart-support engines, lung-caskets, blood substitutes, nutrient loops, spinal discipline frames, gland annulments, and sealed digestive bypasses.

The **Sixth Shearing** removes the continuant from ordinary life. The subject may no longer eat, embrace, sleep, or bleed in a way recognized by household staff. At this stage the heir becomes less person than appointment.

The **Seventh Shearing** is not legally admitted in many districts. It refers to reduction into throne-casket, skull-engine, nerve-reliquary, command sarcophagus, or distributed organ capsule. The synod insists that the Seventh Shearing is the highest dignity. Living heirs often describe it as being turned into a room.

## III. Social Use

The Synod of Mechanical Excision is most powerful among factories where delay is treated as sin. Its adherents dominate furnace houses, metallurgical dynasties, siege-engine manufactories, voidframe yards, ammunition assurance boards, and those cold administrative towers where a missed signature may kill more workers than a plague.

Its appeal is obvious.

The excised lord does not tire during negotiations.

The excised lord does not weep during layoffs.

The excised lord does not tremble when a child asks whether the factory must remain open during ashfall.

The excised lord does not grow intoxicated at feasts, does not soften after romance, does not become generous after childbirth, does not forget a slight merely because old age has made forgiveness fashionable.

This makes excisionist houses efficient.

It also makes them terrible neighbors.

---

## IV. Heresies and Failure Modes

Mechanical Excision becomes censurable when the subject’s body is reduced faster than the identity can occupy the replacement apparatus. Improperly staged reduction produces echo-panic, command loop stutter, phantom appetite violence, self-disassembly mania, ancestral misrecognition, and the condition known among house surgeons as **beautiful obedience**, in which the preserved lord continues to execute pre-existing duties perfectly while no longer demonstrating any evidence that someone remains inside the duty.

The Ferrum Sanctum classifies beautiful obedience as a near-Heliun risk if the apparatus begins self-correcting without organic referent.

The Synod objects.

The Synod always objects.

Its surgeons insist that identity is not a feeling, not a face, not a sorrow, not a laugh, not the ability to remember a favorite fruit, and not the warmth of a hand. Identity, they argue, is pattern fidelity under obligation.

The Collegia Mortis responds that corpses also possess patterns if one arranges the bones.

Debate remains ongoing and has required armed supervision in three recorded provinces.

---

## V. Relationship With Other Synods

Excisionists despise Wet-Core Preservationists as sentimental gardeners who keep too much weak meat alive. They distrust Electro-Hollow savants for preserving voice and memory in vessels that smell too much like the Heliun disaster. They mock Shidnan cartographers for chasing soul-outline where a clean cut would reveal which parts mattered. They grudgingly respect Electroquant Tappers because neural discipline requires courage, though they accuse them of turning consciousness into arithmetic smoke.

They reserve their deepest contempt for noble houses that purchase mechanical limbs as fashion while continuing to eat, breed, dream, and faint like everyone else.

To the excisionist, half-measure is not moderation.

It is cowardice in polished steel.

---

## VI. Closing Warning

The Synod of Mechanical Excision has preserved geniuses who would otherwise have died. It has kept industrial knowledge active across centuries. It has restored crippled commanders, saved poisoned heirs, maintained weapon foundries through plague years, and made possible continuities that ordinary medicine could not imagine.

It has also produced tyrants who no longer remember mercy as a bodily event.

When dealing with excisionist houses, remember that they may mean every word of their devotion to humanity. They may have cut themselves apart precisely to serve longer, think clearer, produce more, waste less, and stand between civilization and collapse.

Then remember that a man who has removed every source of hesitation may be very sincere when he orders ten thousand workers sealed inside a contaminated factory to preserve output.

Sincerity is not safety.
