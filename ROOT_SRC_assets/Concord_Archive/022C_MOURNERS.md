**Mourners** are Bestiae Cineris adapted to exploit rescue instinct. They cry, limp, plead, whimper, pray, or appear trapped. Their bodies are built from the shapes compassion is least willing to inspect.

### Morphology

Mourners may resemble wounded children, burned pets, collapsed pilgrims, injured soldiers, trapped relatives, or small animals associated with domestic comfort. Under stress, their outlines slip. Extra joints unfold. The crying mouth may continue crying after the head turns away. Some cast shadows shaped like the adult predator form they are attached to elsewhere.

### Behavior

A Mourner chooses thresholds: rubble gaps, trench edges, half-open doors, lift shafts, breach margins, smoke banks, and places where rescuers must break formation. It calls specific names when it has learned them and generic kinship terms when it has not. It may produce real heat signatures, lawful blood chemistry, or correct childhood details stolen from nearby minds.

### Countermeasures

No rescue is performed by one person. No crying subject is touched until shadow, temperature, name response, and floor pressure are checked. Remote retrieval is preferred. If the subject begs not to be scanned, scan twice. Rescue teams must memorize the cruel truth: a real child may die because a false child has killed hundreds. The Concord records this as one of the prices the Cinerum makes the living pay.

Field maxim: **Compassion moves with witnesses.**
