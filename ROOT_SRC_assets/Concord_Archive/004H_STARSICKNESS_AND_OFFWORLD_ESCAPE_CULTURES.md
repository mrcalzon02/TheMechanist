# 004H STARSICKNESS AND OFFWORLD ESCAPE CULTURES
## Concordial Civic-Liminal Archive Record

## ARCHIVAL SEAL AND MIGRATION SCOPE

Issued under joint Civic-Liminal seal for use by transit magistrates, fleet recruiters, Logistica embarkation clerks, Liminal Office screeners, Praetoria dock precincts, and Cultus Vitae pilgrim confessors. This record concerns starsickness, offworld longing, escape cultures, fraudulent migration markets, lawful departure channels, and the belief among common spirehold constituents that salvation may be found beyond the Stack.

There are citizens who dream upward. There are citizens who dream outward. The first want a higher floor. The second want the sky to open and admit them.

## DEFINITION OF STARSICKNESS

Starsickness is not a medical diagnosis in ordinary usage, though Cultus Vitae clinics maintain related entries for fixation, dissociative horizon hunger, void-romantic mania, and transit-delusion fraud vulnerability. In civic speech, starsickness names the ache to leave the spirehold, the planet, the known ration cycle, the inherited floor, the gang boundary, the noble shadow, and the entire vertical machinery of local existence.

The starsick citizen does not necessarily love the void. Many have never seen it except through stained screens, recruitment murals, shrine glass, or illegal projection houses. What they love is departure. The ship is a door. The ash route is a rumor of elsewhere. The fleet seal is a miracle written in metal.

## SOURCES OF OFFWORLD LONGING

Offworld longing is strongest in districts where pressure is high but memory remains wide. A population that knows only the Stack may endure it as world. A population that hears stories of frontier stations, fleet decks, agricultural domes, noble garden moons, ash-sail vessels, free salvage claims, or sister polities beyond Concord law becomes harder to keep satisfied with a leaking room.

Veterans spread longing and dread in equal measure. Dock workers spread technical lies. Pilgrims spread sanctified exaggerations. Smugglers spread opportunity. Liminal agents spread silence, which is more powerful than all of them. A citizen who sees a Liminal choir escorted through a sealed transit gate may spend years imagining what waits beyond it.

## LAWFUL DEPARTURE CHANNELS

Lawful departure from a spirehold is possible through military enlistment, fleet labor assignment, noble household transfer, Logistica convoy service, Liminal auxiliary screening, Cultus Vitae pilgrimage labor, Mortis corpsefleet duty, penal relocation, specialist guild contract, colonial levy, or emergency evacuation. Each route contains danger. Each route also contains status. A person who leaves becomes larger in the imagination of those who remain.

The most common lawful route is military service. Legio recruiters understand starsickness well. A poster promising duty beneath alien skies may recruit a youth more effectively than one promising glory. The chance to die elsewhere can appear preferable to living where one was born.

Fleet labor is the second great channel. Dock gangs, void sweepers, hull patchers, cargo chain crews, air scrub attendants, and corpse-haul teams draw heavily from lower districts. Many depart believing they have escaped the Stack only to find that a vessel is a spirehold turned sideways and taught to starve more efficiently.

## FRAUDULENT ESCAPE MARKETS

Where longing gathers, fraud fattens. False transit writs, forged crew seals, counterfeit pilgrim manifests, illegal cryo berths, noble service scams, ash-route lotteries, dead person's embarkation identities, and promised colony allotments circulate through lower markets. Some frauds merely steal money. Others sell bodies to penal crews, experimental clinics, gang ships, rogue machine houses, or hostile cults requiring transportable victims.

The phrase clean passage is especially suspect. No passage is clean. Any seller who promises departure without debt, risk, scrutiny, or patronage is selling either a lie or a coffin.

Praetoria dock precincts are instructed to treat victims of passage fraud as intelligence sources before treating them as criminals. This instruction is frequently ignored, producing fewer confessions and more corpses.

## CULTURES OF DEPARTURE

Starsick cultures form around symbols of elsewhere. Children collect shipping tags. Workers tattoo route numbers they have never traveled. Lovers swear by docks. Gangs name themselves after vessels. Illegal taverns project false horizons. Shrine choirs sing departure hymns beneath ceilings too low for banners. Old women tell stories of worlds where rain falls downward and is not rationed.

Some departure cultures remain harmless pressure vents. Others become recruitment pools for smugglers, rebels, splinter human polities, ash cults, or Liminal exploitation rings. The desire to leave Concord territory can become political without ever becoming ideologically coherent. The citizen may not hate the Concord. The citizen may simply believe that somewhere beyond its reach, suffering has a different shape.

## RELATION TO SUPPRESSED SPLINTER POLITIES

Rumors of sister empires, lost human enclaves, rebel conclaves, and pre-Continuity polities intensify starsickness. Officially, such rumors are sedition when they encourage unlawful departure or weaken continuity obedience. Practically, they are impossible to extinguish. Empty space is too large for administrative certainty. Humanity has vanished into it many times and occasionally returns wearing different laws.

The common citizen hears suppression as confirmation. If there were nothing beyond, why forbid the story?

This reasoning is dangerous and not always wrong. Officials are instructed to answer with material confidence rather than only denial. A well-fed district listens less hungrily to forbidden maps.

## RETURNED AND BROKEN DEPARTURES

Those who return from offworld service often do not cure starsickness. They complicate it. Some return broken, irradiated, ash-marked, maimed, sterile, promoted, wealthy, haunted, or silent. Some bring gifts that prove the stars are real. Some bring diseases. Some bring forbidden names. Some bring stories of worlds worse than home. Some bring enough money to move their families upward, becoming local saints of departure.

The most dangerous returnee is the one who left, suffered, and still insists leaving was worth it. Such persons become living indictments of the Stack.

## FINAL MIGRATION ASSESSMENT

Starsickness is not disloyalty by nature. It is the human response to confinement under pressure. A civilization built to preserve humanity must expect humans to desire horizons. The task is not to abolish longing. It is to keep longing from becoming prey.

The Stack survives by holding people. It remains legitimate by allowing some to leave. A sealed world breeds dreams sharp enough to cut locks.
