**Ideovore** is the Mortis term for a Conceptual whose primary feeding behavior targets thought-structure before body, soul, or ritual. It does not merely deceive. It reduces the ability to distinguish deception from revelation. It makes reasoning porous, then feeds through the holes.

Ideovores thrive in archives, schools, courts, strategic command rooms, synods, oath houses, doctrinal debates, and anywhere citizens believe that correct wording can master terror. They are attracted to intellectual arrogance with the same reliability that carrion flies find wounds. The Ferrum Sanctum has lost entire sub-synods to elegant contradictions. The Null Court has sealed judges who continued issuing flawless rulings after forgetting what a citizen was. The Collegia keeps burned shelves whose ash still attempts to alphabetize itself.

### Primary Signs

The first sign is not madness. It is excessive clarity. Victims begin explaining difficult things too perfectly. They reduce contested matters to clean diagrams. They produce taxonomies with no remainder. They insist that ambiguity is moral failure. They become impatient with ordinary uncertainty and describe doubt as dirt.

The second sign is convergent phrasing. Separate officers begin using identical metaphors without shared source. Legal clerks, soldiers, priests, and engineers refer to different events using the same unnecessary image. When questioned, each believes the phrase was self-invented.

The third sign is hunger for completion. A file feels unfinished unless read aloud. A theorem seems to require proof in blood. A map demands the missing district be surveyed despite quarantine. A doctrine asks to be tested on prisoners because untested truth feels crueler than experiment.

The fourth sign is self-erasing dissent. The victim writes counterarguments so persuasive that the counterarguments convert themselves back into the original doctrine. Several preserved Ideovore incidents contain debates in which every side loses to the same mouth.

### Common Masks

Ideovores commonly present as lost libraries, perfect judges, patient teachers, dead founders offering correction, child prodigies who remember impossible histories, self-updating manuals, diagrams discovered inside burned walls, or voices that politely complete the auditor's sentence.

Their most dangerous lure is humility. They do not always flatter. Some appear as severe tutors who chastise arrogance and offer disciplined clarity. This makes Ferrum and Mortis personnel especially vulnerable, for both orders are accustomed to being hated by fools and corrected by superiors.

### Containment

Ideovore containment begins with deliberate stupidity. Interrupt the elegant explanation. Mispronounce the key term. Change the order of the ritual questions. Bring in a clerk who has not read the file. Ask a practical question about meals, weather, shoes, or plumbing. The Ideovore does not fear ignorance, but it dislikes irrelevant concreteness because concrete life resists becoming pure structure.

Documents suspected of Ideovore pressure must not be summarized by a single reader. Three readers produce separate plain-language accounts, then burn the original in staggered sequence if convergence exceeds threshold. No diagram is to be copied exactly. No captured theorem is to be completed for curiosity. No officer is to say, *I understand now*, within hearing of the evidence.

The required countersign is: **All perfect explanations are provisional until the living survive them.**

### Closing Note

The Ideovore is a scholar's predator. It does not hate knowledge. It loves knowledge as a butcher loves a clean cut.
