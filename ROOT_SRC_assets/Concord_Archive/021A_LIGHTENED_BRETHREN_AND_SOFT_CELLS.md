The **Lightened Brethren** are the most common Unburdened formation and the least theatrical. They are not called brethren because they are male, clerical, or uniform. They are called brethren because each cell insists that all weight is shared until no one can tell whose weight remains.

They appear in lower stacks, failing manufactories, refugee corridors, ash-weather shelters, debt districts, plague dormitories, penal release yards, and any settlement where citizens have learned that official mercy arrives with forms they cannot read.

### Conduct

Soft Cells feed people. They wash bodies. They sit with the dying. They arrange child care. They mediate household violence. They forgive ration theft. They speak quietly against despair. The first month of a Lightened cell may reduce crime more effectively than Praetoria patrols.

This is why foolish governors tolerate them.

By the third month, members no longer complain of hunger. By the sixth, names begin to blur. By the ninth, the dead are said to have become light and nearby. By the twelfth, the district may answer a single question with a hundred mouths.

### Doctrine

Their teaching avoids grand metaphysics. They say: carry less. Eat less if eating hurts others. Own less because ownership ties the soul to rust. Fear less because fear is the body's final tax. Remember less because memory is a hook. Name less because names nail citizens to suffering.

Every sentence can sound virtuous when heard by the exhausted.

### Suppression

Do not begin with accusations. Begin with material relief. Feed the district under Concord seal without ceremony. Fix the pump. Restore wages. Return a missing body if possible. Give citizens reasons to be angry, hungry, petty, tired, laughing, and particular again. These things are not spiritual failures. They are signs that persons remain.

Cells that proceed to pluralized memory, ash breathing, or dooring are terminated under Custodia Cineris authority.

Field maxim: **Kindness without name becomes weather.**
