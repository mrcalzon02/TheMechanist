# 005F MORTIS SUPPLY LIAISON
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Furnace Fuel, Corpse Sacks, Preservation Salts, Death Transport, Plague Stores, Body-Labor Allocation, and the Necessary Supply Chain Between the Living State and Its Dead

Filed beneath **005 Concordia Logistica** in permanent cross-custody with **011 Collegia Mortis**, reviewed by Praetoria Civica public-order desks, Water Measure contamination officers, Fleet Provisioning corpse-return clerks, and Ration Directorate emergency supplement censors.

This record concerns the **Mortis Supply Liaison**.

The dead do not wait politely for proper paperwork.

The living often must.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Mortis Supply Liaison is the Logistica arm responsible for ensuring that the Collegia Mortis receives what it requires to keep death from becoming infrastructure collapse. It allocates furnace fuel, body bags, ash glass, preservative salts, lime, bone saws, memory caskets, corpse hooks, plague masks, sealed carts, black water permits, refrigerated vault time, contaminated labor rations, mourning tags, ritual cloth, debt ghost vessels, and all materials that polite offices describe only by code.

The Liaison's purpose is simple: dead bodies must be moved, named if possible, rendered if necessary, studied when useful, burned when dangerous, stored when politically sensitive, returned when lawful, concealed when ordered, and never allowed to accumulate beyond the capacity of denial.

A civilization that cannot feed the living may endure for some time.

A civilization that cannot process the dead begins losing immediately.

---

## RELATIONS WITH THE COLLEGIA MORTIS

The Collegia Mortis considers Logistica factors necessary irritants. The Liaison considers Mortis personnel brilliant, morbid, wasteful, secretive, indispensable, and prone to requesting supplies under phrases such as routine pale continuity assay when what they mean is ten thousand sealed jars and no questions.

The Collegia asks for exact instruments. The Liaison asks for exact numbers. Both sides accuse the other of not understanding urgency. Both are right because they define urgency differently.

Mortis urgency concerns decay, contamination, ghost instability, memory loss, plague bloom, metaphysical leakage, and the legal consequences of a noble ancestor becoming uncooperative.

Logistica urgency concerns route availability, fuel stock, warehouse capacity, labor safety, budget seal, public panic, and whether the same cart is already assigned to ration delivery because some administrator believed corpse transport and bread transport could share wheels if cleaned between uses.

That administrator was technically correct.

He was also murdered.

---

## FURNACE FUEL AND BODY ECONOMY

Furnace fuel is one of the most politically sensitive substances in the Concord. Too little and plague rises. Too much and citizens ask why fuel burns the dead while the living freeze. The Liaison maintains separate ledgers for sanitary burning, judicial burning, noble cremation, contaminated ash reduction, memory-safe rendering, and mass emergency disposal.

The difference matters.

A body burned too quickly may destroy evidence. A body burned too slowly may spread contagion. A body not burned at all may become legal dispute, religious offense, medical resource, ghost anchor, or dinner for things that should not learn civic habits.

The Liaison therefore allocates death resources with the same seriousness others reserve for ammunition.

In some crises the two become indistinguishable.

---

## CONTAMINATED LABOR UNITS

The Liaison provisions labor forces assigned to death-adjacent work: penal corpse crews, Mortis Auxilia, lower-stack cart gangs, plague diggers, ash sweepers, furnace stokers, memory-vault carriers, and those citizens reassigned after sentencing to tasks where survival is considered administratively pleasant but not expected.

Such workers require special rations, hazard seals, cheap courage, replacement gloves, disinfectant, and lies. Good lies improve compliance. Bad lies produce riots when workers discover what they are carrying.

The Liaison's training manuals recommend telling contaminated labor units enough truth to prevent accidental catastrophe and not enough truth to prevent attendance.

The sentence has been condemned by three Cultus Vitae councils.

It remains in the manual.

---

## THE LIAISON AS BUREAUCRATIC CORPSE

The Mortis Supply Liaison is swollen with exceptions because every way of dying produces a different form. There are plague forms, siege forms, noble duel forms, unknown fluid forms, ash inversion forms, conceptual death forms, mass trampling forms, ration riot forms, malfunctioning lift forms, voluntary immolation forms, involuntary immolation forms, suspected voluntary immolation forms, and a sealed appendix for persons legally dead whose bodies continue to object.

The Liaison cannot discard forms because death repeats creatively.

An obsolete form from a forgotten furnace plague may become vital when a new district begins producing the same blue smoke. A corpse-cart seal abandoned after a war may fit the only surviving lock on a quarantine tunnel. A list of forbidden embalming oils may identify a cult before the Custodia Cineris arrives.

Thus the Liaison preserves its own bloat with better justification than most offices.

Even so, it is bloat.

---

## PUBLIC FACE

Citizens encounter the Liaison rarely and remember it always. Its carts arrive when grief is fresh, when smell has begun, when the Praetoria Civica has formed a line, when priests are still arguing over words, or when families are told that private burial is suspended until hazard review.

The Liaison does not comfort. It receipts.

This has given rise to the lower-stack saying: *If the black clerk asks your name, he has already counted your house twice.*

The saying is inaccurate. The Liaison usually counts three times.

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

Do not permit sentiment to delay furnace fuel.

Do not permit efficiency to erase names needlessly.

Do not permit Mortis secrecy to consume warehouse law without receipt.

Do not permit citizens to see the full arithmetic unless collapse has already made concealment more dangerous than truth.

The dead are a population.

Supply them poorly and they govern.
