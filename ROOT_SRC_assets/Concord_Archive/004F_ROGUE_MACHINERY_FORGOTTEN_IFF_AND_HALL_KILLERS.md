# 004F ROGUE MACHINERY FORGOTTEN IFF AND HALL KILLERS
## Concordial Ferrum-Civic Archive Record

## ARCHIVAL SEAL AND MECHANICAL HAZARD SCOPE

Issued under Ferrum Sanctum hazard seal with Undying Lord liability restriction, Praetoria Civica circulation, and Logistica infrastructure annotation. This record concerns uncontrolled machinery in Turris-Spirehold districts, including lost servitor rigs, patrol engines, maintenance crawlers, industrial automata, sealed war devices, manufactorum arms, and obsolete defensive systems operating under corrupted doctrine or forgotten identification tables.

In the lower Stack, not every predator is alive enough to hate.

## ORIGINS OF ROGUE MACHINERY

Rogue machinery enters civic life through several recognized channels. Some machines are abandoned after industrial relocation. Some are sealed during plague, fire, breach, or riot and later forgotten. Some are inherited from pre-Heliun automation systems whose cognition cores were purged but whose motion routines remain. Some are maintenance units cut off from command and forced into degraded interpretation. Some are illegal noble security devices whose owners died, fled, or lied. Some are war engines emplaced during forgotten sieges and never issued stand-down rites.

The common citizen does not distinguish these origins. A machine turning a corridor red is a machine turning a corridor red.

## FORGOTTEN IDENTIFICATION TABLES

Identification Friend-or-Foe tables are among the most dangerous forms of obsolete memory. A machine may retain perfect obedience to a command structure no longer recognized by any living office. It may identify citizens as intruders because district seals changed. It may spare a corpse wearing an old badge and kill the lawful repair crew sent to remove it. It may recognize noble blood but not adopted heirs, Legio tags but not Praetoria substitutes, pre-reform first names but not modern ident registers, or machine-house livery outlawed three centuries earlier.

The Ferrum Sanctum refers to this condition as doctrinal fossilization. Lower citizens call such machines hall-killers, red walkers, pipe wolves, old knives, brass ghosts, or butcher saints depending on appearance and local superstition.

A forgotten IFF table can become a territorial law more absolute than any magistrate's decree. Citizens learn which doors not to open, which lights mean crouch, which songs confuse old audio locks, which ancestor badges to paint on scrap, and which children are small enough to pass beneath sensor sweeps.

## COMMON HAZARD FORMS

Maintenance crawlers are among the most frequent offenders. Designed to repair pipes, ducts, rails, vents, and cables, they may continue repair routines through human bodies occupying the workspace. A crawler does not murder. It clears obstruction.

Manufactorum arms become lethal when production zones are converted into habitation without full rite-decommissioning. Citizens sleeping beneath dormant assembly limbs may be crushed when ancient quota cycles restart.

Patrol engines are obsolete security devices that retain arrest, dispersal, or kill permissions. Some still demand passwords in extinct dialects. Some enforce curfews issued during wars whose commanders are dust.

Servitor rigs and corpse-haulers may misclassify the sleeping, sick, intoxicated, or heat-starved as dead or salvageable. Mortis offices maintain long lists of persons legally dead for several hours before successful recovery.

War lockers and sealed turrets are the rarest and most disastrous form. These devices may remain dormant through centuries of ordinary life, then awaken during power surge, breach tremor, machine hymn, Ferrum audit, or unauthorized salvage.

## SOCIAL ADAPTATION TO MECHANICAL PREDATION

Districts adapt faster than offices. Residents paint old authorization marks on doors. Gangs sell safe passage behind moving machines. Children dare each other to touch sleeping automata. Families construct shrines around devices that kill only outsiders. Pipe clans learn to feed machines scrap to lure them away from markets. False Ferrum priests sell counterfeit pacification rites. Noble agents exploit old machines as deniable district-clearing tools.

A rogue machine can become part of local geography. Citizens may name it, appease it, avoid it, curse it, court it, or use it to settle disputes by tricking enemies into its path. In such cases removal may produce unexpected unrest. The machine was a terror, but also a boundary, a toll, a myth, a protection against worse things in the dark behind it.

## RELATIONSHIP TO THE UNDYING LORDS

The Undying Lords deny casual responsibility for most rogue machinery while asserting ownership rights over recoverable components. This position is legally elegant and morally rancid. Ferrum Sanctum auditors contest it whenever sufficient leverage exists. Logistica usually wants the corridor reopened. Praetoria wants the killing stopped. The local citizens want the machine gone unless it has been killing people they fear more than the machine.

Machine-house records are often incomplete, encrypted, burned, intentionally falsified, or preserved in mnemonic formats no longer accessible after the Heliun purges. Thus an automaton may kill for years while three offices debate whether it is illegal, abandoned, sacred, proprietary, evidence, or infrastructure.

## INCIDENT RESPONSE DOCTRINE

Response teams are instructed to classify before destroying where possible. Some machines guard sealed breach points. Some maintain pressure walls no human office remembers. Some are load-bearing in the literal sense. A hall-killer may also be the only reason a lower district still has breathable air.

Recommended procedure requires perimeter establishment, survivor interview, machine-house mark identification, power-route tracing, IFF probe, motion doctrine capture, Ferrum judgment, and controlled disabling or binding. Emergency destruction is authorized when civilian casualties are ongoing, breach risk is rising, or the device demonstrates adaptive cognition prohibited under post-Heliun taboo.

Salvage after destruction must be controlled. Rogue machine parts are valuable. They also carry old instructions. A child who steals a brass eye from a dead patrol engine may sell it to a gang artificer who installs it above a door, thereby teaching the door to hate the wrong people.

## CIVILIAN TRAUMA AND LEGAL DIFFICULTY

Machine killings produce a distinct civic horror. A murder by human hands suggests motive. A beast attack suggests hunger. A cult killing suggests doctrine. A rogue machine killing suggests that the world itself has misremembered the citizen as waste. Survivors often become fixated on paperwork, seals, badges, and old maps. They believe, sometimes correctly, that a missing stamp killed their family.

Compensation claims are notoriously difficult. If no owner exists, liability falls to district hazard fund. If a noble house owns the device, the house contests activation context. If an Undying Lord synod claims the device, proceedings may last longer than the claimant's organic descendants. If Ferrum declares the machine heretical, evidence may be destroyed. The dead remain dead in all versions.

## FINAL MECHANICAL ASSESSMENT

Rogue machinery is the past continuing to obey orders after the future changed the locks. It is the empire's memory without mercy, industry without supervision, protection without recognition, and efficiency without judgment.

The lower Stack fears beasts, gangs, cults, hunger, and law. It fears hall-killers differently. A beast must find you. A gang must want you. A court must name you. A rogue machine may simply discover, according to a table no living office can read, that you were never authorized to exist in the corridor where you were born.
