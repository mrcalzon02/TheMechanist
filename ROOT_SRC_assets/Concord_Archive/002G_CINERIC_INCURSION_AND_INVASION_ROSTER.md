This record is not maintained for comfort. It is maintained because the dead remember poorly, the living remember conveniently, and the Concord survives only when enough officers know which kind of memory is trying to enter the room.
## I. PURPOSE OF THE ROSTER

The incidents preserved in the parent Cinerum record are not complete. They are instructional fragments, chosen because each teaches a different failure. This roster records additional incursions, invasions, great battles, and ash-front disasters whose full files remain sealed, damaged, contested, or too dangerous for ordinary officer education.

No list of Cineric events is final. The Ash Sea does not respect chronology well enough to make archives comfortable.

## II. THE ARGENT MAW DESCENT

The Argent Maw began as a luminous breach above the polar cathedral of Suth Veyr. Witnesses described a silver aperture ringed by the faces of canonized dead. Pilgrims gathered. The Cultus Vitae requested recognition of a miracle. Mortis examiners requested artillery. The cathedral bells rang without contact for seventeen hours while frost formed on candles still burning.

At descent, the aperture did not open downward. The sky bit the cathedral. Stone, pilgrims, banners, and three kilometers of city were drawn upward into a mouth-shaped absence. The Legio counterattack shattered the bell towers to break the resonance. Survivors later insisted they heard singing from above for nine years.

## III. THE LOW-BELL SIEGE OF KARSHT

Karsht’s lower districts were besieged by a sound rather than an army. Every bell below the forty-second floor began ringing at a frequency that induced obedience in the sleep-deprived. Citizens walked into furnace queues voluntarily, claiming their names had been called. Praetoria Civica units trying to enforce curfew became bell-ringers. Custodia Cineris teams survived by deafening themselves before entry.

The siege ended when Cohortes Ferrata collapsed the bell-foundries under their own foundations. The city still forbids round metal vessels below certain floor levels. Citizens complain. The complaint is recorded as a sign of insufficient historical education.

## IV. THE RAIN OF HANDS AT PELL-GRAVE

Pell-Grave suffered a six-hour fall of severed hands, each bearing a wedding ring from a different century. No two hands decayed at the same rate. Some carried ash-script marriage contracts binding the finder to the dead. Several families accepted these contracts under famine pressure in exchange for food appearing on tables. Within a week, half the town claimed spouses in the Cinerum and refused lawful remarriage, taxation, and military levy.

The Null Court annulled the marriages by declaring the hands unpersons. The hands signed the appeal.

## V. THE ASH-GLASS INVASION OF TRENCH SAINT ORUBA

During the Oruban Crusade, deadward pressure vitrified forty kilometers of trench line into black glass while soldiers still occupied it. The trapped bodies remained visible and continued signaling by eye movement. Legio commanders initially attempted rescue. Mortis officers argued the signals were not human. The dispute ended when the glass began issuing orders in the voices of the trapped officers, some tactically excellent.

The trench was bombarded until no reflective surface remained larger than a coin. Veterans still avoid polished boots before battle.

## VI. THE VESPER CONVOY THAT ARRIVED TWICE

A Logistica convoy lost in ash-route transit emerged at Vesper Gate with full cargo, intact seals, and crews unable to remember departure. Nine days later the same convoy arrived again, also intact, also sealed, also claiming ignorance. The duplicates accused each other of theft, heresy, and poor driving. The second cargo contained famine grain that saved the station. The first contained grain that induced dream obedience.

Both convoys were burned. The famine resumed. The station survived angry, which is preferable to grateful under unknown terms.

## VII. THE CHARCOAL CHILDREN OF NEMAR FOLD

Nemar Fold reported children emerging from old crematory stacks during winter shortage. They were warm, polite, hungry, and claimed to be descendants of the houses that took them in. Family records adjusted themselves to include them. Within months, inheritance disputes collapsed because the charcoal children had better documentation than the living.

Custodia Veritatis discovered that each child corresponded to an unburned grief event in the district’s prior plague. The children were not impostors. They were solutions offered by a predator that understood lineage.

## VIII. DOCTRINAL SUMMARY

Cineric invasion is not always martial. It may arrive as sound, paperwork, weather, affection, duplicate supply, inheritance, marriage, efficient tactics, or relief. The Legio kills monsters. The Concord survives by learning that some monsters arrive as answers to reasonable needs.
