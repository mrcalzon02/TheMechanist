# 002I — SUPPRESSED SPLINTER CONCLAVES AND SISTER EMPIRES

**Cineric Concord Central Mortis Archive**  
**Record Family:** Restricted Political Dossier / Human Disunity Beyond the Concordial Myth  
**Seal:** Ash-Black / Custodia Veritatis / Eternus Legio Strategic Suppression / Null Court Historical Sequestration  
**Circulation:** High legates, Custodia historiographers, Null Court continuity judges, Logistica long-route censors, and Liminal Office navigators cleared for contact-denial doctrine.  
**Penalty for unauthorized recitation:** identity deletion, exile into unmarked service, or surrender to a quiet court without advocate.

This record is not maintained for comfort. It is maintained because the dead remember poorly, the living remember conveniently, and the Concord survives only when enough officers know which kind of memory is trying to enter the room.
## I. THE FORBIDDEN ADMISSION

Humanity is not unified.

This sentence is treason when spoken in a ration line, sedition when printed in a schoolbook, and ordinary fact inside the sealed cabinets of those who must plan suppression actions beyond Concordial maps. The Cineric Concord is the largest surviving human order known to its own officers, but vastness is not totality. Empty space is intractable. Ash routes fail. Records burn. Shorelines close. Worlds vanish from schedule and continue without permission. Fleets flee. Governors declare necessity. Noble houses lose wars and call exile sovereignty. Conclaves dissent. Some human polities predate the Great Expansions and even the Continuity Wars. Some were never conquered. Some were conquered and escaped. Some were forgotten and prefer the condition.

Public unity is a weapon. Private disunity is the map.

## II. SISTER EMPIRES

The term sister empire is not complimentary. It refers to human polities large enough to possess law, fleets, industry, lineage, and military continuity outside Concordial obedience, yet small enough that the Concord expects eventual suppression, absorption, collapse, or loss to the Wastes. Some are hereditary fragments of pre-Concord regimes. Some are ash-route colonies that severed contact during famine cycles. Some are ideological descendants of defeated Sarantaran, machine, civic, or cultic orders. Some are little more than warlord logistics wrapped in stolen ceremony.

They are universally treated as mortal enemies of the Legio because they prove that the Concord’s claim to human necessity is incomplete. Their existence tells soldiers that obedience is not the only human answer to darkness. This is intolerable even when the sister empire itself is cruel, doomed, foolish, or already half-eaten.

## III. WHY SUPPRESSION IS ROUTINE

Suppression actions are not always wars. They include route denial, archive poisoning, interdiction of marriage ties, seizure of Liminal bloodlines, sabotage of manufactoria, assassination of continuity claimants, silent purchase of famine grain, Custodia insertion, identity-seal invalidation, and the careful spreading of true histories at moments calculated to cause succession crisis.

The Legio calls such actions frontier stabilization. The Custodia calls them historical hygiene. The Null Court calls them necessary disinheritance. The victims call them invasion, when they live long enough to file the accusation.

## IV. KNOWN SPLINTER TYPES

The Sarantaran Remnants claim descent from governing structures overthrown by Immog and preserve laws in which the First Distiller is classified as a military usurper and metaphysical contaminant.

The Mournful Compact maintains that Nezramite grief should be negotiated with rather than suppressed, arguing that sorrow is the only honest state after the First Distillation. Most contacts end in acoustic quarantine.

The Free Furnace Leagues reject Collegia Mortis corpse law and burn their dead without Concordial indexing. Their worlds suffer fewer debt ghosts and more unclassified revenants. Both facts are used as propaganda by both sides.

The Salt-Chart Principalities are route-polities built around Liminal houses that fled Concord breeding contracts. They navigate differently, die differently, and charge obscenely for passage when desperate Concord fleets find them.

The Red Account Cities recognize Logistica debt as the highest form of citizenship and claim the Concord has mismanaged the sacred economy of survival. Their rebellions often begin as audits and end as sieges.

The Pale Mechanic Courts shelter Undying dissidents, Heliun sympathizers, or machine houses unwilling to submit to Ferrum censorial doctrine. They are hunted with unusual zeal.

## V. LOST HUMANITIES

Not every absent polity is rebellious. Some are simply gone from knowledge. A world may miss three convoys and become legend. A fleet may enter an ash route and arrive six centuries late with laws intact and maps useless. A colony may survive behind a closed shoreline, breeding customs around an emergency that ended before Concord historians were born. The void between stars is wide enough for entire civilizations to become rumor without dying.

This uncertainty wounds Concordial ideology more deeply than open rebellion. Rebels can be named traitor. Unknown humans cannot be easily categorized. They may be purer, worse, older, luckier, freer, more corrupted, or simply hungry. The Concord therefore prefers not to discuss them until a campaign requires killing them.

## VI. PUBLIC DENIAL

The official teaching remains: humanity endures beneath Concordial continuity. This is not wholly false. It is merely incomplete enough to be useful. The average citizen does not need to know that sister empires exist. The average citizen needs ration, water, work, prayer, and a reason not to listen when ash speaks with a human accent.

High officers require the worse truth. Humanity is far from a unified front. It survives as enclaves, empires, cults, routes, houses, prisons, fleets, and mistakes scattered across a darkness large enough to hide every form of treason and every form of courage.
