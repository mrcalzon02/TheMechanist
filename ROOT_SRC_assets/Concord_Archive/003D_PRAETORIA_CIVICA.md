# 003D PRAETORIA CIVICA
## Concordial Military Archive Record Concerning Military Police, Civil Enforcement Cohorts, Curfew Arms, Barracks Law, Riot Discipline, Checkpoint Courts, and the Steel Edge Where Soldier and Citizen Are Forced to Meet

Filed under the parent seal of **003 Eternus Legio and Military**, civil-crossed by the Null Court, observed by the Custodia Veritatis where necessary, administratively contested by municipal magistracies, and retained for all commanders who must remember that a city can be lost without a single enemy army entering it.

This record concerns the **Praetoria Civica**.

The Praetoria Civica are the Legio arm most citizens believe they understand because they have been shouted at by it.

This belief is wrong but understandable.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Praetoria Civica are the military police and civil enforcement formations of the Eternus Legio. They govern the boundary between military necessity and civic order in territories under Legio authority, partial occupation, emergency seal, ration discipline, evacuation control, quarantine, mutiny suppression, spire-defense mobilization, or post-breach reconstruction.

They arrest soldiers.

They arrest citizens.

They arrest officers when officers are foolish enough to believe rank is armor against law.

They enforce curfew, ration priority, checkpoint passage, convoy discipline, barracks order, deserter retrieval, arms licensing, forbidden assembly decrees, emergency labor drafts, evacuation queues, and the endless small commands by which terrified populations are prevented from becoming weather.

To the citizen, they are often indistinguishable from ordinary law enforcement.

To those who understand authority, they are far more frightening.

Civil police enforce law within the city.

The Praetoria Civica enforce the condition under which the city continues to be available for law.

---

## ORIGIN AND NECESSITY

The Praetoria Civica emerged because the Legio repeatedly discovered that victories at the front could be undone behind the line by panic, looting, hoarding, desertion, rumor, noble sabotage, fraudulent evacuation orders, black ration markets, and civilians who believed their suffering exempted them from physics.

No wall holds if the gate crew sells hinge pins for bread.

No evacuation succeeds if nobles fill refugee trains with furniture.

No ration plan survives if every district declares itself uniquely deserving.

No army remains an army if its soldiers learn they may rob the citizens they claim to protect.

The first Praetoria were barracks disciplinarians and convoy provosts. They became civil enforcers during the early spire collapses, when local courts could not issue judgment fast enough and municipal police either fled, joined riots, or were eaten by the crowds they attempted to calm. The Legio placed armored provosts at ration vaults and discovered that the sight of a soldier willing to shoot a noble and a beggar by the same rule had a stabilizing effect upon civic philosophy.

---

## THE CIVIC BATON AND THE RIFLE

Praetoria doctrine holds that every enforcement act contains two instruments: the baton and the rifle.

The baton represents correction within continuity. It moves a citizen back into line, breaks a thief's hand, closes a door, disperses a crowd, drags a drunk soldier from a shrine, or turns a noble carriage away from an evacuation ramp.

The rifle represents the end of persuasion.

A Praetorian must know which instrument is present even when neither is visible. A checkpoint order spoken politely may be a rifle order. A rifle held during a ration queue may still be a baton action if the crowd obeys. Confusing the two kills cities. Too much baton allows collapse to organize. Too much rifle teaches the population that obedience and death are neighbors, at which point rebellion becomes a form of scheduling.

The Praetoria are trained in the mathematics of fear.

Not enough fear and the crowd surges.

Too much fear and the crowd becomes an animal.

Correct fear and the crowd becomes a queue.

---

## ORGANIZATION

Praetoria units are organized into **provost files**, **checkpoint courts**, **curfew cohorts**, **riot shields**, **barracks correction teams**, **convoy discipline bodies**, and **civil seal detachments**.

A **provost file** is the smallest working unit, sufficient for arrest, patrol, gate inspection, and soldier discipline.

A **checkpoint court** combines armed enforcement with emergency clerks empowered to issue immediate passage rulings, identity classifications, ration priority adjustments, detention orders, and corpse transfer permissions.

A **curfew cohort** controls district movement during emergency hours. Its members know the difference between a citizen seeking medicine, a smuggler moving false bread, a cult courier, a frightened child, and a noble servant claiming all four at once.

A **riot shield** is a formed body trained in crowd compression, shield-wall advance, stun-fire, smoke discipline, water denial, bridge clearing, and the precise moment when a crowd ceases to be citizens and becomes a kinetic threat.

A **civil seal detachment** enters municipal offices, archives, courts, guild houses, and noble bureaus to secure records before officials can correct their own treason into innocence.

---

## RELATION TO THE NULL COURT

The Praetoria Civica are not the Null Court's soldiers, though many citizens believe any armed person carrying a sealed order must belong to the same nightmare.

The Null Court can make reality illegal by wording.

The Praetoria must stand in the street when the wording arrives.

This relationship is resented by both sides. Null magistrates consider Praetorians blunt instruments, useful for making rulings incarnate but prone to moral impatience. Praetorians consider Null magistrates dangerous weather in robes, capable of outlawing drinking water through legal elegance and then sleeping indoors while soldiers are beaten to death enforcing the correction.

Nevertheless, the Praetoria carry Null orders when required. They have redistricted hab-blocks, emptied noble estates, renamed citizen categories, sealed ration goods, transferred child populations, enforced execution quotas, and once spent fourteen months attempting to determine which of several thousand legally identical Armand del Monto citizens had actually stolen a pump valve.

The Del Mondo enforcement files remain compulsory reading in Praetoria academies.

The lesson is not that courts are foolish.

The lesson is that soldiers must learn to survive being ordered by paper.

---

## RELATION TO CIVIL POLICE

Municipal watchmen, spire guards, guild constables, road reeves, market inspectors, and noble bailiffs all resent the Praetoria Civica until the first breach siren sounds. After that, resentment becomes requests for reinforcement.

The Praetoria do not replace civil police where civil police remain functional. They supersede them where military continuity requires it. A local watch may arrest a murderer. The Praetoria arrest the murderer, the watchman who hid him, the ration clerk who supplied him, the noble patron who protected him, and the crowd that has gathered in a pattern resembling a martyr cult.

Civil law asks who committed the crime.

Praetoria law asks whether the crime has become a threat to continuance.

---

## BARRACKS LAW

The Praetoria are hated most honestly by soldiers.

A soldier caught stealing civilian water during ration seal may be flogged, branded, reassigned to Mortis Auxilia, or shot. A soldier who assaults a citizen under protection may be publicly stripped and handed to civil justice if civil justice still exists. A soldier who abandons equipment may be chained to that equipment until he understands its value. A soldier who invents false orders during panic is hanged with the false order nailed beneath his chin.

These punishments are not cruelty for its own sake. The Legio is armed. An armed institution that cannot police itself becomes an occupying enemy by accident.

The Praetoria exist to prevent that accident from becoming policy.

---

## CIVILIAN REPUTATION

The common citizen fears the Praetoria more immediately than the distant Legate, the secret Custodian, or the hidden Cohors Noctis.

The Praetorian is the one at the door.

The Praetorian is the one who says the queue ends here.

The Praetorian is the one who takes the son for labor draft, the daughter for witness custody, the father for curfew breach, the mother for ration fraud, and the corpse for examination.

This fear produces hatred. The Archive does not deny it.

Yet when the cult comes, when the noble guards begin shooting over water, when the lower gates fail, when the dead walk into the market wearing family faces, the same citizens search for Praetorian helmets because terror prefers a uniform it recognizes.

---

## EQUIPMENT AND SYMBOLS

Praetoria equipment includes shock batons, compact rifles, shield plates, identity hooks, restraint wire, seal hammers, curfew lamps, portable loudhailers, document spikes, riot masks, ration keys, body ledgers, and black-red authority sashes marked with command jurisdiction.

The most hated Praetoria tool is the **queue chain**, a weighted line used to impose order on ration crowds, evacuation ramps, and bridge crossings. Citizens remember the chain as humiliation. Praetorians remember the chain as the reason the front ranks did not crush the children.

Their badge is the closed gate beneath a sword.

Their unofficial mark is a bootprint over paperwork.

---

## CASES OF NOTE

During the **Bitter Lift Evacuation**, Praetoria officers shot eight noble retainers attempting to load antique furniture into a medical ascent platform. The lift carried two hundred wounded citizens instead. The noble house filed complaint for seventy years. The complaint was eventually denied because none of the furniture remained alive to provide testimony.

In the **Salt Queue Panic**, a Praetorian riot shield advanced too quickly and caused a crush that killed nine hundred citizens. The commanding officer was executed. The crowd was later found to have been infiltrated by hunger cult agitators. The execution was not reversed. Competence is judged by outcome as well as enemy intent.

At **Turris Halewick**, Praetoria checkpoint clerks discovered that a local magistracy had issued fourteen thousand emergency passage exemptions to dead citizens in exchange for grain. The dead were arrested in absentia. The living clerks were arrested in person.

---

## FINAL DOCTRINAL NOTE

The Praetoria Civica are not loved because they stand where affection cannot operate.

They are not trusted because trust is too slow during collapse.

They are not admired because their victories look like boredom, queues, locked doors, confiscated weapons, restrained soldiers, and citizens still alive enough to complain.

Let them be hated within measure.

A city that can still hate its enforcers is a city that has not yet become ash.
