# 017F WET-CORE PRESERVATIONISTS
## Restricted Record Concerning Living Nerve Cores, Organ Continuance, Warm Caskets, Biological Anchors, and the Sentimental Refusal to Admit That the Flesh Has Been Defeated Until the Last Useful Cell Stops Arguing

Filed beneath the parent seal of **017 Undying Lords and Mechanical Continuance**, inspected by the Ferrum Sanctum, heavily annotated by Collegia Mortis preservation surgeons, and restricted from circulation among hospital cults, noble maternity houses, and organ-debt petitioners.

This record concerns the **Wet-Core Preservationists**.

Among the Undying Lords, few schools are mocked more often and depended upon more frequently. Excisionists call them gardeners of weakness. Electroquant Tappers call them slow technicians. Electro-Hollow synods call them sentimental. Shidnan cartographers call them necessary witnesses to the shape of embodied continuity.

The Wet-Core houses answer none of these insults quickly because many of their senior members require several minutes to warm the speaking tubes.

They preserve flesh.

Not all flesh. Not vanity flesh, not ornamental flesh, not the full wasteful animal sprawl of unmodified mortality. They preserve the minimum living biological core required, in their doctrine, to keep a person anchored in humanity.

They believe the body is not merely a failure to be replaced.

They believe it is a treaty.

---

## I. Core Doctrine

The Wet-Core doctrine holds that memory and command alone are insufficient. A person without living biological reference risks becoming tool, echo, ghost, habit, or beautiful obedience. The preserved lord must retain warm tissue that suffers, repairs, metabolizes, responds, and remains vulnerable enough to remind the machine that the subject is not merely a process.

This doctrine has enormous moral appeal until one sees how the cores are maintained.

Wet-core continuants may consist of a brain, spinal root, endocrine knot, heart-fragment, gut culture, gland cluster, or distributed organ braid housed within a life-support casket. The casket may be beautiful, plated, perfumed, and serenaded by family choirs. It is still a box keeping meat from admitting defeat.

The houses insist that this is more humane than reduction to cold mechanism.

Their organ ledgers complicate the claim.

---

## II. Organ Debt

Wet-Core Preservation requires supply: compatible tissue, blood cultures, immune stabilizers, neural graft material, glandular correctives, nerve sheath, marrow stock, and sometimes whole bodies whose names are later discovered to have been misspelled in consent records.

Noble houses maintain organ-debt rolls among relatives, servants, penal laborers, vat-lines, purchased debtors, and medically compatible citizens unfortunate enough to have been born under the wrong jurisdiction. The Concord regulates this practice. The regulation is frequently excellent, thoroughly indexed, ceremonially witnessed, and ineffective whenever a failing continuant owns the only water recycler in a district.

Ferrum inspectors look for unauthorized harvest.

Collegia Mortis examiners look for corpse-law violation.

Null Court judges look for wording.

The living donors look for doors.

---

## III. Warm Casket Culture

Wet-core houses cultivate warmth. Their continuance chambers are humid, perfumed, pulsing, and full of soft mechanical noises that resemble breathing even when no lungs remain. Servants wear heated gloves. Pipes are polished not to shine but to radiate comfort. Family members speak to caskets at meals. Children are taught to place their palms against ancestor-glass and feel the faint answering heat.

This produces loyalty rarely seen in colder machine houses.

It also produces dependence so intimate that heirs may believe the ancestor loves them because the casket warmed when they entered.

Sometimes it did.

Sometimes the technician increased the temperature to prevent rebellion.

Both possibilities are recorded.

---

## IV. Strengths

Wet-Core preservation excels at maintaining emotional continuity, sensory recognition, grief response, family memory, and certain embodied intuitions that other synods lose. Wet-core lords are often better negotiators with living populations. They remember hunger more vividly. They may still experience pain, comfort, disgust, affection, fatigue, and fear in forms recognizable to ordinary persons.

This makes them less monstrous.

It also makes them easier to torment.

A wet-core continuant can be threatened by coolant failure, infection, tissue rejection, donor refusal, warmth deprivation, nerve inflammation, and the slow panic of feeling trapped inside a body reduced to its most helpless truths.

Many become gentle.

Many become tyrants of need.

---

## V. Heresies and Crimes

The principal wet-core crimes include involuntary donor harvest, kin-vat falsification, corpse reactivation, penal body overuse, black-market gland purchase, living servant integration, and the forbidden practice called **house pooling**, in which several partial continuants share a biological support system and gradually lose track of whose pain is whose.

The Ferrum Sanctum tolerates individual wet cores. It despises pooled identity.

The Collegia Mortis classifies improper pooling as death contamination.

The Cultus Vitae calls it a prayer that forgot which mouth began it.

The houses call it efficient.

The argument usually ends when the support tank cracks.

---

## VI. Closing Assessment

The Wet-Core Preservationists are the conscience of the Undying Lords when they are sincere, and the appetite of the Undying Lords when they are not. They remind the machine houses that humanity may require vulnerability. They also turn vulnerability into a supply chain.

When auditing a wet-core estate, count not only the preserved lord but the donors, the vats, the missing servants, the revised medical consents, the unusually warm nursery, and the children who have been told since birth that their blood is a family honor.

A cold machine may enslave a district.

A warm casket may convince the district to volunteer.
