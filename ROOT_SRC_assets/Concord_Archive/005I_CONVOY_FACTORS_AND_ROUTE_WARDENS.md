# 005I CONVOY FACTORS AND ROUTE WARDENS
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Overland Supply, Ash-Road Caravans, Armed Ledgers, Toll Suppression, Smuggler Accommodation, Dead-World Runs, and the Men and Women Who Carry Receipts Through Places That Eat Witnesses

Filed beneath **005 Concordia Logistica**, cross-referenced with Limes Warden frontier tables, Legio escort doctrine, Praetoria Civica road-law enforcement, Turris gate schedules, and Fleet Provisioning surface-to-vessel transfer records. Retained for convoy masters, route wardens, bonded drivers, escort officers, toll adjudicators, and factors assigned to roads whose official safety rating is an act of optimism.

This record concerns the **Convoy Factors** and **Route Wardens** of the Logistica.

The ledger is not safe because it is written.

It becomes safe only after it arrives.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

Convoy Factors are mobile Logistica officers responsible for preserving the legal identity, measured integrity, route priority, and delivery consequence of goods in transit. Route Wardens secure, inspect, deny, open, repair, extort less than bandits, and sometimes fight along the roads, causeways, ash lanes, maintenance corridors, bridge chains, dead-world tracks, and outer district passages through which those goods move.

A stationary clerk may believe supply is a problem of arithmetic.

A convoy factor knows arithmetic bleeds.

Every crate between origin and destination passes through weather, theft, bribery, jurisdiction, breakdown, hunger, rumor, cult attention, noble interference, local need, escort incompetence, map error, and the temptation to become someone else's emergency reserve.

The convoy factor's duty is to make the crate remain itself.

---

## ARMED LEDGERS

Convoy ledgers are written to be defensible in court and useful under gunfire. They identify cargo, route, hazard, seals, substitutions, escort composition, authorized losses, forbidden losses, emergency release conditions, bribe declarations, expected tolls, known false tolls, and names of officials whose signatures are likely counterfeit but politically inconvenient to reject without artillery nearby.

A convoy factor carries duplicate records: one visible, one sealed, one hidden, one memorized, and, on dangerous routes, one deliberately false for bandits who respect paperwork but not enough.

The phrase armed ledger refers both to these records and to the factor's escort. The Logistica insists it is a metaphor. Route Wardens disagree and clean their rifles.

---

## ROAD LAW AND TOLL DISEASE

Roads develop parasites. Some are bandits. Some are local militias. Some are noble checkpoint households. Some are religious kitchens demanding tithe. Some are desperate districts taxing passing food because their own allotment failed. Some are official tolls that became unofficial over generations and then reentered official law through habit.

The Logistica calls this Toll Disease.

Eradicating Toll Disease entirely is impossible and often undesirable. A local toll may maintain a bridge better than distant authority. A gang may keep a route safer than underfunded Praetoria posts. A noble gate may be illegal but useful during ash storms. The question is not whether unauthorized extraction occurs. The question is whether it damages continuity more than suppressing it would.

Convoy Factors are trained to distinguish theft from local logistics wearing a knife.

This distinction is subtle.

It is also why some factors live.

---

## DEAD-WORLD RUNS AND BREACH ROUTES

Routes through dead worlds, breach zones, abandoned outer districts, failed spires, ash-choked industrial belts, and territories marked provisionally lost require special doctrine. Convoys in such areas carry less expectation of rescue and more expectation of being treated as rumor if they fail.

Dead-world runs are commonly assigned to bonded drivers, penal navigators, Limes escorts, Mortis salvage crews, and factors with either excellent records or enemies in scheduling. The cargo may be medicine, ammunition, evacuation tags, furnace fuel, memory caskets, replacement pump cores, or sealed things whose names are not written because the ash is assumed to read poorly but bureaucracy never takes chances.

Route Wardens on breach roads learn to distrust straight lines. Conceptual contamination has been known to make roads return to origin while appearing to proceed. Ash drifts may contain wheels from convoys that have not departed yet. Dead districts sometimes display lights to mimic safe stations.

The manual's guidance is brief:

If the road remembers you before you arrive, turn back unless under Legio order.

## THE MOBILE CORPSE

Convoy bureaucracy bloats differently from office bureaucracy. It accumulates scars, patched route maps, old toll tokens, dead driver superstitions, emergency oaths, local bribe rates, forbidden songs, obsolete bridge codes, and margin notes written by people who did not survive but were correct.

A central office may call such material irregular.

A convoy factor calls it navigation.

This creates endless conflict between archive purity and road truth. The Logistica officially prefers archive purity. Unofficially, it promotes factors who bring the cargo home.

The mobile corpse is ugly, but it moves.

---

## RELATIONS WITH SMUGGLERS

Convoy Factors hate smugglers professionally and respect them privately. Smugglers know routes official maps deny. They understand hunger markets faster than ration courts. They can move medicine where permits fail and weapons where permits were wisely denied.

The Logistica distinguishes between parasitic smuggling, corrective smuggling, noble smuggling, cult smuggling, survival smuggling, and smuggling later legalized by embarrassment. Route Wardens are authorized to suppress, tax, recruit, ignore, or execute depending on cargo, context, and whether the smuggler has already solved a problem the office was still classifying.

This flexibility horrifies purists.

Purists rarely ride outer roads.

---

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

A route is a promise extended through danger.

Do not trust maps without wheels beneath you. Do not trust seals without knowing who benefits from honoring them. Do not trust safe roads that are too quiet. Do not trust desperate citizens near food unless prepared to feed or frighten them.

Carry the receipt.

Sometimes it is the only proof that civilization passed this way.
