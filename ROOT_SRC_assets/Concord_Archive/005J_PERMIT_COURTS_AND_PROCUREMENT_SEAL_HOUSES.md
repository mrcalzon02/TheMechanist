# 005J PERMIT COURTS AND PROCUREMENT SEAL HOUSES
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Purchase Authority, Requisition Rights, Contract Writs, Bribe Differentiation, Emergency Taking, Noble Supply Claims, and the Fine Legal Distinction Between Theft and Paperwork Arriving First

Filed beneath **005 Concordia Logistica**, reviewed by the Null Court for seizure doctrine, Custodia Veritatis for counterfeit authority warnings, Praetoria Civica for enforcement procedure, Ferrum Sanctum for industrial procurement contamination, and the Ration Directorate for the recurring question of whether food already promised to one mouth may lawfully become someone else's strategic necessity.

This record concerns the **Permit Courts** and **Procurement Seal Houses**.

Ownership is a delay between need and authority.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

Permit Courts determine who may request, purchase, seize, redirect, borrow, consume, manufacture, store, or destroy goods under Concordial law. Procurement Seal Houses issue and validate the marks by which such determinations leave the court and become physical consequences in warehouses, factories, depots, convoys, ration lines, and private homes.

They are the Logistica's mouth for acquisition.

A requisition without seal is theft. Theft with the correct seal is emergency procurement. Emergency procurement with the wrong seal is still theft but may be promoted posthumously if it saves enough citizens. The Courts exist to preserve these distinctions because civilization depends less on preventing seizure than on knowing what to call seizure afterward.

---

## REQUISITION RIGHTS

Requisition rights are graded by emergency, office, cargo class, destination, and the political resilience of the person being deprived. The Legio may requisition under military necessity. Mortis may requisition under contamination necessity. Water Measure may requisition pumps, filters, tanks, and materials that become pumps after enough shouting. The Cultus Vitae may request ceremonial and consolation supplies but rarely appreciates the word request. Noble houses may claim ancestral procurement rights whose legal basis is sometimes older than the road to the warehouse.

The Permit Courts adjudicate collisions.

When two authorities claim the same goods, the Court asks which refusal creates the greater continuity damage, which claimant can retaliate, which claimant has paperwork, which claimant will survive public denial, and whether dividing the goods produces two failures instead of one.

Justice is not absent from this calculation.

It is simply outnumbered.

---

## CONTRACT WRITS AND SUPPLY PROMISES

The Procurement Seal Houses maintain contract writs binding manufactoria, farms, guilds, noble estates, military workshops, temple kitchens, penal yards, salvage crews, and private merchants to future delivery. A contract writ is a promise with teeth filed to fit a specific mouth.

Common contract failures include false capacity, material substitution, noble interference, worker death, ash contamination, convoy loss, machine refusal, cult seizure, and the traditional merchant claim that all defects occurred after acceptance.

Seal Houses maintain blacklists, graylists, favorlists, emergency forgiveness tables, and obituary clauses. The last category exists because many suppliers have attempted to escape penalty by dying. The Logistica considers this understandable but insufficient.

## BRIBE DIFFERENTIATION

No honest account of procurement can avoid bribery. The Courts distinguish between destructive bribery, facilitative bribery, ceremonial gift, noble pressure, survival gratuity, information purchase, hostage courtesy, and retroactively sanctioned acceleration.

The distinction is not moral comfort. It is operational necessity.

A bribe that sends medicine to a noble's private clinic during plague is destructive. A bribe that persuades a lazy gate clerk to release furnace fuel already lawfully assigned to a plague ward may be recorded as corrective acceleration if no superior wishes to admit the gate clerk existed. A ceremonial gift offered to a Seal House may be corruption if hidden and diplomacy if displayed under three witnesses.

Junior auditors often struggle with this doctrine.

Senior auditors struggle more, because they understand it.

---

## THE COURTS AS BUREAUCRATIC CORPSE

Permit Courts accumulate precedent like warehouses accumulate dust. Each seizure dispute creates language. Each emergency creates exception. Each exception creates a new form. Each form creates a route through which future need may crawl while pretending to obey the past.

Over centuries the Courts have become a corpse of permissions. Some writs authorize tools no longer made, offices no longer staffed, districts no longer populated, wars no longer remembered, and noble privileges everyone agrees are offensive until the noble house threatens to stop funding the pumpworks.

Yet obsolete permission can save the present. An old bridge requisition clause may permit steel seizure during sudden collapse. A dead army's fodder law may authorize food movement when modern ration law is frozen by appeal. A forgotten plague writ may open a sealed road when contemporary authorities are still discussing jurisdiction.

The corpse is bloated.

It still has keys in its pockets.

---

## COUNTERFEIT AUTHORITY

Permit seals attract fraud like meat attracts flies. Criminals counterfeit requisition marks, desperate officials overstate authority, noble agents reuse expired seals, cults fabricate charity writs, and false Custodia claimants occasionally attempt to redirect entire depots with confidence and theatrical jewelry.

Seal Houses maintain verification ladders. The ladders fail whenever local systems are outdated, offworld indexes are delayed, or the claimant is genuine but the record has not arrived within the last century. In such cases the Courts advise containment without insult, verification without obedience, and apology preparation in both directions.

This advice is elegant.

Field performance is less so.

---

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

Procurement is violence politely sequenced.

Do not be fooled by ink. A permit may empty a household, arm a regiment, doom a clinic, save a district, enrich a thief, or expose a traitor. Read the seal. Read the need behind the seal. Read the hand holding it.

Then decide whether the empire benefits more from obedience, delay, or denial.
