# 005A RATION DIRECTORATE AND FAMINE COURTS
## Concordial Logistica Archive Record Concerning Food Entitlement, Hunger Law, Emergency Reduction, Ceremonial Distribution, and the Judicial Starvation of Populations Considered Too Disorderly to Feed Without Supervision

Filed beneath the parent seal of **005 Concordia Logistica** by the Central Measure Archive, countersigned by the Famine Courts, reviewed by the Praetoria Civica for riot consequence, cross-noted by the Cultus Vitae for ceremonial distribution disputes, and retained for all bonded factors whose arithmetic may decide whether a district eats, kneels, bargains, revolts, or dies quietly enough to be called stable.

This record concerns the **Ration Directorate** and the subordinate **Famine Courts**.

The hungry citizen believes bread is food.

The Logistica knows bread is permission made edible.

---

## PRIMARY DEFINITION

The Ration Directorate is the arm of the Concordia Logistica charged with converting harvest, import, corpse-render byproduct, fungal output, grain reserve, noble tithe, military surplus, temple charity, penal production, and emergency substitute matter into lawful entitlement. It calculates what may be eaten, by whom, in what quantity, on what day, beneath what seal, under what hazard condition, and with what consequence for breach.

The Directorate does not grow food. It decides whether food enters history as sustenance, property, contraband, spoilage, sacrifice, evidence, waste, or weapon.

Its clerks are trained to read a granary as a battlefield. Its auditors are taught that a crate of meal incorrectly routed can become a riot, a noble scandal, a plague vector, a cult offering, a military defeat, or a district memory of betrayal that lasts six generations. It is therefore an office of numbers whose numbers have screams inside them.

The Famine Courts are not separate from the Directorate. They are the Directorate's judgment-mouth. When scarcity exceeds routine ration adjustment, when emergency reduction must be imposed, when accusation of hoarding must be tried, when ration theft threatens unit cohesion, when noble kitchens exceed authorized ceremonial consumption, or when an entire district must be moved from civil entitlement into survival schedule, the Famine Courts speak.

Their judgments are short.

Their queues are long.

---

## ORIGIN AND NECESSITY

The Directorate descends from camp quartermasters of the First Distillation, distiller caravans, ash-dike granary masters, fungus vault matrons, and those unpleasant survivors who learned during the Continuity Wars that mercy without inventory produced massacres at distribution gates. The oldest surviving ration tablets do not contain recipes, prayers, or patriotic declarations. They contain counts, weights, names, substitutions, and penalties.

The phrase preserved above the Primary Hall of Famine Measure reads:

*The first loaf given without count creates the second corpse.*

Citizens often mistake this for cruelty. Senior factors insist it is apology.

The Directorate became permanent after the Seventeenth Bread Riot of Vhal-Turris, when three philanthropic noble houses opened independent grain stores during a water shortage without providing crowd sequence, eligibility marks, or Praetoria enclosure. Forty-seven thousand citizens died within sight of food. The noble houses blamed greed. The Logistica blamed unmeasured mercy. The Null Court agreed with the Logistica and placed public charity under ration review thereafter.

Since that ruling, all significant distribution is a juridical act.

## HUNGER CATEGORIES

The Directorate classifies hunger into lawful categories before it will authorize relief. This practice is hated by priests, mocked by soldiers, exploited by nobles, and obeyed by every city that has survived long enough to understand why it exists.

**Common Hunger** denotes ordinary insufficiency within tolerable productivity range. It is managed by routine rationing, work-credit adjustment, low-grade supplements, and public reassurance.

**Civic Hunger** denotes insufficiency likely to affect tax yield, worker attendance, militia stability, child survival, or registered marriage rates. It requires district-level intervention and an apology proclamation if the affected population is politically visible.

**Military Hunger** denotes any shortage capable of reducing garrison readiness, march endurance, ammunition handling, watch discipline, or obedience to command. It may override Common Hunger without public announcement.

**Noble Hunger** denotes the inability of a recognized house to maintain ceremonial table, bloodline health, guest obligation, or Draught-adjacent metabolic stability. It is never named as such in public ledgers. It is commonly hidden under Continuity Hospitality, House Medical Necessity, or High Guest Provision.

**Mortis Hunger** denotes the food, salts, fuel, oils, suspensions, and furnace-adjacent matter required to prevent corpse law from collapsing into plague law. Civilians complain when Mortis receives grain. They complain louder when bodies stop being processed.

**Extinction Hunger** denotes shortage under which the Directorate acknowledges that all citizens cannot be preserved. The phrase is sealed. The decisions are not.

## THE FAMINE COURTS

Famine Courts are convened under black ledger seal and dull brass lantern. They do not ask whether suffering exists. That is assumed. They ask where suffering shall be assigned so continuity remains possible.

A standard Famine Court consists of a presiding Hunger Magistrate, three bonded measure factors, one Praetoria Civica riot witness, one Cultus Vitae consolation officer if the district has temple standing, one Mortis observer when expected death exceeds threshold, and a Logistica clerk whose name is omitted from public writs because clerks have been murdered more often than magistrates.

Their powers include:

- emergency reduction of rations by class, district, profession, age, contamination status, military necessity, or legal standing;
- authorization of substitute food matter, including fungus loaves, rendered protein, ash-treated nutrient slabs, bitter hydroponic root, industrial starch, and classified Mortis supplement;
- reclassification of hoarded food as state reserve;
- compulsory seizure of noble ceremonial stores during declared collapse;
- sentencing of ration forgery, distribution gate violence, warehouse arson, or falsified famine reports;
- suspension of feast days, temple distributions, mourning meals, and wedding banquets;
- designation of districts as non-viable consumption zones.

The last power is rarely discussed. It means a place will no longer be fed except through evacuation, labor extraction, military holding necessity, or political embarrassment.

## THE DIRECTORATE AS BUREAUCRATIC CORPSE

The Ration Directorate is among the oldest Logistica organs and displays the common sickness of very old offices. It has registries that survive the granaries they describe. It maintains ration categories for species of crop extinct before several modern noble houses were founded. It continues to print emergency entitlement slips for districts sealed under ash glass. It stores clay famine tallies beside electro-sealed hunger graphs because no synod has agreed which is legally superior after archive corruption.

A citizen may starve because a warehouse is empty.

A citizen may also starve because a warehouse is full but listed under a dead district, a wrong floor, an invalid seal, a quarantined prior famine, or a noble table exemption written by a clerk whose skull is now kept as evidence in the Collegia Mortis.

This is not considered malfunction.

It is considered historical layering.

The Directorate's defense is simple: every rule was written because someone died without it. The accusation is equally simple: every rule now kills someone else.

---

## PUBLIC MYTH AND PRIVATE PRACTICE

Among common citizens the Directorate is called the Bowl Office, the Empty Hand, the Spoon Court, the Bread Teeth, the Counting Mouth, or simply Them Upstairs. Children are warned that wasted crumbs will be heard by ration auditors through the floor. This is false, except in districts where the floor contains weight plates.

Among nobles the Directorate is despised as vulgar arithmetic until a rival house's banquet can be indicted under emergency consumption law. Among soldiers it is cursed until ammunition biscuits arrive before a siege. Among priests it is accused of profaning charity until charity mobs trample supplicants. Among smugglers it is studied with professional affection.

The Directorate survives because everyone hates it and everyone needs it.

That is the strongest form of legitimacy available to the Logistica.

## CLOSING INSTRUCTION

Let no factor confuse hunger with simplicity.

A starving crowd is not a mouth. It is a map of past decisions, failed transports, noble exemptions, blocked lifts, corrupted seals, broken pumps, false harvests, battlefield priorities, and prayers that arrived without sacks behind them.

Feed it wrongly and it becomes fire.

Do not feed it at all and it becomes history.
