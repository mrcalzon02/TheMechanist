# 004E LOWER SPIRE PRESSURES XALTECH AND UNBURDENED MUTATION
## Concordial Civic-Contagion Archive Record

## ARCHIVAL SEAL AND CONTAMINATION SCOPE

Issued under Collegia Mortis contamination seal with Custodia Cineris, Cultus Vitae, and Praetoria Civica restricted circulation. This record concerns the increasing hazard gradient encountered in lower Turris-Spirehold districts, with emphasis on Zaltech hunger-pressure, lower-dialect Xaltech corruption cults, Unburdened mutant offspring, ash-marked vagrancy, and the social conditions through which hostile transformations find shelter.

The lower one descends in a Stack, the more often metaphor becomes zoology.

## VERTICAL HAZARD GRADIENT

Spirehold danger is not distributed evenly. Upper districts suffer intrigue, inheritance murder, poison, legal extinction, political assassination, and refined forms of despair. Mid-stack districts suffer labor collapse, ration pressure, gang toll, disease, and industrial failure. Lower districts suffer all of these plus the creeping pressures of ash proximity, waste accumulation, forgotten machinery, bad heat, bad water, uncollected dead, undocumented births, and the long shadow of districts erased from current maps.

Hostile faiths prefer wounded places, but not because the wounded are wicked. Wounds are openings. A starving district hears a hunger god differently from a fed one. A freezing district hears a promise of painless dissolution differently from one with heat. A nameless child hears a Conceptual rescue mask differently from a citizen whose ident seal works.

## ZALTECH AND XALTECH FLOOR CORRUPTION

The formal enemy designation remains Didam of Zaltech for the great hunger-religions and their organized feeders. In lower spire dialect, the name is frequently degraded into Xaltech, Xaltekh, Salt-Teeth, Zalt, or the Mouth Below. Such distortions are not harmless. They allow citizens to speak near danger without appearing to invoke it directly, and they allow cult agents to hide behind claims of superstition.

Zaltech pressure enters through hunger before doctrine. The feeder does not first ask a citizen to worship. The feeder asks when the last full meal was taken, whose child is weakest, which ration officer sells places in line, whether the dead neighbor's flesh was truly wasted, and why the Concord forbids certain hungers while noble houses gorge upon Draught and name it duty.

This hypocrisy is among Zaltech's strongest recruitment tools. A citizen watching upper bodies preserved through forbidden appetite is vulnerable to the argument that all civilization is cannibalism with paperwork. The argument is false in its totality and dangerous in its partial truth.

Lower Xaltech cells are often family-shaped. They begin as shared kitchens, famine mutuals, burial economies, or revenge circles against hoarders. The transition from survival cannibalism to hunger theology may be slow enough that participants cannot identify the day they crossed. Custodia Cineris teams are instructed to treat every famine kitchen with respect and suspicion in equal measure.

## UNBURDENED OFFSPRING AND MUTATION DRIFT

The Unburdened produce social and biological aftermath. Not every child born to Unburdened influence is enemy-formed. Not every deformity is doctrinal. Not every painless infant is a vector. Nevertheless, lower districts with prolonged Unburdened presence exhibit recurring patterns: dulled pain response, reduced fear of injury, abnormal calm during violence, ash-grey dermal bloom, soft-tissue looseness, soundless crying, heat indifference, and communal naming rejection.

These offspring are often hidden. Families conceal them from love, fear, or both. Gangs weaponize them. False hospices collect them. Cultus Vitae clinics argue internally over treatment, quarantine, mercy, and execution. Mortis registrars complain that premature disposal destroys data needed for prevention. Praetoria officers complain that delayed disposal kills patrols. Both complaints possess case support.

The Archive forbids blanket extermination by phenotype alone without current contamination confirmation, except under breach emergency, active dissolution event, or Custodia Cineris seal. This restriction is unpopular among frightened districts. It remains necessary. The Concord cannot preserve humanity by teaching frightened officers to mistake every damaged child for an enemy.

## LOWER-DISTRICT ECOLOGY OF HIDDEN THINGS

Mutated offspring, hunger cells, ash-marked vagrants, painless doctors, lost machine servitors, nameless hosts, and illegal mercy orders cluster in the same districts because those districts offer concealment. The lower Stack is full of sealed rooms no office admits owning, old ducts wide enough for children, corpse chutes that no longer reach registered furnaces, drainage vaults converted into homes, markets that move nightly, and shrines to saints whose names are missing from approved lists.

A patrol sees filth and disorder. A trained civic-contagion officer sees habitat.

Eradication efforts fail when they burn habitat without replacing civic function. A district cleared of Xaltech feeders but left hungry will produce new feeders. A district stripped of Unburdened hospice hands but left without pain care will invite new painless doctors. A district purged of ash-marked vagrants but left without registry restoration will create new nameless hosts. The enemy grows where the Concord's claim is most visibly absent.

## CIVILIAN EXPERIENCE OF CONTAMINATION PRESSURE

The ordinary lower-spire citizen often understands danger before officials do. They know which hallway sings when no one walks it. They know which butcher never displays bones. They know which child does not flinch. They know which heat vent attracts the same pale figures every night. They know which gang now refuses to eat with outsiders. They know which clinic has no screams.

They do not always report. Reporting may bring quarantine, floor closure, ration suspension, child seizure, gang retaliation, or a purge team that cannot distinguish witness from accomplice. Silence is not always sympathy. It is often risk calculation.

Officials are therefore instructed to cultivate witnesses through protection, food, heat, treatment, and credible anonymity. Threat alone produces confessions, but not necessarily truth.

## SUPPRESSION, RECOVERY, AND MERCY LIMITS

The lower Stack requires layered response. Praetoria Civica may stabilize perimeter. Custodia Cineris confirms hostile form. Cultus Vitae assesses treatable victims. Collegia Mortis secures bodies and residue. Logistica restores ration flow. Ferrum teams examine machinery involvement. Null Court orders may be required for district quarantine, but must be drafted with precise consumption and water exemptions to avoid repeating historic legal catastrophes.

Mercy is not softness in this work. Mercy is classification discipline. Killing every frightened, sick, mutated, hungry, or undocumented body teaches the lower floors that the Concord cannot tell citizen from monster. That lesson is a recruitment hymn for every enemy beneath the Stack.

## FINAL CONTAMINATION ASSESSMENT

Zaltech hunger, Xaltech street corruption, Unburdened mutation drift, and lower-spire ash pressure do not arrive as armies at first. They arrive as answers to needs the lawful order failed to meet. They offer food, painlessness, belonging, warmth, silence, revenge, or release.

The lower Stack is not weak because it is morally inferior. It is weak where it has been made desperate. Desperation is the oldest breach gate. The Archive records that no wall has ever been strong enough to compensate indefinitely for an empty bowl.
