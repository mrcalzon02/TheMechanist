**Choir Parasites** and **Name-Eaters** are small or distributed Bestiae forms that rarely resemble grand monsters. They may exist as ash insects, throat growths, whispering soot, mirror motes, ink clots, registry stains, or tiny mouths on the inside of bells.

### Feeding

Choir Parasites feed through synchronized sound. They enter choirs, chants, factory calls, mourning lines, children's games, and military cadence. Name-Eaters feed through civic identity. They blur names, steal middle names first, corrupt ident prefixes, or cause citizens to answer to the dead.

The Collegia Mortis considers Name-Eaters especially offensive because they attack the administrative boundary between person and residue. The Nomenclature Ledger exists partly because names alone became unreliable long before spaceflight; Name-Eaters make the old failure predatory.

### Signs

Singers lose individual timing. Clerks miswrite the same name repeatedly. Children invent a game in which everyone shares one name. Funeral bells produce extra tones. A citizen forgets a middle name but remembers a dead neighbor's childhood nickname. Registry ink dries into tiny tracks.

### Countermeasures

Stop group speech. Heat bells. Separate singers. Reissue temporary ident tokens. Use written numbers rather than spoken names until Mortis clears the site. Burn contaminated ink, throat cloth, prayer ribbons, school slates, and choir masks.

Field maxim: **When the name is being eaten, silence is not rudeness. It is armor.**
